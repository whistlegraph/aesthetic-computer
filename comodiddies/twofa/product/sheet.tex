\cathead{brushmint}{The short version}
A security brush. An electric toothbrush that is also a hardware
security key --- a YubiKey you brush your teeth with. It charges over
USB-C on the bottom, and that same port plugs into your laptop to
authenticate and hold your credentials.

The security key problem is a habit problem: keys get lost, left in
drawers, forgotten on other keychains. A toothbrush has the strongest
habit loop of any object you own --- twice a day, every day, one fixed
location. The 2FA Brush piggybacks identity on hygiene: the one
device you never forget to touch.

And nobody borrows your toothbrush.

\modgap

\cathead{keyindigo}{Brushing is the passphrase}
The 6-axis motion sensor learns your \textbf{brushprint} --- the
personal signature of how you brush. Two minutes in the morning
unlocks the day's credentials. A stolen 2FA Brush is just a brush.

\cathead{brushmint}{The family bathroom problem}
Four identical brushes are four identical attack surfaces, so the 2FA
Brush refuses to be identical: snap-on color rings disambiguate at
arm's length, and the brushprint disambiguates when the rings get
swapped by a six-year-old. Picking up the wrong brush fails
\textit{both} factors --- which is, for once, the hygienic outcome too.

\modgap

\cathead{brushslate}{Recovery story}
Credentials pair to the handle and back up (encrypted, opt-in) under
BrushCare\,+. Lose the brush, buy a new one, brush for two minutes to
re-attest, restore. The bristles were due for replacement anyway.
