Jeffrey Alan Scudder

b. 1989, Assonet, MA
aesthetic.computer · jas.life · whistlegraph

Jeffrey Alan Scudder is an American artist, educator, and software programmer whose practice spans digital painting, lecture performance, instrument building, and creative computing. His work develops computational and conceptual tools that redefine approaches to traditional drawing and painting, grounded in an attention to digital materiality and the politics of picture-making.

Central to Scudder's practice is Radical Digital Painting, a framework he has articulated through a widely circulated manifesto, lecture performances, and exhibitions since 2017. The project advances an optimistic position on painting's future, beginning from the assertion that "painting is just a kind of picture message," and proposing that digital tools are not supplements to painting but its next material ground.

Scudder is the creator of Aesthetic Computer, a mobile-first runtime and social network for creative computing, and the inventor of the Whistlegraph, a hybrid art form combining drawing, singing, and poetry into reproducible scores. The Whistlegraph TikTok account, @whistlegraph, has reached over 2.7 million followers through musical drawing tutorials and lecture-performances. His software works include No Paint, a constraint-based drawing application, and KidLisp, a minimal programming language for generative art. An archive of 3,996 drawings is catalogued at Jeffrey's Drawings. His work is held in the collections of KADIST (San Francisco) and SMK (National Gallery of Denmark).

He holds an MFA from Yale School of Art (Sculpture, 2013) and a BFA from Ringling College of Art and Design (Fine Art, 2011). He has taught at Parsons School of Design and UCLA Design Media Arts, and has held a position as Assistant Professor of Emerging Digital Practices at Southern Oregon University.
