Whistlegraph

A drawing form where every line is a sung syllable. You draw it while you sing it. The finished drawing is a score — and the score teaches you how to play it.

Invented by Jeffrey Alan Scudder in 2019. Developed as a trio with Alex Freundlich & Camille Klein through the pandemic. Grown to 2.6 million on TikTok. Shown at the New Museum, Feral File, KADIST, and SMK. It continues on Aesthetic Computer.

Every whistlegraph has a code — type imab at aesthetic.computer, or open whistlegraph.org/imab

The form

A whistlegraph is a memorizable hybrid of drawing, singing, and poetry in which every mark corresponds to a sung syllable — producing a graphic score that is simultaneously artwork, song, and instruction manual for its own reproduction.

Unlike the graphic scores of Cage or Cardew, a whistlegraph requires no interpretation. The relationship between mark and sound is one-to-one, fixed during the original performance and preserved in the drawing. The score is not a prompt for improvisation; it is a set of precise instructions disguised as a picture.

  • Visual — a drawing made in real time. Each stroke is deliberate.
    pen on paper · chalk on blackboard
  • Musical — a sung melody. Each note aligns with a stroke.
    simple enough to memorize in one viewing
  • Textual — lyrics that work as poetry. Short enough to remember.
    describe · narrate · instruct
