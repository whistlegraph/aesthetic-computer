One-sentence description
Six players, six laptops; each laptop's macOS menubar is taken over by menuband — a Swift overlay that turns each keystroke into a piano-roll note — and the six menubars, projected side by side on the back wall, are the score the audience reads while the room jams as one collective keyboard.
What you'll show~250 w · drafting

A one-week residency culminating in a single public concert. NELA Computer Club — the biweekly Aesthetic.Computer demo show that runs at Plot.Place in Chinatown LA — comes to CultureHub for a touring engagement. We bring six laptops, install menuband on each, and rehearse a single piece for the week. On the night, five players take the stage at La MaMa; one player remains in LA at Plot.Place and joins over WiFi MIDI through CultureHub's networked-performance studio. Each player's menubar is captured and projected as a horizontal strip on the back wall, six strips stacked. The keys press, the menubars score, the room hears it. The whole concert is one piece, ~30 minutes.

§Pitch

NELA Computer Club is a biweekly demo night at Plot.Place in Chinatown, Los Angeles, where artists show works-in-progress on each other's machines. The Computer Club is small, regular, and unambitious in the right way — people show up, plug in, take a turn. It is the working scene that Aesthetic.Computer's other claims are built on top of.

The proposal is to take that scene to NYC for one week. Menuband Jam is the piece NELA brings.

menuband — the menubar promoted to instrument.

Menuband (slab/menuband/, currently at v0.9.1) is a Swift app that pins a glass panel into the macOS menubar at the very top of the screen. It listens to keystrokes — deliberate-only, no accidental toots — and renders each one as a piano-roll note ticking across the bar. There is a dark-mode piano theme; the title carries a sharp colored shadow for max contrast against any desktop. Underneath, MenuBandSynth + MenuBandSampleVoice + MenuBandMIDI route the keys to actual sound, with USB MIDI in/out and (in development for this residency) WiFi MIDI between machines. The menubar is no longer chrome; it is the score.

The jam — six players, one piece.

Six laptops. Each runs menuband. Each player has their own keyboard, their own role, their own range of notes. The audience reads the score off the back wall: six captured menubars stacked horizontally, every keystroke from every player visible the moment it's pressed. The piece is not improvised from nothing — we rehearse all week — but it is not click-tracked either. It's a jam in the live-jazz sense: a written head, an open middle, a written tail.

The sixth player is in LA.

One menuband stays at Plot.Place in Chinatown, played by a NELA regular at the home venue. WiFi MIDI carries the notes between rooms; the captured menubar from LA is the sixth strip on the back wall at La MaMa. This is the only part of the proposal that needs CultureHub's networked-performance studio — and it is exactly the part that justifies the residency. The piece does not work without the network; the network is not an embellishment.

Why this and not more.

Aesthetic.Computer has many subsystems — KidLisp, notepat, AC Native, the device library, the papermill. None of them are at this booth. The Menuband Jam is one piece, six players, one week, one concert. NELA Computer Club is the working community that hosts it; menuband is the working instrument that carries it; CultureHub is the working venue that lets the sixth chair stay home in LA. The residency is a single dense fit, not a survey of everything we make.

§The Lineagespecific, not canonical

PLOrk · Princeton
The original laptop orchestra (2005–). Six laptops, one piece. Hand-fabricated speaker hemispheres. The working precedent for this format; menuband's ambition is the same musicality at consumer-laptop access.
The Hub · Bischoff, Brown, Gresham-Lancaster, Perkis, Stone, Trayle
Bay Area network-music ensemble (1986–). Cross-machine MIDI as composition. Six players each, sometimes in different rooms. The Hub is the closest direct precedent for the Menuband Jam.
Cornelius Cardew · Scratch Orchestra
An artist-led collective attempt to build the system that would perform their scores. NELA Computer Club is this pattern, biweekly, in Chinatown LA.
Mark Fell · Multistability
Tight gridded notation as compositional material. Menuband's piano-roll-on-the-menubar inherits that notation-as-instrument posture.
Goodiepal
Radical Computer Music. The argument that the computer itself is under-designed as an artistic instrument. I toured Europe with him in 2018; menuband's menubar as score idea descends from those conversations.
CultureHub · Re-Fest lineage
Networked-performance work staged across rooms. The Menuband Jam keeps that lineage tight: one remote chair, no extra production, the network used because the piece needs it.
