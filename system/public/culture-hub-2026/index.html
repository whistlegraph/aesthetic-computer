§Pitch

Notepat Jam is a six-channel audio piece for six laptops — and the six laptops are the point.

notepat — the keyboard as a piano.

notepat is an instrument inside Aesthetic.Computer: a keyboard piano where every key is a note and the screen is the score. You can play it right now at aesthetic.computer/notepat. It is small, immediate, and made to be picked up by anyone.

AC Native — the laptop as the instrument.

AC Native (fedac/native/) is an operating system I build that boots from a USB stick. Plug it into almost any laptop and the whole machine becomes one Aesthetic.Computer instrument: no desktop, no menubar, no apps — just notepat, fullscreen. A 40-year-old desktop convention is set aside; the machine becomes a single thing you play.

Six salvaged machines.

For this residency I revive a rack of six aging laptops — donated, thrifted, e-waste-bound — by booting each into AC Native. The instruments are not bought; they are rescued. The six are synced over the studio's local network — no internet, no cloud — into one tempo and one key.

A piece composed for the room.

CultureHub LA's 5:1 Kalio surround system is why this belongs here. Each laptop drives a discrete speaker, so the piece is genuinely multi-channel — six voices placed around the audience, who sit inside the piece rather than facing a stage. I perform it solo: live notepat played over parts I build into the machines across the residency.

Open programming.

Alongside the concert, I run open programming: a public workshop and dialogue where anyone can bring a laptop and, with me, boot it into AC Native and play notepat. A dead machine becomes an instrument in an hour. The point of the residency is not only the piece — it is showing the room how the piece is made, and handing the method back.

§The Lineagespecific, not canonical

PLOrk · Princeton
The original laptop orchestra (2005–). Six laptops, one piece, hand-fabricated speaker hemispheres. Notepat Jam keeps the format but folds it to one performer and a surround system.
The Hub
Bay Area network-music ensemble (1986–). Cross-machine signal as composition. Here the network is local and small — six machines, one room.
Mark Fell · Multistability
Tight gridded notation as compositional material. notepat's key-is-a-note, screen-is-the-score posture inherits that notation-as-instrument stance.
Goodiepal
Radical Computer Music. The argument that the computer itself is under-designed as an artistic instrument. I toured Europe with him in 2018; AC Native descends from those conversations.
Permacomputing / salvage
The growing practice of keeping old hardware alive instead of discarding it. AC Native makes a USB stick the act of revival — an e-waste laptop becomes a working instrument.
