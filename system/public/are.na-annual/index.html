§pitch

i want to write about whistlegraph — a drawing form i invented in 2019 where every line is a sung syllable of a poem. you draw it while you sing it. the finished drawing is a score. the score teaches you how to play it.

between 2019 and 2023, a trio (me, alex freundlich, camille klein) scaled the form from a cabin in ashland, oregon to 2.6 million followers on tiktok — no paid promotion, no trend-jacking, no dance. the first viral hit was i'm a butterfly. flapping for you guys. it's just a costume. i put on in my room. — four strokes, four sung lines, a collaboration with the musician bo en. kids on tiktok learned it faster than most kids learn twinkle twinkle.

i want to write about why it worked there.

the graphic-score tradition failed at one thing.

cardew's treatise is 193 pages of beautiful, unreadable geometry. cage's fontana mix needs a transparent overlay. riley's in c wants 53 phrases and an ensemble. the 20th-century graphic score succeeded as art — notations, moma, 1969 — and failed as communication. the premise was that notation could be anything. the consequence was that performance required a trained interpreter.

whistlegraph takes the same ingredients and swaps one assumption. the relationship between mark and sound is one-to-one, fixed during the first performance and preserved in the recording. no interpretation. no ambiguity. a child who can hold a pen can perform one after watching it once.

that's the actual innovation, and it's the reason a drawing went viral on a platform designed for lip-sync. a dance challenge works because the choreography is the score; a whistlegraph works for the same reason, but the score is also original, also the artwork, also the instruction manual. papert would have called it a microworld. eshun's sonic fiction has a visual analogue: a drawing that constructs the performance it depicts.

three registers, one object.

as art, whistlegraph lives downstream of cardew, cage, and the fluxus event score — rhizome / new museum commissioned a 22-minute chalk whistlegraph in 2022; feral file sold 45 editions the same year; smk, kadist, schneider hold work in collection. as content, it broke the viral-mechanics assumption that tiktok only rewards bodies and pop audio. as interface design, it is the founding principle of aesthetic.computer — every piece a self-documenting score, every url a memorizable performance.

the trio separated in november 2023. the form didn't. what's left is the claim: reproducibility, not novelty, is the real score of a form. the essay is about what it would mean to design more things this way — objects whose instructions and performance are the same object.

§the lineage the essay actually arguesspecific, not canonical

goodiepal
el camino del hardcore (2012, alku 83) — a 191-page score printed in comic sans and courier on grey cardboard. radical computer music for alternative intelligences. the most honest predecessor.
jacob ciocci
paper rad co-founder. wrote the butterfly effect / rules set you free for the 2023 whistlegraph zine (ed. asher penn, sex magazine, 750 copies) — the essay that placed the form in the rule-based community-art lineage.
bo en
the musical collaborator on i'm a butterfly. pop melody as score, not pop audio as backing track.
cornelius cardew
scratch orchestra and nature study notes — not just treatise. the group was the attempt to solve the interpretation problem. whistlegraph attacks the same problem from the other side: remove interpretation from the score.
pauline oliveros
sonic meditations. attention as performance. whistlegraph asks the same of the viewer: watch, learn, reproduce.
seymour papert
mindstorms (1980) — microworlds. a whistlegraph is a microworld: constrained, memorizable, constructible by the viewer.
kodwo eshun
more brilliant than the sun (1998) — sonic fiction. whistlegraph is its visual analogue: a drawing that constructs the performance it depicts.
paul klee
pedagogical sketchbook (1925) — the line that goes for a walk. whistlegraph's lines take walks while singing.
olia lialina / jodi
rhizome artbase neighbours. whistlegraph sits in the same collection as early net art — the precedent for tiktok-native work entering an institutional archive.
jalaiah harmon
renegade (2019). proof that a reproducible body-score can spread through diffusion, not recommendation. whistlegraph is the drawing equivalent.

§submission-form version~200 words

the score that teaches itself. i want to write about whistlegraph — a drawing form i invented in 2019 where every line is a sung syllable. between 2020 and 2023, a trio i was in scaled it to 2.6 million tiktok followers from a cabin in ashland, oregon. no paid promotion. the distribution model was the form itself.

the 20th-century graphic-score tradition (cardew, cage, brown, feldman) is an art-historical success and a communication failure — the notation could be anything, so performance required a trained interpreter. whistlegraph keeps the graphic score and removes the interpretation: mark-to-sound is one-to-one, fixed at performance time. a child who can hold a pen can reproduce it.

the essay moves through three registers. as art — downstream of cardew, ciocci, goodiepal — but for anyone. as content — the first viral graphic score. as interface design — the founding principle of aesthetic.computer.

what i want to work out: why reproducibility, not novelty, is the real score of a form. and what it would mean to design more objects this way — instructions and performance as the same object.
