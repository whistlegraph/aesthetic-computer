About KidLisp Keeps

A Keep turns your KidLisp piece into a permanent Tezos token — the live HTML artifact, thumbnail, and metadata pinned to IPFS forever. Sign in, open any piece from Unkept, and press Keep on Tezos.

ꜩ On-chain

Each keep is an FA2 NFT on Tezos mainnet. Only the piece author can mint it, and each piece can only be kept once — the contract rejects duplicates by content hash.

Royalties are locked at mint: 9% to you, 1% to KidLisp. They're baked into the token and honored by objkt and all FA2-compatible marketplaces.

🔒 Security

These properties are enforced on-chain — verifiable in the contract source above.

  • Only you can mint. The backend issues a short-lived signed permit bound to your wallet address, the content hash, and the contract. Without it, keep reverts. There is no admin minting path.
  • Only you can burn your token. burn_keep checks current_owner == sender. Admin cannot burn on your behalf.
  • Content hash and royalties are immutable. edit_metadata always re-injects the original values from mint. Nobody can change them.
  • You can permanently freeze your metadata. lock_metadata is irreversible and owner-only — not even admin can unset it.
  • Pause never affects transfers. If the contract is paused, minting and metadata edits stop — FA2 transfers remain live. Your ability to move or sell is always unaffected.
☁ IPFS & Media

When you keep a piece, the backend bundles the KidLisp source into a self-contained HTML artifact and renders a thumbnail via the Oven service.

Both are pinned to IPFS via Pinata. The artifact URI and thumbnail URI in your token metadata point directly to IPFS — no KidLisp servers required to view your token on objkt or any marketplace.

Use Rekeep in the modal to regenerate the media if your piece's source changes after minting.
