Menu Band

Built-in macOS instruments, in the menu bar.

Download 0.1 · Apple Silicon · 1.1 MB View source

What it does

A tiny piano sits at the right of your menu bar. Click any key to play. Type letters to play. Send notes to your DAW over MIDI.

Menu Band publishes a virtual MIDI source named Menu Band — pick it as an input in any DAW and the keys you tap (or letters you type) play through your DAW's instruments. A built‑in General MIDI synth covers hundreds of patches when no DAW is running.

About

Menu Band is a political project: accessible music-making is as essential as time, network connectivity, and battery life. Free and open source. By aesthetic.computer.

Three input modes

  • PointerMouse only. Two octaves of QWERTY-mapped piano keys. Default.
  • NotepatGlobal keystroke capture, two-octave layout (z x c v d s e f w g r a q b h t i y j k u l o m p n).
  • AbletonGlobal keystroke capture, Ableton Live's M-mode QWERTY (one octave: a w s e d f t g y h u j k o l p ;).

Hover any segment in the popover to preview it in the menubar piano — letter labels appear, key range adjusts, and tapping keys plays through that mode without committing.

What's new 0.1

First release. Three-state input picker (Pointer / Notepat / Ableton) with hover-preview, MIDI loopback self-test, virtual MIDI source for any DAW, instrument picker covering all 128 General MIDI patches.

Requirements

macOS 11 (Big Sur) or later · Apple Silicon · ⌃⌥⌘P toggles the last typing mode · Accessibility permission requested only when you turn on Notepat or Ableton.
