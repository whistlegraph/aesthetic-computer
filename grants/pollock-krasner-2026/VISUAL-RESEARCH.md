## Visual read

The five Johannes Vogt works form the clearest coherent sequence currently recovered. All are square, framed, large-scale works with strong color fields and dense graphic events. Together they establish a persuasive formal vocabulary: layered marks, diagrammatic figures, soft digital color, hard-edged strokes, and the picture treated as both field and interface. The gallery page provides individual titles, dates, dimensions, and clean photographs.

The VFC work is visually continuous with that vocabulary but materially denser and more recent. Its rounded rectangular support, heavily accumulated surface, handwritten date/title, and embedded QR code distinguish it from the 2018 prints. It may be the most useful bridge from Radical Digital Painting back to current physical studio work.

The Internet Archive whiteboard is unusually strong documentation: the object fills the frame, its wheeled support is visible, and the drawing is legible. It is clearly an exhibited physical drawing rather than a screenshot. Its weakness is classification—PKF may see a didactic exhibition diagram rather than an autonomous work—so the title and artist's intention matter.

*The Longest Whistlegraph Ever (so far)* has abundant visual material, but the current contact-sheet image is only the microsite cover. The final application should instead use one straight, evenly lit image of the complete physical graphic score or a manuscript sheet, with a second installation view only if needed. Because it is a three-person collaboration, it should occupy no more than one work slot unless PKF gives specific guidance.

The two platter photographs prove that additional physical paintings exist in Jeffrey's history. They do **not** prove title, completion year, authorship, medium, dimensions, or current location. They are find-this-object prompts, not application images.

1. Open with the strongest 2025/2026 physical painting after a clean photograph is recovered.
2. Add a second recent physical painting from Turbo Cheap or the same studio body.
3. Add a third recent work showing the body is sustained rather than a single return.
4. Use two or three of the Johannes Vogt prints—not necessarily all five—to establish the 2018 origin of the visual language.
5. Use the *Meeting Mr. Kid Pix* whiteboard if PKF confirms it is eligible.
6. Use the complete *Longest Whistlegraph* score as one collaborative work, if eligible.
7. Fill the remaining slots with recent canvas/board works, not additional project screenshots.

The present ten-image contact sheet is a research map, **not** the recommended final ten. A final portfolio made of five digital prints plus project documentation could reinforce PKF's concern that the primary practice is digital/performance. The final sequence should be weighted toward recent canvas, board, sculpture, and paper objects.
