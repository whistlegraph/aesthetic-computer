# Pollock-Krasner Foundation — Jeffrey Career Audit

> **Audited:** 2026-07-20
> **Sources:** monorepo, papers platter, prior grant prose, and git history
> **Conclusion:** Plausible fit, but eligibility and portfolio are not yet substantiated

## Finding

Jeffrey has a substantial, documented professional career: Fine Art BFA, Sculpture MFA, solo and group exhibitions, residencies, teaching, press, public collections, and an extensive painting/drawing performance practice. The repository nevertheless does not currently prove the two facts that matter most for this application:

1. that at least ten exhibitions during 2016–2026 meet PKF's professional-exhibition definition; and
2. that ten recent, finished, individually documented works in painting, sculpture, or works on paper are available for submission.

The public CV says the June 2018 Johannes Vogt exhibition of large-format digital prints on paper was Jeffrey's last public physical-picture exhibition before the May 2026 Venice Family Clinic presentation of new physical work. That eight-year gap is material. It should not be hidden with software exhibitions or performance listings.

- `system/public/justanothersystem.org/cv.html` contains the fuller painter-centered biography: stretched canvas, studio painting, software, performance, the 2018 prints-on-paper show, and the 2026 return to physical exhibition.
- `papers/jeffrey-platter/README.md` is primarily a portrait/provenance index. It is useful for biography and image-source routing, not as an artwork inventory.

- `system/public/aesthetic.computer/disks/25.4.13.19.24.mjs` identifies a Jeffrey drawing connected to the 2026 Venice Family Clinic auction and links its asset/lot. It does not record physical dimensions or medium.

## Claims not safe yet

- “At least ten qualifying PKF exhibitions.”
- Any list of ten physical works without object-by-object records.
- That a digital painting is a work on paper merely because it can be printed.
- Sole authorship of collaborative Whistlegraph works.
- Dimensions, medium, title, ownership, or exhibition history inferred from social captions or AI-generated image descriptions.
- Financial hardship or need not supplied directly by Jeffrey. PKF's current FAQ says decisions are based on artistic merit, not financial need.

## Recommended positioning if eligibility is confirmed

Jeffrey is a painter trained in fine art and sculpture whose pictures have moved among canvas, board, printed paper, software, and live drawing. The physical portfolio should be a coherent recent body—not a retrospective sampler of Aesthetic Computer. Software belongs in one short paragraph as the long experiment that sharpened his commitment to immediacy, authorship, and the material behavior of a mark.
