A wide horizontal tableau on warm cream-colored paper, drawn in
colored pencil and gouache in a Hockney register: heavy hatching
for stage shadows, optical-mixing dots for projection-light
diffusion, sharp gouache rectangles for the menubar piano-roll
notes, tapered confident strokes, the composition gradually
softening to nothing at all four edges so the periphery
DISSOLVES INTO PURE CREAM PAPER with no border, no frame, no
proscenium. Muted natural palette (terracotta, sage, ochre, dusty
pink, slate blue, brick red, warm grey, oxblood, brass) accented
by TWO saturated pop-color zones: (1) the Citrus-green of five
Apple MacBook Neos in a row (Apple's official Citrus colorway
— lime-yellow shells, white apple emblems, lids open); (2) the
crisp ticking gouache rectangles of the menubar piano-roll, in
hot pink, electric cyan, golden ochre, fresh green, and deep
purple — small but high-contrast, the visual rhythm of the
piece.

Format: 1536×1024 horizontal (3:2). Subject is the menubar-as-
score itself — five tiny piano-rolls running across the tops of
the screens, and one massive synced version on the back wall.
Less venue context, less institutional framing; more the immediate
visual experience of menuband as a collective instrument.

THE FRAME: a wide low stage-level shot inside a warm-dim
black-box theatre. Five players seated in a row at a long low
plywood platform. Behind them on the back wall, ONE BIG
projection — six horizontal menubar piano-roll strips stacked
vertically, all six synced and mid-piece, each strip a band-tee
silkscreen of small bright rectangles in the pink/cyan/gold/green/
purple palette. The projection is the brightest surface in the
image and dominates the eye behind the row of laptops; warm dust
catches in the projector beam (rendered in optical-mixing pencil
marks).

THE PLAYERS, in a single horizontal row at peer eye-level (no
centred figure, no elevation, no hero — exactly the same height
and lighting across all five). From left to right:

PLAYER 1 — woman, late thirties, shoulder-length dark hair, wide
round earrings, wearing a flannel button-down, hands resting on
the keys, faint smile of concentration.

PLAYER 2 — man, mid-twenties, paint-spattered grey hoodie,
short tousled hair, leaning slightly forward, eyes on his
menubar.

PLAYER 3 — woman, mid-fifties, knitted cream cardigan over a
patterned shirt, reading-glasses pushed up on her forehead,
mid-keystroke.

PLAYER 4 — man, early thirties, light-blue T-shirt with a
patterned breast-pocket (cf. jeffrey-platter performance
reference 2018-07-13), warm under the projector spill, deadpan
attentive.

PLAYER 5 (jeffrey, FAR RIGHT, never centred): render from the
jeffrey-platter performance-domain references — medium-length
brown hair tucked behind ears, no beard, wearing a light
crewneck sweatshirt (cf. 2018-11-04 reference) — hands on the
keyboard mid-keystroke, the same eye-line as the four others,
the warm projector spill on his face.

ABOVE EACH PLAYER'S SCREEN: at the very top, the macOS
menubar is the score — a horizontal strip of small crisp gouache
rectangles ticking left-to-right in pink, cyan, gold, green,
and purple. Tiny but precise. Five small menubar strips on the
five screens; the sixth strip lives only in the back-wall
projection above.

LIGHTING: cool stage spill on the players' shoulders, warm
projector glow on their faces, deep brick wall barely visible
in the periphery before fading to cream paper. A single low cool
light bar at the top of the frame casts long pencil-hatched
shadows behind each player.

NO INSTITUTIONAL TEXT: no hand-lettered "CultureHub", "La MaMa",
"NELA Computer Club", "Aesthetic Computer", "Plot.Place", or
"Menuband" spelled out anywhere. Identity is carried entirely by:
the row of five Citrus-green Neos, the six menubar piano-roll
strips on the back wall, the menubars on each screen, the peer
horizontality of the players. Incidental product text on
physical objects is acceptable when NOT the institution
illustrated (the apple emblem on a lid, a tiny "MIDI" stamp on
a USB hub on the table, a small generic stage-lighting model
number). Letterforms anywhere else appear as illegible
scribble-gestalt only.

Hockney colored-pencil + gouache, cream paper, all four corners
dissolve to nothing.
