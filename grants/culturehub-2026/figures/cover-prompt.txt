A wide horizontal illustration on warm cream-colored paper, drawn in
colored pencil and gouache: heavy directional hatching for the
dim-room shadows, optical-mixing dots for the glow of laptop screens
and the haze in the air, sharp gouache rectangles for the bright
on-screen notes, tapered confident strokes, the composition softening
at all four edges so the periphery DISSOLVES INTO PURE CREAM PAPER —
no border, no frame, no proscenium.

Muted natural palette — terracotta, sage, ochre, dusty pink, slate
blue, brick red, warm grey, oxblood, brass — accented by ONE saturated
pop-color zone: the glowing screens of the laptops, crisp gouache
rectangles of a ticking piano-roll in hot pink, electric cyan, golden
ochre, fresh green, and deep purple. Small but high-contrast — the
visual rhythm of the piece.

FORMAT: 1536×1024 horizontal (3:2). THE SUBJECT is a loose ring of six
old, mismatched, salvaged laptops glowing in a dim flexible studio — a
small sound-art showroom — with one performer and a seated audience
inside the same ring.

THE LAPTOPS — six aging, rescued machines, deliberately NOT new and
NOT matching: a scuffed black corporate laptop with a worn trackpad, a
yellowed beige plastic clamshell, a dented silver ultrabook with a
cracked lid corner, a chunky grey laptop covered in peeling stickers,
a slim dark one with a faded keyboard, a boxy older model with a thick
bezel. Different brands, different eras, visibly secondhand — thrifted,
donated, pulled back from the e-waste pile. They sit in a loose ring
on low plywood tables, lids open, all six SCREENS FACING INWARD and
glowing. Each screen shows the same kind of interface — a
keyboard-piano with a horizontal piano-roll of small bright rectangles
ticking across it — but each at a different moment, varied, never a
copy of the others and never a thumbnail of the room.

THE SPEAKERS — five studio loudspeakers on tall thin stands placed
around the edges of the room facing inward, and one low boxy
subwoofer on the floor. They ring the whole space; the six laptops and
the people are INSIDE that ring of speakers.

THE AUDIENCE — six to eight people of varied ages, twenties to
sixties, seated low on the floor and on small benches in a loose
circle, relaxed, leaning in, listening. Warm screen-light on their
faces. Real everyday clothes — flannels, hoodies, knit cardigans,
printed tees, button-downs. No phones raised. They are clearly inside
the piece, not facing a stage.

THE PERFORMER — one man, early thirties, OFF-CENTER and at the exact
same eye-level as the seated audience around him, NOT elevated, NOT
spotlit, NOT a hero — just one more person in the ring, hands on the
keyboard of one of the salvaged laptops, mid-keystroke, deadpan and
attentive. Medium-length brown hair tucked behind the ears, no beard,
wearing a light crewneck sweatshirt.

LIGHTING: the room is warm and dim; the only bright sources are the
six glowing laptop screens, which throw colored light up onto every
face and hand and catch warm dust in the air (rendered in
optical-mixing pencil marks). Long pencil-hatched shadows spill
outward from the ring of laptops.

NO INSTITUTIONAL TEXT: do not spell out any company, software, venue,
or product name anywhere. No legible words at all. Identity is carried
entirely by: the ring of six mismatched salvaged laptops, the bright
ticking piano-rolls on their screens, the encircling loudspeakers, and
the audience seated inside the sound. Any letterforms appear only as
illegible scribble-gestalt. Worn texture and scuffs on the old laptops
are welcome but carry no readable text.

Colored pencil and gouache, cream paper, all four corners dissolve to
nothing.
