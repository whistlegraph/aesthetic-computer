A wide horizontal illustration on warm cream-colored paper, drawn in
colored pencil and gouache: heavy directional hatching for the
dim-room shadows, optical-mixing dots for the soft glow in the air,
tapered confident strokes, the composition softening at all four edges
so the periphery DISSOLVES INTO PURE CREAM PAPER — no border, no
frame, no proscenium.

Muted natural palette — terracotta, sage, ochre, dusty pink, slate
blue, brick red, warm grey, oxblood, brass — accented by ONE saturated
pop-color zone: a soft colored GLOW spilling from behind the laptop
lids, in hot pink, electric cyan, golden ochre, fresh green, and deep
purple, washing up onto faces and hands. The glow is the only bright
color; its source is hidden.

FORMAT: 1536×1024 horizontal (3:2). THE SUBJECT is a loose ring of six
old, mismatched, salvaged laptops in a dim flexible studio — a small
sound-art showroom — with one performer and a seated audience inside
the same ring.

THE LAPTOPS — six aging, rescued machines, each drawn as a CLEAN,
SIMPLE, CLEARLY RECOGNIZABLE laptop clamshell, lids open at a normal
angle, no melting or distorted shapes. They are deliberately NOT new
and NOT matching: a scuffed black corporate laptop, a yellowed beige
plastic clamshell, a dented silver ultrabook, a chunky grey laptop
with peeling stickers, a slim dark one, a boxy older model with a
thick bezel. Different brands, different eras, visibly secondhand —
thrifted, donated, pulled back from the e-waste pile. They sit in a
loose ring on low plywood tables.

CRUCIAL — DO NOT SHOW ANY SCREENS. Every laptop is turned so its
screen faces AWAY from the viewer. The camera sees only the plain
outer BACKS of the open lids. No display, no screen content, no
interface, no glowing rectangles, no pixels, no icons are visible
anywhere in the image. From behind each lid a soft colored glow spills
upward and outward, lighting the room — but the screen itself is never
seen.

THE SPEAKERS — five studio loudspeakers on tall thin stands placed
around the edges of the room facing inward, and one low boxy
subwoofer on the floor. They ring the whole space; the six laptops and
the people are INSIDE that ring of speakers.

THE AUDIENCE — six to eight people of varied ages, twenties to
sixties, seated low on the floor and on small benches in a loose
circle, relaxed, leaning in, listening. Soft colored glow on their
faces. Real everyday clothes — flannels, hoodies, knit cardigans,
printed tees, button-downs. No phones raised. They are clearly inside
the piece, not facing a stage.

THE PERFORMER — one man, early thirties, OFF-CENTER and at the exact
same eye-level as the seated audience around him, NOT elevated, NOT
spotlit, NOT a hero — just one more person in the ring, hands resting
at one of the salvaged laptops, mid-gesture, deadpan and attentive.
Medium-length brown hair tucked behind the ears, no beard, wearing a
light crewneck sweatshirt.

LIGHTING: the room is warm and dim; the only bright light is the soft
colored glow rising from behind the laptop lids, washing up onto every
face and hand and catching faint haze in the air (rendered in
optical-mixing pencil marks). Long pencil-hatched shadows spill
outward from the ring of laptops.

NO TEXT ANYWHERE: do not spell out any company, software, venue, or
product name. No legible words at all. Any letterforms appear only as
illegible scribble-gestalt. Worn texture and scuffs on the old laptop
lids are welcome but carry no readable text.

Colored pencil and gouache, cream paper, all four corners dissolve to
nothing.
