A wide horizontal tableau on warm cream-colored paper, drawn in
colored pencil and gouache in a Hockney register: heavy hatching for
stage shadows, visible perceptual-breakdown marks (optical-mixing
dots for projected light, striping for fabric, moiré on screens),
tapered confident strokes, the composition gradually softening to
nothing at all four edges so the periphery DISSOLVES INTO PURE CREAM
PAPER with no border, no frame, no proscenium, no curtain. Muted
natural palette throughout (terracotta, sage, ochre, dusty pink,
slate blue, brick red, warm grey, oxblood, brass) accented by TWO
saturated pop colour zones: (1) the Citrus-green of five Apple
MacBook Neos — official Citrus colorway, lime-yellow shells, white
apple emblems, lids open; (2) bright pinpoint rectangles of pink /
cyan / gold / green / purple in the menubar piano-roll captures.

THE SCENE: the interior of a small black-box theatre stage (the
CultureHub-at-La-MaMa register: brick walls visible at the edges, a
low plywood platform, exposed cable runs, a single cool-white light
bar). Five players seated behind a long low plywood platform, like
a row of typewriters at a 1960s newsroom desk. From left to right:
four NELA Computer Club regulars — mixed ages 24–55, mixed genders,
real Los Angeles thrift-shop outfits (a flannel button-down, a
paint-spattered hoodie, a knitted cardigan, one striped tee, one
cable-knit sweater) — and on the FAR RIGHT, jeffrey scudder, ~5'10",
short brown hair, navy button-down over a faded printed tee, hands
on keyboard mid-keystroke, deadpan attentive expression. All five at
peer eye-level, NONE centred, NONE elevated; jeffrey at the right
end is the same height and the same lighting as the others.

ONE CHAIR IS CONSPICUOUSLY EMPTY — beyond jeffrey at the far right
end, a sixth chair holds only a small flat monitor showing a
wireframe / colored-pencil representation of a Chinatown-Los-Angeles
storefront interior (the Plot.Place register: small art space, a
folding table, hand-drawn signage in the window). A small hand-
lettered card taped to the monitor says "PLAYER 6 · LA" in scribble-
gestalt — barely readable, more emblem than text.

ABOVE EACH OF THE FIVE LAPTOPS, the macOS menubar at the very top of
each screen is doing the work of a piano-roll score: a horizontal
ribbon of small crisp gouache rectangles ticking left-to-right in
pink, cyan, gold, green, and purple — a sixth of these ribbons
appears on the empty-chair monitor, ticking at the same rate as the
others. Each menubar-ribbon is tiny but precise, a band-tee silkscreen
of a piano-roll.

ON THE BACK WALL behind the row of players, ONE very wide
projection: six horizontal menubar strips stacked vertically, all
six menubars synced and mid-piece, the audience reading the score
off the wall. The projector beam comes in through warm dust;
optical-mixing pencil marks render the dust catch. The six stacked
strips fade softly toward the cream paper at the corners.

DO NOT NAME THE INSTITUTION ANYWHERE — no hand-lettered "CultureHub",
"La MaMa", "NELA Computer Club", "Aesthetic Computer", "Plot.Place",
or "Menuband" spelled out. Identity carried entirely by emblem (the
row of green Neos, the stacked menubars on the back wall, the empty
sixth chair with its little Plot.Place monitor, the row of peer
players). Incidental product or manufacturer text already physically
belonging to a depicted object is acceptable when NOT the institution
illustrated (the apple emblem on a lid, a tiny "MIDI IN" stamp on a
USB hub, a generic stage-lighting model number on a fixture).
Letterforms may appear as illegible scribble-gestalt where the form
of letters is part of the object's character ("PLAYER 6 · LA" on the
monitor card is the one exception, and it should still read more as
pencil-gesture than as type).

Format: 1536×1024 horizontal (3:2). The whole composition reads from
across a room as: a row of five green laptops + a sixth empty chair
with a small monitor + five players at peer height + a wide six-stripe
score on the back wall.
