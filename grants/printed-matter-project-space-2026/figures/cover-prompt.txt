A wide horizontal tableau on warm cream-colored paper, drawn in colored
pencil and gouache in a Hockney register: heavy hatching for shading,
visible perceptual-breakdown marks (optical-mixing dots, striping,
moiré in the printed surfaces), tapered confident strokes, the
composition gradually softening to nothing at all four edges so the
periphery DISSOLVES INTO PURE CREAM PAPER with no border, no frame,
no environmental vignette. Muted natural palette throughout
(terracotta, sage, ochre, dusty pink, slate blue, brick red, warm
grey, oxblood, brass) accented only by ONE saturated pop colour: the
Citrus-green of an Apple MacBook Neo (the official Citrus colorway
— a clean lime-yellow shell with the white apple emblem on the lid,
the laptop sitting open at a precise 110° angle).

THE SCENE: a humble plywood reference desk inside a converted
industrial hall (NYABF-at-MoMA-PS1 atmosphere, late afternoon light
through high steel-mullion windows). On the desk, the single Citrus-
green MacBook Neo, mid-keystroke. Stacked next to it: three or four
arxiv-format papers in two-column print, hand-lettered titles
dissolving into illegible scribble-gestalt at the edges. A small
thermal printer sits beside the laptop, with a single 2.75″ × 4.75″
KidLisp card sliding out of its slot — the card showing a tiny pixel-
render at top, a few lines of source mid, and a QR code at bottom.

BEHIND THE DESK, a tacked-up wall of papers with binder-clips, like
a row of trading cards: fourteen small dossier emblems already in the
AC papermill lineage — an elephant spine, a Greek temple, a brass
seal with a lyre, a hex pin with crossed iron and brush, a brick arch
with an Edison bulb, a typed index card, a floppy with a rhizome
doodle, a chalkboard schematic, an inverted stoop, a Sony Portapak,
a stencilled 7" sleeve, a pixel-burst badge, a clenched-fist picket
sign, a glass museum vitrine. To the left of the dossier wall, a
small hand-drawn bar chart: seven horizontal segments labelled
"137 MB" in pencil, no other readable text.

AT THE DESK, three figures at peer eye-level — a horizontal reading-
circle composition, NEVER centred or elevated, the kind of casual
peer composition you'd find in a 1970s public library photo or a
Hockney pool-side group sketch. The middle figure is a deadpan
attentive man, ~5'10", short brown hair, wearing a navy thrift-shop
button-down over a faded printed tee, leaning forward to look at the
laptop screen — this is jeffrey. On his left, a young woman visitor
with reading glasses pointing at the screen and asking a question.
On his right, an older bearded visitor reading a card he just
received from the printer. All three at the same eye-line. None of
them centred; jeffrey is offset slightly right of centre.

DO NOT NAME THE INSTITUTION ANYWHERE — no hand-lettered "Aesthetic
Computer", "Printed Matter", "AC Reader", or "NYABF" anywhere in the
image. Identity carried entirely by emblem (the wall of singular
dossier icons, the Citrus-green Neo, the printed card, the thermal
printer). Incidental product or manufacturer text already physically
belonging to a depicted object is acceptable when it is NOT the
institution being illustrated (a small "BROTHER" or generic printer
mark on the printer body, an "M" on the apple emblem, a typed
copyright line on a paper, the blurred title-stripe on an arxiv
front-page). Letterforms may appear as illegible scribble-gestalt
where the form of letters is part of the object's character.

Format: 1536×1024 horizontal (3:2). The whole composition reads from
across a room as: green laptop + wall of dossier emblems + three
people at peer height + one card mid-air leaving the printer.
