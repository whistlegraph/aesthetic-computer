A wide horizontal tableau on warm cream-colored paper, drawn in
colored pencil and gouache in a Hockney register: heavy hatching
for shading, optical-mixing dots and striping for tonal blends,
moiré in printed surfaces, tapered confident strokes, the
composition gradually softening to nothing at all four edges so the
periphery DISSOLVES INTO PURE CREAM PAPER with no border, no frame,
no framing rectangle. Muted natural palette (terracotta, sage,
ochre, dusty pink, slate blue, brick red, warm grey, brass) with
TWO saturated pop-color zones: (1) the Citrus-green of a single
Apple MacBook Neo (Apple's official Citrus colorway — clean
lime-yellow shell with the white apple emblem, lid open at ~110°);
(2) the screen of that laptop showing a vivid KidLisp piece in
mid-render — a chunky-pixel generative-art composition, hot pink
recursive wires tangling against a deep slate-blue field, blocks
of zebra-striped fill, a hurricane spiral mid-zoom, the kind of
six-line piece on the AC platform.

Format: 1536×1024 horizontal (3:2). Subject is the act of
publishing a piece — KidLisp on screen, card on its way out of
the press. Less booth, less wall, less institutional context;
more the close, immediate aesthetic of a single piece becoming
print.

THE FRAME: a tight medium shot at table-height, low natural
late-afternoon light from the right. Two figures at peer eye-
level on either side of the laptop, both leaning in toward the
screen, attention all flowing TO THE PIECE on the green Neo —
the laptop is the visual centre, not the people.

LEFT FIGURE (visitor 1): A young woman in her late twenties,
medium-brown hair pulled back, round wire-frame reading glasses,
wearing a rust-colored cable-knit sweater over a thin grey tee.
She is leaning in, finger pointing softly at a specific pixel
cluster on the screen, deadpan attentive. Index in motion, head
slightly cocked.

CENTER (the laptop is the centre, NOT a person): the open
Citrus-green MacBook Neo. The screen is the brightest surface in
the image and dominates the eye — a chunky-pixel KidLisp piece
mid-frame, hot pink recursive lines tangling against deep slate
blue, with a small monochrome render-source block visible at the
bottom of the screen showing a few lines of parens-prefixed code
in scribble-gestalt.

RIGHT FIGURE (jeffrey, no halo, not centred): visibly to the
right of the laptop, head and shoulder cropped at the right edge.
Render him from the jeffrey-platter's KidLisp-domain reference
frames: short-to-medium brown hair, no beard, wearing a faded
bright green sweatshirt over a thin denim shirt visible at the
collar (cf. 2019-06-21 Kid-Pix/floppy reference), a small beaded
bracelet on one wrist. Expression: focused, deadpan attentive —
exactly the same eye-line as the visitor on the left, never
elevated.

ON THE TABLE in front of the laptop: a small thermal-style
printer (humble, generic, unbranded) is mid-eject of a single
2.75″ × 4.75″ card. The card carries a tiny pixel render at the
top (the same KidLisp piece on the laptop screen, captured), a
few lines of source mid, and a QR code at the bottom. The card
catches the screen light. ONE card only — no stack, no mess.

NO institutional context: no tacked-up paper-wall behind the
figures, no row of dossier emblems, no chart, no civic library
register. The background fades to soft cream paper a few feet
behind the laptop, with only one or two subtle grace notes — a
half-visible wooden shelf at the upper right with a single
houseplant, a tiny crooked QR-card pinned to the wall — both
dissolving into the cream paper at the edges.

DO NOT NAME THE INSTITUTION ANYWHERE — no hand-lettered
"Aesthetic Computer", "Printed Matter", "AC Reader", "NYABF",
"KidLisp", or any spelled-out brand. Identity is carried by:
the saturated-Citrus laptop, the chunky-pixel KidLisp aesthetic,
the printed card, the peer-eye-level composition. Incidental
product or manufacturer text on physical objects is fine when
NOT the institution being illustrated (the apple emblem, a
generic small "PRINT" stamp on the printer body, faintly
illegible code-stub on the screen). Letterforms anywhere else
appear as illegible scribble-gestalt only.

Hockney colored-pencil + gouache, cream paper, all four corners
dissolve to nothing.
