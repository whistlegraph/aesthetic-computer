**Tell us about a time you created demand, captured value, or sold someone on something. The weirder the better. (150 words max)\*** — *(~150 words)*

> For three years I toured Europe performing what I called Radical Digital
> Painting: I'd stand on a stage and draw, live, projected huge, as if drawing
> were a rock set — sixty-five shows, from Café OTO in London to the Louisiana
> Museum in Denmark, often opening for the experimental band Goodiepal & Pals. The
> premise was absurd on paper: come watch a man draw. But I sold rooms full of
> strangers on the idea that making marks on a screen could be a live performance
> — that software could be played like an instrument. The bet compounded: the
> National Gallery of Denmark and KADIST ended up collecting the work, and the
> same conviction became Aesthetic Computer. I've spent a decade creating demand
> for one strange idea — that the computer is something you *play* — and the
> market is finally catching up to it.
