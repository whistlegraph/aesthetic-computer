## The one-line pitch

**Aesthetic Inc. is an instruments studio: we productize "new media
instruments" — starting with notepat — out of a creative-computing platform that
already has ~18,000 users and 16,000+ programs. We build technology you *play*,
not technology that makes you efficient.** ← maps directly to Restless Egg's
"luxury technology / beyond efficiency" thesis.

## Who's applying (the structure that makes this easy)

- **Founder:** Jeffrey Alan Scudder — painter (Yale MFA), toolmaker, educator
  (UCLA / Parsons), 65+ performances on "digital tools are painting's next
  material." A decade of building instruments people use.
- **Company:** **Aesthetic, Inc.** — already incorporated (C-corp, Nov 2024),
  already banking, already has an equity vehicle (`aesthetic.direct` + SAFE
  "booted-by" holders). Restless Egg = **lead investor into a real cap table**,
  not a from-scratch incorporation.

## Lead product: notepat (flagship), studio behind it

1. **notepat** — an expressive software instrument that runs everywhere, down to
   PID 1 on bare metal; a physical-modeling 128-voice GM synth as the moat;
   notepat.com live; Max-for-Live + VST3 in progress; HN front page ×2.
2. **Pipeline:** the nom educational-game family (shared engine + locale packs),
   KidLisp creative-coding, the AC Native instrument OS.
3. **Why a studio, not one app:** the shared runtime + synth engine + publishing
   commons is the factory; notepat is the first product off the line. Restless
   Egg funds turning the factory's output into retained, paid products.

## The 6 things the app must land (their proposal asks)

1. **The obsession/frontier-practice origin** — No Paint → AC → instruments. A
   decade-long bet that computing should be *played*.
2. **Technical / experiential originality** — bare-metal creative OS + physical
   modeling synth running on $50 hardware; nobody ships an instrument that boots
   as PID 1. This is the defensible "category-defining" claim.
3. **Real users / market demand** — ~18k accounts, 16k+ programs, HN traction.
   *Be honest about the retention gap* (see below) — they'll respect candor.
4. **Path to a durable business** — productize notepat (paid pro instrument /
   plugin / app), then the nom games (edtech family), with the platform as the
   funnel. "Shared Earnings" = profitable instrument company, not forced unicorn.
5. **Why now** — AI makes every user a programmer; the bare-metal OS just landed
   (Mar 2026); Windows-10 EOL strands cheap capable hardware. The instrument can
   go where the computer goes.
6. **Why Restless Egg specifically** — they fund *artist-founders* and *luxury
   tech beyond efficiency*; that's literally Aesthetic Inc.'s written mission.

## How Restless Egg supports the founder (the "get the money" section)

Frame the support honestly around what the capital actually changes:

- **Runway to go full-time on productization.** Today the founder's time is split
  across freelance gigs ($600–$1,200 invoices) and grant-chasing just to keep
  building. ~$100k initial + up to ~$175–200k follow-on = **6–18 months of
  focused founder runway** to ship notepat as a real product instead of a piece.
- **A lead on the cap table** turns the existing scattered SAFE "booted-by"
  supporters into a fundable round — it legitimizes and anchors what
  `aesthetic.direct` started but couldn't scale ("has not produced
  institutional-scale funding").
- **The salons / cohort / "philosopher of technology" network** is the right room
  for an artist-founder who has built alone — distribution, design, and the
  business-model thinking the project has deliberately avoided until now.
- **"Shared Earnings" fits the truth of the work.** AC doesn't need to pretend to
  be a hypergrowth SaaS; it can be a durable, profitable instruments business
  that keeps the commons alive underneath. The side letter lets the founder take
  the honest path without betraying the project.

## The honest risk section (lead with it — don't hide it)

- **Retention, not acquisition, is the problem.** ~92% one-and-done; ~1,457
  annually active; no analytics pipeline. *The whole point of the raise is to fix
  this* — turn a creative-output platform into a retained instrument product.
- **Revenue is small and diversified today.** State it plainly; the investment is
  what funds the first real product revenue.
- **Founder concentration.** One dominant maintainer; the raise funds a team.
- **Commons vs. company.** Be explicit that the open platform stays open; the
  company productizes instruments out of it. Don't let it read as enclosing the
  commons — that's the founder's value, and investors should buy *into* it.

## Strategic note (founder decision required)

This is genuinely worth doing IF the founder is willing to (a) dilute ~5% of
Aesthetic Inc., (b) commit to a *product* narrative for ~6 months, and (c)
reconcile any existing booted-by/SAFE terms with a lead investor. The "Shared
Earnings" structure makes (b) far less of a betrayal of the commons than a
typical accelerator would. **It does not conflict with SSRC** (different entity
posture: SSRC funds the *individual practitioner / commons*; Restless Egg funds
*the company*). You can pursue both honestly and simultaneously.
