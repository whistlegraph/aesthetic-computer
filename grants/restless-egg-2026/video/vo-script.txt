I'm Jeffrey Scudder — a painter who spent the last decade building instruments instead of paintings. This is notepat. A software instrument — keyboard, touch, MIDI, even pressure. Under it, a hundred and twenty-eight voice synthesizer I built from scratch — it models real instruments instead of playing back samples. The wild part — notepat runs anywhere. Your browser, your phone, and as the first thing that boots on a salvaged fifty-dollar laptop. No operating system underneath it. It grows out of Aesthetic Computer, where eighteen thousand people have already made sixteen thousand little programs. My company turns that into instruments people play, and pay for. I want to make computing something you play again. That's what I'd do with Restless Egg.
