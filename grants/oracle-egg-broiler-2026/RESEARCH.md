> **Deadline:** 2026-05-15, 23:59 PST

> **Length:** 3 to 7 days, full facility access

> **Run by:** two active musicians; artist-run incubator for experimental music & performance

## What BROILER supports

Ambitious new work in **sound and performance**: projects that challenge form,
expand sonic language, take risks. Projects should be in **early stages or
active development** — BROILER is not for finished work touring through.
Culminates in a public performance.

## Why AC fits

- **notepat.com** is an 8,466-line polyphonic instrument that runs in any
  browser and on AC Native OS at 192 kHz / 32 voices. It is *exactly* a
  laptop-orchestra instrument waiting for a room.
- The **PLORK paper** (`papers/arxiv-plork/`) literally argues for a planetary
  laptop orchestra. BROILER is a perfect-scale demo of that thesis.
- The **AC Library** lending fleet means we can bring 8–12 AC Blank ThinkPads,
  flash them on day 1, rehearse for 2–3 days, perform on day 5 — a full
  workshop-to-performance arc inside the residency window.
- Oracle Egg's audience is exactly the right one for *Whistlegraph* and the
  *Sucking on the Complex* paper — performance + score + open instrument.

## Suggested angle

**"Library Orchestra: notepat.com on twelve flashed laptops."** Pitch:
- Day 1: Flash 12 AC Blank ThinkPads (donated/used, $128/seat) — the room
  becomes an instrument library.
- Days 2–4: Open rehearsal with LA musicians and any Oracle Egg residents
  available; develop a 30–45 minute score for 12 networked notepats + voice
  + room mics. Use AC's WebSocket relay so all 12 laptops can phase, sync, and
  cross-modulate.
- Day 5 (or 6/7): Public ticketed performance. Documentation reel for online
  release.

This frames AC not as software but as a **room-scale performance instrument**,
which is BROILER's exact lane.
