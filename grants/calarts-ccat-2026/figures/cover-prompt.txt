A wide horizontal tableau on warm cream-colored paper, drawn in
colored pencil and gouache in a Hockney register: heavy hatching
for the dim corners of the studio, optical-mixing pencil marks for
projector-beam dust, sharp gouache rectangles for the GPU-sim
projection on the back wall, tapered confident strokes, the
composition gradually softening to nothing at all four edges so
the periphery DISSOLVES INTO PURE CREAM PAPER with no border, no
frame, no proscenium. Muted natural palette (terracotta, sage,
ochre, dusty pink, slate blue, brick red, warm grey, oxblood,
brass, walnut workbench tones) with TWO saturated pop-color
zones: (1) the Citrus-green of one Apple MacBook Neo on the
workbench (Apple's official Citrus colorway — clean lime-yellow
shell, lid open ~110°, with a hand-rendered four-stroke
whistlegraph butterfly in place of the apple emblem — this is
jeffrey's machine and only his); (2) the back-wall projection of
a moving GPU slime-mould / generative-biology pattern in
saturated chartreuse, bone white, and deep magenta — organic,
filamentous, mid-evolution, NEVER a recursive image of the room
itself.

Format: 1536×1024 horizontal (3:2). Subject is the lived posture
of a creative-computing technical director — two figures at a
workbench in a studio/lab, attention on the work, not on the
camera. Less institutional foyer, less dramatic spotlight; more
the immediate texture of the studio at 4 PM.

THE FRAME: a tight medium shot at workbench-height, low natural
late-afternoon light from a tall industrial window at the LEFT
edge of the frame (the window itself dissolves into cream paper at
the very edge). The room is a working creative-computing lab — a
long plywood workbench across the front, a single GPU tower
visible on a low shelf at upper-right (mesh side-panel, faint
green status LED, NOT a server rack), a projector mounted to a
ceiling track in the upper-middle of the frame casting a warm beam
across the back wall, a coil of HDMI and USB-C cables hanging on a
peg, a whiteboard at lower-right with quick warp-and-blend
calibration triangles drawn in marker (illegible scribble for any
labels), a small spare projector lens on the bench, and one
hand-labeled bin on a shelf reading "RACK 02" in scribble-gestalt
print.

THE FIGURES, in a single horizontal arrangement at peer eye-level
— same height, same lighting, no hero, no elevation, no centred
figure:

LEFT FIGURE (visiting artist): a person in their late twenties,
deep-blue knitted cardigan over a thin grey tee, round wire-frame
reading glasses pushed up onto their forehead, medium-brown hair
behind their ears. They are leaning slightly toward the workbench,
finger pointing softly at a specific cable on the bench (NOT at
the screen), expression deadpan attentive — mid-question. Their
own machine, a worn navy-blue ThinkPad with a faintly visible
trackpoint nub, sits open on the bench in front of them.

RIGHT FIGURE (jeffrey, FAR RIGHT, NEVER centred, head and
shoulder partially cropped at the right edge): render him from
the jeffrey-platter performance-domain references — medium-length
brown hair tucked behind ears, no beard, wearing a soft warm-rust
crewneck sweatshirt over a thin denim shirt visible at the collar
(cf. 2018-11-04 reference). One hand resting lightly on the
Citrus-green Neo's lid; the other holding a USB-C cable mid-route
toward the visiting artist's ThinkPad. Expression: focused,
deadpan-helpful, mid-explanation. Same eye-line as the visiting
artist, never elevated.

THE WORKBENCH (the visual centre, NOT a person): the
Citrus-green Neo with the whistlegraph-butterfly emblem on its
lid is the saturated focal point — slightly off-centre toward
jeffrey. Its screen shows a clean dark-grey terminal with a few
lines of monospaced text in scribble-gestalt — illegible, no real
words, but clearly a working command shell. Beside it: the
visiting artist's navy ThinkPad, lid open, screen showing a
single calibration test pattern (a warp grid of nine squares).
Between the two laptops: a small thermal-printed sticker label on
the bench edge, a coiled USB-C cable, and the spare projector lens
sitting on a microfiber cloth.

THE PROJECTION on the back wall: ONE landscape rectangle of GPU-
simulation imagery — chartreuse and bone-white filamentous
slime-mould patterns evolving across deep magenta, in a clear
"speculative-biology / GPU compute" register (cf. mxsage / Sage
Jenson's growth simulations, but original: bands of fine
optical-mixing dots and tapered tendrils, never a literal copy).
The projection is the brightest distant surface in the image,
warm dust catching in the projector beam (rendered in
optical-mixing pencil marks), and it never reflects the room
itself. NEVER recursive — the projection content is independent
of the scene.

LIGHTING: cool natural late-afternoon spill from the left window
on the visiting artist's shoulder; warm projector backwash from
the upper-middle on the back wall and on jeffrey's right
sleeve; one warm shop-lamp at the right edge of the bench (the
lamp itself implied, off-frame, casting a small warm pool on the
Citrus Neo); deep walnut-toned wood receding into the periphery
before fading to cream paper.

NO INSTITUTIONAL TEXT: no hand-lettered "CalArts", "CCAT",
"BB6", "Aesthetic Computer", "Technical Director", "Studio Hand"
spelled out anywhere. Identity is carried entirely by: the
Citrus-green Neo with the whistlegraph butterfly, the workbench
gear, the GPU-sim projection in the Jenson register, the peer
posture of the two figures, the worn cabling. Incidental product
text on physical objects is acceptable when NOT the institution
illustrated (the apple emblem on the lid is replaced by the
whistlegraph butterfly; a small generic "RACK 02" stamp on a
shelf bin; a faint projector model number; an illegible
terminal). Letterforms anywhere else appear as illegible
scribble-gestalt only.
