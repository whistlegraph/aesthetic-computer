A wide horizontal tableau on warm cream-colored paper, drawn in
colored pencil and gouache in a Hockney register: heavy hatching
for the dim corners of a lived-in home studio, optical-mixing
pencil marks for projector-beam dust, sharp gouache rectangles for
the GPU-sim projection on the back wall, tapered confident strokes,
the composition gradually softening to nothing at all four edges so
the periphery DISSOLVES INTO PURE CREAM PAPER with no border, no
frame, no proscenium. Muted natural palette (terracotta, sage,
ochre, dusty pink, slate blue, brick red, warm grey, oxblood,
brass, walnut workbench tones) with TWO saturated pop-color zones:
(1) the Citrus-green of one Apple MacBook Neo on the bench
(Apple's official Citrus colorway — clean lime-yellow shell, lid
open ~110°, with a hand-rendered four-stroke whistlegraph
butterfly in place of the apple emblem — this is jeffrey's machine
and only his); (2) the back-wall projection of a moving GPU slime-
mould / generative-biology pattern in saturated chartreuse, bone
white, and deep magenta — organic, filamentous, mid-evolution,
NEVER a recursive image of the room itself.

Format: 1536×1024 horizontal (3:2). Subject is jeffrey's own
working studio at late afternoon — a real, lived-in artist's
workspace in northeast LA, NOT a pristine institutional lab, NOT a
black-box theatre, NOT an art-school foyer. The walls are
exposed-brick or warm-painted plaster; the desk is reclaimed-wood
or scuffed plywood; this is the working room AC actually gets made
in.

THE FRAME: a tight medium shot at workbench-height, low natural
late-afternoon light spilling in from a tall window with a
half-drawn shade at the LEFT edge of the frame, the window itself
dissolving into cream paper at the very edge. Two figures at the
bench, both at peer eye-level, both fully visible, neither cropped
at the frame edge — equal prominence. Slight overlap is fine; the
visiting artist is at left, jeffrey is right of centre.

LEFT FIGURE (visiting artist): a person in their late twenties,
deep-blue knitted cardigan over a thin grey tee, round wire-frame
reading glasses pushed up onto their forehead, medium-brown hair
behind the ears, leaning slightly toward the bench, finger
pointing at a coiled USB-C cable on the bench (NOT at any
screen), expression deadpan-attentive — mid-question. Their own
machine, a worn navy-blue ThinkPad with a faintly visible
trackpoint nub, sits open in front of them.

RIGHT FIGURE (the studio host, FULLY in frame, head and torso
visible through the chest, NOT cropped at the right edge — same
prominence as the visiting artist, peer eye-level, never centred
or elevated). A man in his mid-thirties with medium-length
slightly-disheveled warm-brown hair falling just past the ears
(softly tousled, NOT slicked back, NOT clipped short), clean-
shaven, no beard, no stubble. Soft small smile of focused
concentration. Wearing a faded soft-pink graphic T-shirt over a
thin pale-denim shirt collar, a pair of white in-ear-style
headphones draped casually around his neck. One hand resting
lightly on the lid of the Citrus-green Neo; the other holding a
USB-C cable mid-route toward the visiting artist's ThinkPad.
Same eye-line as the visiting artist; both heads at the same
height in the frame.

THE WORKBENCH (the visual centre, NOT a person): the Citrus-green
Neo with the whistlegraph-butterfly emblem on its lid is the
saturated focal point — slightly off-centre toward jeffrey. Its
screen shows a dim dark-grey terminal with a few illegible lines
of monospaced scribble-gestalt — clearly a working command
shell, no real words. Beside it: the visiting artist's navy
ThinkPad, screen showing one calibration test pattern (a warp

half-finished coffee (cf. 2017-06-21 reference), and a sticky
note with a single illegible scribble. This is a desk that is
worked at, not staged.
