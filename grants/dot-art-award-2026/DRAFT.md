## Artist biography (≥100 words) — draft

Jeffrey Scudder (@jeffrey) is an artist and programmer in Los Angeles
who builds and performs his own painting software. Since 2016 his
instrument-like programs have been shown as live "radical digital
painting" performances and as public websites anyone can play. No
Paint, his collaboration with a proposing machine and a community of
brush makers, ran from 2016 through 2021 and now continues inside
Aesthetic Computer — a networked creative computing environment he has
developed in public since 2021, where every tool, brush, and painting
is addressable by URL. His work is held in public collections
[verify: KADIST, SMK — confirm exact credit lines], was profiled by
Rhizome in 2017, and passed through Akademie Schloss Solitude's Web
Residencies. He treats software as an artwork that does not end: a
living domain, versioned and replayable, maintained the way a painter
keeps a studio.

## Process narrative (≥100 words) — draft

No Paint is a game of consent between a painter and a proposing
machine. The software chooses a brush or a filter from a weighted deck
— Softy, Bubbles, Grid Worm, Walker, a rare wanderer, a common stamp —
and animates a live proposal over the painting. The person has two
buttons: **No** discards the proposal; **Paint** freezes time and
commits it. Every painting is a negotiated history of accepted
accidents. Between 2016 and 2021 the deck grew brushes and stamps
contributed by friends under painter handles, and playlists that
performed the deck in sequence. In 2026 the original project file was
recovered, hash-verified, and inventoried — 82 layouts, 1,197 images,
35 weighted proposals — and the loop was rebuilt natively inside
Aesthetic Computer, where it keeps running at the same address. The
artwork is the whole span: the person, the proposing machine, the
community, and the domain that holds them.
