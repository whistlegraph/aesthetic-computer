## What it is (2027 cycle — REDESIGNED)

**Important structural change.** Earlier cohorts (2022, 2023–25, 2024–26) were
**two-year awards at $100,000/year**. The cycle we're applying to is a
**one-year, unrestricted award of up to $60,000** (Jan–Dec 2027), plus up to
~$5,000 seed funding for collaborative projects between fellows. The public
positioning is more sober now: "rigorous, original, and community-grounded."
Don't anchor to the old "$250K / radically different futures" language.

## Eligibility (verbatim where it matters)

- "Citizens of any country may apply. **Fellows must reside in the United
  States for the duration of the fellowship year.**" (No visa sponsorship.)
  → Jeffrey qualifies (US-based). Aesthetic Inc is irrelevant — **individuals
  only, not organizations.**
- "There are **no formal degree requirements.**" Sustained record of work is
  accepted in lieu of credentials. → Good; no PhD gate (unlike Smarthistory).
- "Individuals currently enrolled as **full-time students are not eligible.**"
- Open to early- and mid-career; "researchers, artists, journalists,
  community-based researchers, social scientists, humanists, technologists,
  practitioners."

## Application components (exact limits)

1. **Résumé / CV** — up to **2 pages**.
2. **Personal statement** — written **≤1,000 words** *OR* video **≤5 min**.
3. **Work proposal** — written **≤3,000 words** *OR* deck **≤10 slides**.
4. **Two work samples** — each with a brief description.

The **work proposal must address** (treat as the sub-questions):
- Central concept / research question
- How the project engages technology substantively
- Your perspective, method, distinguishing approach
- Anticipated challenges and mitigation
- Awareness of related work / field context
- Public contribution and dissemination plans

> No separate budget line-item or recommender/reference field is listed on the
> public page (unrestricted award, individual applicant). **Verify inside the
> portal** before assuming they're absent.

## Selection criteria (their words)

- **Articulated Practice** — "a clearly defined research or creative practice
  and a coherent body of work."
- **Strength of Project** — "a compelling project that addresses significant
  questions at the intersection of technology and society... clearly describe
  the scope of work and what can be meaningfully advanced during the
  fellowship year."
- **Capacity to Execute** — "a demonstrated ability to carry projects forward
  and bring work to completion."
- **Public Engagement** — "evidence of sharing work publicly or maintaining
  meaningful engagement with relevant fields, audiences, or areas of practice."
- **Fit with Fellowship** — "how participation... would strengthen the
  proposed project and contribute to the cohort experience."
- **Collaboration** — "a willingness to share work in progress and participate
  in sustained exchange with peers."

## Mission / values language to mirror

- "Supports **rigorous, original, and community-grounded work that addresses
  pressing questions about how technology shapes society and public life.**"
- "Thoughtful, practical, and imaginative approaches to how technology is
  **designed, governed, and experienced in public life.**"
- Keywords: *intersection of technology and society*, *expands public
  understanding of technology*, *more informed and equitable technological
  futures*, *community-grounded*.

## Calibration — who wins (past fellows)

The roster skews to **artist-researchers and organizer-practitioners** with a
public-good / justice angle, NOT pure technologists. Direct precedent:

- **Lauren Lee McCarthy** (creator of **p5.js**) — 2024–26 fellow as "Artist."
  A creative-coding tool-builder won. This is our closest analog.
- **Johann Diedrick** (artist/engineer) — racial bias in speech AI.
- **Dorothy Santos**, **Romi Morrison**, **Petra Kuppers** (disability/crip
  futures, XR access), **Tawana Petty** (artist/organizer, surveillance),
  **Fernanda Rosa** (Indigenous internet infrastructure / data sovereignty).

**Framing takeaway:** lead with AC's *public/community dimension* and a
*society-facing question* — who owns the personal computer; what happens to the
people and machines a platform industry discards — and treat the runtime's
technical novelty as evidence of capacity, not the headline. Public engagement
(Flash Days, workshops, the 2,907-handle community, open source) maps directly
onto the "Public Engagement" + "Collaboration" criteria.
