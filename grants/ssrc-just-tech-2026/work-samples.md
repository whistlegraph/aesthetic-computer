### Jeffrey Alan Scudder · (program asks for **two** samples, each with a brief description)

The program requires **two** work samples with brief descriptions. Lead with
#1 and #2 below; #3–#4 are backups depending on what the portal accepts (video

## Sample 1 — AC Native: a public computer, owned down to the kernel
**Format:** short video (≤2 min) of a salvaged ThinkPad booting from USB into the
creative OS in ~7 seconds, plus a link to the public source.
**Link:** `https://aesthetic.computer` · AC Native source in `fedac/native/` ·
*reuse / re-cut the LACMA boot footage in `grants/lacma-2026/` (video-cards, vo).*

**Description (draft, ~55 words):**
> A bare-metal creative operating system that boots a fifty-dollar salvaged
> laptop into a full instrument — sound, graphics, input, network — in about
> seven seconds, with your identity in the device rather than a cloud login. It
> is the project's thesis made physical: owning the personal computer all the
> way down to the first process, on hardware the market discarded.

---

## Sample 2 — Aesthetic Computer: the community commons
**Format:** a short screen capture of the living system — chat overlaid on a
piece, a member's "mood" becoming the site banner, and a member's published
`@handle/piece` opening at its own web address — plus the link.
**Link:** `https://aesthetic.computer`

**Description (draft, ~55 words):**
> A creative-computing runtime and small online society — 2,907 handles, 402
> community-authored programs — where people make pieces in JavaScript or
> KidLisp alongside chats, moods, and a news board, each living at a shareable
> web address. Self-hosted, local-first, no ads or tracking. The commons the
> proposal sets out to widen and sustain.

## Backup samples (use if a third slot opens or to swap)

### Sample 3 — a KidLisp `$code` piece (accessibility as a civic property)
**Link:** a single `$code` piece, e.g. `https://aesthetic.computer/$berz` · QR-shareable.
> A few lines of a deliberately tiny language (118 built-ins) produce generative
> art that anyone can run, fork, or share by QR. Made for, and circulated in,
> Casey Reas and Lauren Lee McCarthy's UCLA Social Software studio — accessibility
> as a civic property: the whole instrument fits in one mind.

### Sample 4 — notepat (a tool people depend on)
**Link:** `https://aesthetic.computer/notepat`
> An open-source instrument that turns a phone or laptop keyboard into a playable,
> shareable score, running identically in a browser and as the default piece on
> the bare-metal OS. Reached the front page of Hacker News — a tool built for
> other people to make their own work with.
