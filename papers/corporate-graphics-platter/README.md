# Corporate Graphics — platter

A reference library of historic bank, corporate, seal, and privacy/identity
marks, collected to inform the **REGARDE** logo work (the consent gate —
*"nothing about you, without you."*). The brief is *trust*, and this is the
lineage a trust/consent mark should sit inside: notary seals, institutional
monograms, and the modernist abstract marks that made organizations feel solid.

**66 marks** fetched from Wikimedia Commons, organized into four sections with
per-mark provenance (designer, year, motif, aesthetic register, why-it-matters).
The whole point is **aesthetic range** — to break out of a single flat-vector
default and find genuinely different ways in.

## How this informs REGARDE

Four distinct registers to pull the logo toward, instead of the flat-green-on-cream
default:

1. **Engraved seal / intaglio** — the Great Seal's all-seeing eye, Treasury's
   scales (1789), the Penny Black. A consent *receipt* is literally a seal; the
   anti-surveillance pitch can *reclaim* the all-seeing-eye as a **returned gaze**.
2. **Swiss constructivist** — Stankowski's Deutsche Bank slash, NatWest's cubes:
   one geometric idea, growth held in a stable frame.
3. **Heraldic-flattened** — Lloyds' horse, Barclays' eagle, UBS's keys: a guardian
   emblem modernized to flat. A gate-keeper animal is on-brief.
4. **Negative-space / figure-ground** — FedEx's arrow, PBS's faces: the meaning
   hidden in the counter. REGARDE's "you held inside the gate" wants exactly this.

The surveillance-consent section maps the **clichés to diverge from**: padlocks,
blue shields, green checks, fingerprints. REGARDE's opening is to look *unlike*
a security vendor — warmer, more civic, a seal not a shield.
