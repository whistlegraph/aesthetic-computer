A single white modernist university art building in the crisp Richard Meier idiom: a clean rectilinear box of porcelain-enameled metal panels on a tight grid, a tall ribbon of glass curtain-wall running up one corner, a thin cantilevered canopy over the entrance, and a slender rooftop clerestory catching pale daylight. The volume is rendered in three-quarter view, its flat white planes faceted into cool grays and warm cream where the sun rakes across, the precise panel seams drawn as faint pencil ruling. The ground floor reads as an open glass lobby, dark and transparent, the rest a quiet stack of solid white walls. Bold and architectural, the lone building filling the frame and dissolving at its footings into bare paper.
