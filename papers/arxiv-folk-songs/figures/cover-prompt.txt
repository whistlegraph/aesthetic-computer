A single chunky computer-keyboard keycap, the kind plucked from a QWERTY laptop, standing alone and slightly tilted so its sculpted top face and beveled sides both read clearly. Its square top is not stamped with a letter but engraved with one bold folk-song musical note — a single solid eighth note with a curling flag — worn smooth as if pressed a thousand times in an oral-tradition sing-along. The plastic has the warm, slightly yellowed, hand-played patina of an old surplus laptop, with faint fingerprint sheen near the center. It rests as a lone emblem, a folk melody compressed into one tappable key.
