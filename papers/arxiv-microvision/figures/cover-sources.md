# Cover-prompt sources — MicroVision

The cover is **a single MEMS scanning micromirror with a green laser beam fanning into a dissolving raster sweep**. Each visual element traces to a real reference about MicroVision's founding technology and product lineage.

## Visual elements → sources

| Element | Source |
|---|---|
| **Polished silicon micromirror in a ceramic chip carrier** | The literal founding technology of MicroVision (1993–present). All MicroVision products from the PicoP pico-projector engine (2014–) through MAVIN, MOVIA L, and MOVIA S lidar (2020–) are built around the same biaxial MEMS scanning-mirror primitive. A single tilting silicon mirror, fabricated using semiconductor processes, deflects a laser to draw an image or scan a scene — no LCD panel, no fixed pixel grid. Source: [MicroVision technology overview](https://microvision.com/), Q4 2018 conference call notes (in family archive `microvision/2019-03-10-pps-calculations.md`). |
| **Green emerald laser beam** | The visual signature of the consumer pico-projection era. Sony MPCL1 (2016), Celluon PicoPro / PicoAir (2016, $299–$450), Sharp Robohon, VOGA V phone (2017, China). All used the MicroVision laser-scanning engine; the green channel of the RGB laser source is the most visually iconic. Source: [`microvision/2016-01-06-ces-2016.md`](../../../microvision/2016-01-06-ces-2016.md), [`microvision/2017-07-26-voga-v-phone.md`](../../../microvision/2017-07-26-voga-v-phone.md). |
| **Biaxial Lissajous raster sweep** | The mathematical signature of MEMS biaxial scanning. Two orthogonal mirror axes oscillating at slightly different frequencies trace out a Lissajous pattern; this is the literal pixel-laydown path of every MicroVision-engine projection or scan. The dissolving-green-dot fan in the cover is the raster sweep mid-frame. Source: MEMS scanning theory, MicroVision product literature (lib MAVIN datasheet, [microvision.com/sensors/mavin-n](https://microvision.com/sensors/mavin-n)). |
| **Gold bond wires from a ceramic chip carrier** | Standard semiconductor packaging for early-2000s MEMS-on-CMOS dies. The visual cue says "this is a chip" without naming a manufacturer. |
| **Faded periphery dissolving into cream** | Vignette aesthetic; the mirror is the iconic emblem, the periphery is uncited (intentional). |

## Founder + key people referenced

- **Glen DeVos** — current CEO (2024–), architect of the "Lidar 2.0" / "Pivot to Profit" strategy. Not visually depicted (cover prioritizes the founding technology over the personnel).
- **Walter Scudder** — the family-archive author whose eleven-year correspondence with the MVIS-blog community feeds half the references. Not depicted, but the dossier exists because of his record.

## What was deliberately excluded

- Any specific product housing (MAVIN box, MOVIA L unit, Sony MPCL1) — would tie the emblem to one product era and miss the technology continuity.
- The MicroVision wordmark / logo — per cover doctrine, the institution is named only by emblem, never by readable text.
- Any car, autonomous vehicle, mining truck, or AR-glasses silhouette — would over-narrate the application and miss the ambidexterity of the underlying tech.
- A point-cloud rendering of a scene — too generic; every lidar company shows point clouds. The MEMS mirror is what's specific.
