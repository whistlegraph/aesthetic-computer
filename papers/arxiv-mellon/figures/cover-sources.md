# Cover-prompt sources — Mellon Foundation

The cover is a **single floating set of brass apothecary balance scales**, with grant-letters stacked on each pan, a column-with-helmet fulcrum, and falling coin-discs beneath. Each element is sourced.

## Visual elements → sources

| Element | Source |
|---|---|
| **Brass balance scales (apothecary, two pans, central fulcrum)** | The **canonical visual metaphor for a private foundation's work**: weighing applications, allocating support. Mellon awards roughly $635M/year in grants (FY2024) — every grant is a weight on one pan, every program area a separate balance. The scales also reference Lady Justice (which Mellon's giving in the Monuments Project and Creatives Rebuild New York implicitly invokes). |
| **Rolled grant-letters on each pan** | Mellon's **grant database** at mellon.org/grants/grants-database/ documents tens of thousands of awards. The rolled letters tied with thin ribbons read as both "old-money correspondence" and "grant award letter" — the actual artifact a recipient receives is a wire-transfer + a formal letter. |
| **Column-with-helmet finial as fulcrum** | The **Mellon family's classical-architecture inheritance**: Andrew W. Mellon (1855–1937, Treasury Secretary) endowed the National Gallery of Art's neoclassical building (1941) and the Mellon family townhouse aesthetic was Beaux-Arts. The Athena/classical-helmet finial nods to civic-virtue-as-philanthropy. Source: [Wikipedia: Andrew W. Mellon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andrew_W._Mellon), [National Gallery of Art history](https://www.nga.gov/about/history.html). |
| **Falling coin-discs beneath the scales** | Visualizes the **5% IRS-required minimum payout** for private foundations. Mellon disbursed $635M in FY2024 against ~$7.8B in total assets — ~8.1% payout rate, above the floor. The coins falling represent qualifying distributions out into the field. |
| **Brass surfaces with warm reflective light** | Pulled from the [Mellon townhouse interiors](https://www.mellon.org/people/trustees) — the org's NYC headquarters (140 East 62nd Street historically; current 140 E 62nd) has the wood-paneled-board-room aesthetic of old-money philanthropy. |

## Founder + key people referenced (none depicted)

The cover deliberately shows objects, not people — Mellon's identity is institutional. But the visual references the lineage:
- **Avalon Foundation** (1940, founded by **Ailsa Mellon Bruce**) + **Old Dominion Foundation** (1941, founded by **Paul Mellon**) merged June 30, 1969 to form the Andrew W. Mellon Foundation. EIN 13-1879954.
- Presidents: Hamilton (1969–71) → Pusey (1971–75) → **Sawyer (1975–87)** → **Bowen (1988–2006)** → Randel (2006–13) → Lewis (2013–18) → **Elizabeth Alexander (2018–)**.
- Alexander's tenure (2018–) brought the 2020 rebrand (Pentagram), the [Monuments Project](https://www.mellon.org/initiatives/monuments-project) ($250M 2020, doubled to $500M 2023), and Creatives Rebuild New York ($115M 2021).

## What was deliberately excluded

- **Any portrait of Andrew W. Mellon, Paul Mellon, or Ailsa Mellon Bruce** — politically charged (Mellon family wealth ties to Gulf Oil, Alcoa).
- The 2020 [Pentagram-designed wordmark rebrand](https://www.pentagram.com/work/mellon-foundation) — a wordmark would break the no-text rule.
- The Monuments Project's specific funded works (Confederate-monument removals, alternative memorial commissions) — too narrative for a cover.
- The Avalon + Old Dominion 1969 merger — visualized as a single balance, not two.
