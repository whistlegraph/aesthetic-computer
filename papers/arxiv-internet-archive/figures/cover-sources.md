# Cover-prompt sources — Internet Archive

The cover illustration is a **floating Greek Revival temple** with translucent walls revealing book stacks, a single book rising from the dome, faint web-graph cobwebs around the upper third. Each visual element traces to a real reference.

## Visual elements → sources

| Element | Source |
|---|---|
| **The Greek Revival temple silhouette** (Corinthian columns, triangular pediment, low dome) | The actual Internet Archive headquarters at 300 Funston Avenue, San Francisco. Purchased September 2009 for $4.5M from the Fourth Church of Christ, Scientist. 1923 Greek Revival design by **Carl Werner**. The building's facade is so identified with the org that it appears in the IA logo lockup. Photo: archive.org/about, [Wikimedia](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Internet_Archive_(building)). |
| **Translucent walls revealing book stacks** | Internal scanning operations + Wayback Machine archive philosophy: "the building is the archive." 99 PB stored as of October 2025; 1 trillion web pages archived. Source: archive.org/details/about. |
| **Single book floating up from the dome apex** | Open Library / Controlled Digital Lending program. The visual implies preservation-as-ascension. Conceptually riffs on the Hachette v. Internet Archive ruling that constrains CDL but doesn't end it. |
| **Stained-glass windows with abstract pixel patterns** | The original church's stained glass remains in the building. The pixel-pattern overlay is a visual rhyme: church-as-archive, sacred-text-as-data, religious-iconography → digital-iconography. |
| **Faint web-graph cobweb lines around the upper third** | The Wayback Machine's defining metaphor — the Web's link topology preserved as a graph. Visual reference: IA's own [Wayback Machine logo](https://archive.org/web/) (sphere with timeline tick marks) abstracted to a network of fine lines. |
| **Dust palette: warm yellows, dusty teals, oxblood reds, soft graphite** | Pulled from period photography of the Funston Ave church and from IA's longstanding visual identity (the Wayback Machine's amber-on-black UI). |

## Founder reference (not depicted in this cover, intentional)

**Brewster Kahle** founded Internet Archive in May 1996 after selling Alexa Internet to Amazon (1999, $250M stock). He is "Founder / Digital Librarian / Board Chair" and runs the Kahle/Austin Foundation. The cover deliberately shows the *building*, not the man — IA's institutional posture is that the archive outlasts its founder.

## What was deliberately excluded

- No human figures (the temple-as-object reading is cleaner without them)
- No legible text or org name (the building reads as IA without a wordmark)
- No cyberpunk neon (IA's public face is library-warm, not techno-cool)
- No reference to the Hachette/UMG litigation (a cover is not the place; the dossier's §6 covers it)
