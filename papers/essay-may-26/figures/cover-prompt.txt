A single soft cumulus weather-forecast cloud, the kind drawn on a meteorological chart — one rounded, billowing white-and-gray puff with a flat base, rendered as a lone drifting form. Its outline is loose and uncertain, edges feathering into the bare cream paper as if it could become rain or clear away at any moment. A few faint pencil dashes of possible drizzle hang under its base, trailing off and undecided. The cloud is the whole image: a forecast of maybe, weather that has not yet committed to anything.
