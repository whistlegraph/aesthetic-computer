## 2014 Title IX walkout

- Sexual assault case ("Regina"); school found the assailant responsible; not
  criminally prosecuted. Al Jazeera America article **Oct 14, 2014**.
- **Oct 15, 2014**: students interrupted a board of trustees meeting (board
  then included Don Cheadle and *L.A. Times* publisher Austin M. Beutner).
- **Oct 23, 2014**: walkout from classes; peaceful occupation of
  administrative offices; community meeting of hundreds in the main building.
- Faculty solidarity: Prof. Matias Viegener (27 yrs at CalArts); artist/prof.
  Sam Durant joined the office occupation. Key organizer: MFA student Cori
  Redstone. Student demands: school-wide consent training, reformed
  adjudication, transparency, return to shared governance.
- CalArts was one of **85 colleges** under federal Title IX investigation.
  — Steinhauer, Hyperallergic (2014); Artforum (2014); Al Jazeera (2014).

## Financial crisis / layoffs / unionization (2024–2026)

- Enrollment down ~12% post-pandemic: **1,532 (2019) → 1,353 (2023)**.
  Tuition **>$60,000** for 2025–26. Endowment averaged ~$223M (as of 2022).
- Projected **$15M structural deficit**; ~$5.5M saved via spending controls,
  hiring delays, travel limits.
- Rajan told *Inside Higher Ed* (April 2024) CalArts was **"fiscally sound."**
- Spring 2025 voluntary separation: offered to 50–60; **32 accepted (22
  faculty, 10 staff)**.
- **July 2025**: 9 administrative employees laid off (5 union, 4 non-union);
  12 vacant positions cut. Rajan's July 15 letter: *"These are not just
  abstract positions—they are roles held by our colleagues, teammates, and
  friends."*
- Unionization: fall 2024 organizing; **Dec 2024** UAW intent letter to Rajan
  (~600-worker unit); **March 2025** election; **April 2025** ratified.
- *Toward a Sustainable Financial Future for CalArts* (May 2025), CalArts.
  — Hyperallergic (2025); The Art Newspaper (Nov 2024); CalArts (May 2025).

## Founding / OS history

- CalArts founded **1961** by Walt Disney (merger of Chouinard Art Institute +
  Los Angeles Conservatory of Music); Valencia campus opened **1971**;
  brutalist main building by Ladd & Kelsey. Faculty incl. Kaprow, Baldessari,
  Chicago, Schapiro, Paik.
  — Singerman, *Art Subjects* (1999); prior paper `arxiv-calarts/calarts.tex`.
- **Womanhouse** (Jan 30 – Feb 28, 1972): Feminist Art Program, Judy Chicago &
  Miriam Schapiro; 27 collaborators; ~10,000 visitors; Demetrakas documentary.
  Program ran 1971–1974.
  — Womanhouse, Wikipedia (2024); CalArts Library, *Reverberating Feminisms*.
