# Cover-prompt sources — A Fraserin' Art + Tech

The cover is a **floating tangle of institutional artifacts** — clipboard, 990 tax form, donor-wall plaque, banjo, picket sign, cables, small CRT — arranged as a sculptural cluster, dense in the middle, fading at the edges. Each element is a literal piece of the paper's argument.

## Visual elements → sources

| Element | Source |
|---|---|
| **Wooden clipboard with vertical column of names** | Andrea Fraser's signature institutional-critique move: take inventory. *Museums, Money, and Politics* (2018, with Westreich) is, materially, a clipboard rendered as a 700-page book — every U.S. art-museum trustee and their political donations, listed. The clipboard on the cover is that book in its working-tool form. |
| **Unfolded IRS Form 990 silhouette** | The other Fraser tool. Move 6 of the grammar: report the ledger. The 990 is the public document that turns governance into surfacable record; the AC dossier series uses it on every recipient (Rhizome, Eyebeam, SFPC, Pioneer Works, NEW INC, Studio Museum, The Kitchen, Machine Project, Internet Archive) and on the funder side (Mellon's 990-PF). |
| **Donor-wall plaque with engraved name-bands** | The institution's own self-display. Fraser's late work treats the donor wall as art-historical material, not metadata. The plaque is the wall in portable form. |
| **Folk-music banjo or acoustic guitar resting diagonally** | The labor-folk register the title invokes — Almanac Singers, Pete Seeger, Woody Guthrie. The instrument is the form-as-content marker: the song *is* the picket line. Cites Strand G of the prior-art survey. |
| **Picket sign on a wooden stick (blank)** | The 2019–2024 art-museum staff unionizations (MoMA Local 2110, New Museum Union, Whitney Workers United) and the cultural-worker tradition. Blank because the paper isn't a manifesto; the sign is structural, not slogan. |
| **Thin cables threading through the cluster** | The "art-and-tech" half of the title. The cluster is networked, infrastructural, plugged-in — Eyebeam, Rhizome, SFPC, NEW INC, Recurse all live by the cable. |
| **Small CRT-monitor silhouette glowing in the dense center** | Period-marker for the "media arts" rubric the paper widens out from. Same iconography used on the Rhizome dossier cover, deliberately quoted. |
| **Faded periphery, vignette dissolve** | Standard AC dossier-cover treatment. The figure is the iconic form; the periphery is uncited and intentional. |

## Intellectual sources referenced

- Andrea Fraser, "From the Critique of Institutions to an Institution of Critique" (Artforum, 2005)
- Andrea Fraser & Helen Molesworth (eds.), *Museums, Money, and Politics* (MIT/Whitney, 2018)
- Andrea Fraser, "L'1%, c'est moi" (2011)
- Hans Haacke, *Shapolsky et al. Manhattan Real Estate Holdings* (1971) — the pre-Fraser ledger work; precedent for the 990-as-art move
- Mierle Laderman Ukeles, *Manifesto for Maintenance Art* (1969) — labor-side complement
- Almanac Singers / Pete Seeger / Woody Guthrie — folk-tune cadence the title invokes
- W.A.G.E. fee schedule — the artist-labor standards-as-critique tradition

## What was deliberately excluded

- No human figures. The institutions are the subject, not the participants.
- No readable text on any artifact (clipboard names, picket-sign slogan, donor-wall band — all illegible). The paper is about the *form* of these documents, not specific names.
- No specific institutional logos or building exteriors. The dossiers' covers each carry their institution's iconography; this cover sits above them and quotes none.
- No Andrea Fraser portrait. The paper cites her work, doesn't profile her.
