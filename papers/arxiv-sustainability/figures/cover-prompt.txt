A single empty glass tip jar, the kind set out beside a cash register, drawn in three-quarter view. The clear jar is nearly empty: just one lone coin resting at the bottom and a curling slip of paper money slumped against the inner wall. A worn slot cut into the screw-on lid waits for donations that have not come. Light catches the curved glass with a few pale highlights; the jar stands alone, casting a soft pencil shadow, its emptiness the whole point.
