\noindent\textbf{Summary.}\quad
Scott Moore (online as \textit{@notscottmoore}) is a co-founder of Gitcoin and
the founder and a director of Public Works LP. He occupies the position where
the design of quadratic, or ``plural,'' public-goods funding meets the capital
that moves through it, and he has carried that position into the
art-and-technology field directly: Public Works states that it funds
``artists and creative technologists,'' and Moore holds named roles at the
New Museum's NEW INC and at Gray Area. This document maps that node from the
public record alone. Each material claim is sourced; where the record is
incomplete or could not be verified, this is stated rather than inferred. The
occasion for the inquiry is private and is excluded by design.

% ===================== 1 =====================
\section{Subject and scope}

The subject is read here not for biography but as an instrument: through one
well-documented individual, the financing structure of open-source and
``public-goods'' software infrastructure --- and its recent extension into
creative technology --- becomes legible. The method is restricted to publicly
recoverable material: corporate and securities filings, organizational
listings, primary writing, and recorded interviews. Personal and family
matters are out of scope and were not pursued; the public record on the
private individual is thin by his own design, and that boundary is respected
here.

% ===================== 2 =====================
\section{Background and professional trajectory}

Moore is Canadian and was educated at the University of Toronto, where he
studied political science and mathematics and later returned for data
science.\refn{2} His pre-cryptocurrency employment is consistent across
profile aggregators: a machine-learning role at Litchfield Capital Advisors, a
data-analyst role at Bell, a research assistantship at the University of
Toronto, and an international-trade assistantship with the U.S. Department of
Commerce. He co-founded the early ventures Finnovate.io and GitToken (the
latter acqui-hired) and served on grants-advisory committees at ConsenSys and
the Ethereum Community Fund before Gitcoin became his principal
work.\refn{2,4} He describes himself publicly as a ``pixel art + open source
enthusiast'' and maintains a presence on the digital-art platform
Foundation.\refn{1}

% ===================== 3 =====================
\section{The funding mechanism and its lineage}

The mechanism Moore is associated with has a precise intellectual lineage.
Its theoretical basis is the 2018 paper \textit{Liberal Radicalism: A
Flexible Design for Philanthropic Matching Funds} by Vitalik Buterin, Zoë
Hitzig, and E. Glen Weyl, in which a project's matched allocation scales with
the square of the sum of the square roots of its contributions, so that the
breadth of support outweighs its concentration.\refn{5} The design was
popularized under the name ``quadratic funding,'' and subsequently
``plural funding,'' through Weyl's RadicalxChange initiative,\refn{6} and was
first implemented at scale by Gitcoin. A later variant, retroactive public
goods funding, rewards demonstrated impact after the fact and is operated
chiefly by the Optimism Collective, which has earmarked a substantial share
of its token supply for that purpose.\refn{9} Moore's continuing involvement
on the theoretical side is reflected in advisory roles with Metagov and the
Plurality Institute, the latter associated with Weyl.\refn{1}

% ===================== 4 =====================
\section{Institutional descent: Gitcoin}

Gitcoin was founded in November 2017 by Kevin Owocki; Moore and Vivek Singh
are recorded as the other two co-founders, with Moore joining full time in
May 2018 under an arrangement in which the co-founder designation vested over
four years.\refn{3} The project was incubated within ConsenSys, the Ethereum
venture studio associated with Joseph Lubin. In April 2021 Gitcoin raised a
\$11.3 million round led by Paradigm and spun out of ConsenSys as an
independent entity.\refn{4} It subsequently issued the GTC governance token
and organized a token-holder treasury (the Gitcoin DAO), with delegated
``stewards'' directing allocations, and unbundled its matching machinery into
the Grants Stack and Allo Protocol so that third parties could operate their
own rounds.\refn{1,9}

% ===================== 5 =====================
\section{Public Works LP: structure and the public financial record}

Moore's current principal vehicle is Public Works. The organization presents
itself as ``an open source ecosystem fund focused on supporting artists and
creative technologists,'' and lists supported projects including Fuser,
Gitcoin, Metalabel, and Spline.\refn{7,8} Its stated thesis, ``venture
mutualism,'' calls for committing the minimum necessary capital, participating
as a contributor rather than a passive investor, and progressively
distributing ownership to the community; a stated secondary aim is to return
value to the Gitcoin grants ecosystem.\refn{10,11}

The corresponding legal entity is identifiable in United States securities
filings. \textbf{Public Works LP} (SEC CIK 0001998151) is a limited
partnership organized in the \textbf{Cayman Islands} in 2023, with a
registered office at Willow House, Cricket Square, Grand Cayman. It filed a
single Form D notice of exempt offering on 25 October 2023.\refn{12} The
filing characterizes the issuer as a \textbf{pooled investment fund} of the
\textbf{private-equity} type, not registered under the Investment Company Act,
and claims the Rule~506(b) exemption together with the Section~3(c)(1)
investment-company exclusion. The named related persons --- recorded as
\textbf{directors} --- are \textbf{Scott Moore}, Danielle Faul, and Jacobus
Pietersen. The offering amount and the amount remaining are both stated as
\textbf{indefinite}; the total amount sold is reported as \textbf{\$0}, with
the first sale recorded as not yet having occurred at the time of
filing.\refn{12}

Two observations follow strictly from the document. First, the appearance of
``Scott Moore'' as a director of Public Works LP independently corroborates
the identification made through the New Museum and Gray Area listings
(Section~6); the three sources agree. Second, the public financial picture is
deliberately bounded: a Cayman-domiciled 3(c)(1) private fund relying on
Rule~506(b) is not required to disclose its limited partners, and the only
public filing reports an indefinite target and no sales, never subsequently
amended. The existence and form of the fund are recoverable; its size, its
limited partners, and any closes after October 2023 are not, and no figure
for them should be asserted.

Three further checks bound the picture. First, ``Public Works'' returns no
match in the SEC's Investment Adviser Public Disclosure system: the fund is
neither a registered nor an exempt-reporting investment adviser, so no
Form~ADV --- the route through which regulatory assets under management would
otherwise be disclosed --- exists.\refn{13} Second, the entity has no
predecessor names and no separately registered United States general-partner
or management vehicle in EDGAR; the single 2023 notice is the entire federal
trail.\refn{12} Third --- and this resolves the fund's administration
apparatus from public sources --- the two directors other than Moore are both
\textbf{independent directors supplied by Hash}, a Cayman crypto-specialist
fund-governance firm, and the registered office is that of \textbf{Campbells}
(Campbells Corporate Services Limited, ``Floor~4, Willow House, Cricket
Square''). Danielle Faul is a chartered accountant who joined Hash as an
independent non-executive director in 2022 (also BACI treasurer and founder
of Women in Blockchain Cayman); ``Cobus'' Pietersen is likewise a Hash
independent director (formerly Walkers, MUFG Investor Services, and UBS).
Their recurrence on other digital-asset funds --- Faul on Coinbase
Multi-Strategy (US) Feeder Fund LP, Pietersen on Resolv Digital Assets Ltd.
--- reflects that independent-director capacity, not employment by those
funds: Public Works LP is governed through outsourced professional
directorship and corporate-services providers, the customary structure for a
Cayman private fund, not a bespoke board.\refn{13} The fund administrator
proper (the net-asset-value and transfer-agency provider) is named in no
public filing; that detail resides only in the CIMA register addressed below.
(An unrelated registered representative of the same name, based in Florida,
appears in adviser-disclosure search results and is not the subject of this
profile.\refn{13})

The same boundedness appears outside the securities record. The fund's public
website is a single page of mission text with no portfolio, team, or
disclosure section; the Internet Archive holds only that page and the
\textit{mutualism} essay, the earliest from February 2023.\refn{10,14} The
Cayman Islands Monetary Authority register --- queried directly --- confirms
\textbf{Public Works LP as a registered Private Fund} (reference 2051840),
status active, with a status-effective date of \textbf{22 June 2023}. This is
materially informative: registration under the Private Funds Act is triggered
by the acceptance of capital commitments (within twenty-one days, and before
contributions are received), so a June 2023 effective date indicates the fund
had reached the commitment stage by mid-2023 --- roughly four months before
the United States Form~D of October 2023 still reported no sales and a first
sale ``yet to occur.'' The most economical reading is that the Form~D notices
a distinct Regulation~D offering or feeder against an already-registered
Cayman fund rather than contradicting it; the two are not reconciled here
beyond noting the sequence. The fund's auditor is not exposed by the public
register --- it is carried in the non-public annual return --- and so remains
unnamed; that is now a property of Cayman disclosure law, not an unresolved
search.\refn{14} On the other side, fundraising trackers do surface one
deal explicitly attributed to ``Public Works'': participation in the
approximately \$3.2 million seed round of Rise Labs in September 2024, a
syndicate that also included Vitalik Buterin --- closing the lineage of
Section~3, in which a co-author of the founding mechanism appears as a
co-investor in the founder's fund.\refn{15} Other transactions listed under
``Scott Moore'' on those trackers reflect his individual angel activity and
are not separable by those sources from the fund; they are indicative, not
audited. Finally, in recorded interview Moore describes the model in his own
terms --- investing while directing the resulting surplus back to the
community and encouraging portfolio projects to ``exit to community'' --- but
states no fund size, limited-partner base, or check size.\refn{16} The
absence of those figures is therefore consistent across the securities
filing, the web record, and the founder's own public account; it is a
property of the structure, not a gap in the search.

\section{Position in the art-and-technology field}

The element most relevant to Aesthetic Computer is that Moore's funding
practice is not adjacent to art and technology but explicitly directed at it.
Public Works names ``artists and creative technologists'' as its constituency,
and two named institutional roles place Moore inside the same cluster
documented elsewhere in this papermill:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{New Museum / NEW INC.} Moore is listed as a \textbf{Mentor} at
NEW INC, the New Museum's incubator, in the ``Office Hours'' mentor category
for \textbf{Year Twelve}; the page was last updated in September 2025,
indicating a current engagement.\refn{8} NEW INC is itself the subject of an
existing dossier in this collection, embedded in the New Museum's filings;
this connects the subject directly to the established corpus.
\item \textbf{Gray Area.} Moore is listed as a \textbf{board member} and
described as ``an active advisor to Gray Area, a non-profit cultural center
in San Francisco'' --- a creative-coding institution in the same lineage as
the organizations dossiered elsewhere here.\refn{7,8}
\end{itemize}

\noindent The same New Museum listing also records him as a contributor to
``extitutional academic collectives including Metagov, where he researches
networked human coordination,''\refn{8} consistent with the advisory roles
noted in Section~3.

% ===================== 7 =====================
\section{Affiliations and investment activity}

Beyond the principal vehicles, public listings record board positions with
the ZKsync Foundation and the Ethereum Attestation Service; steward roles in
the ENS, Gnosis Safe, and Optimism public-goods communities; and advisory
roles with the Plurality Institute, Metagov, and ENS Labs.\refn{1,2} As an
angel investor he is recorded across aggregators with on the order of
twenty-two disclosed investments concentrated in web3, infrastructure, and
decentralized finance, the most recent on record dated August 2025.\refn{2}
These figures derive from third-party aggregators and should be read as
indicative rather than audited.

% ===================== 8 =====================
\section{Quantitative claims and their reconciliation}

Published figures for the capital Moore is credited with directing do not
reconcile, and the discrepancy is a function of scope rather than dispute.
Gitcoin's own materials cite on the order of \$70 million directed through
quadratic funding across all operators; one biographical source attributes
``upwards of \$50 million'' to Moore specifically; other profiles cite a
cumulative figure above \$500 million across Gitcoin's full
history.\refn{1,5,9} The three measure different things --- individual
attribution, a single mechanism, and an organization's lifetime activity ---
and no audited reconciliation exists. Any single number should be treated as
a scoped claim, not a settled fact.

% ===================== 9 =====================
\section{Limits of the public record}

For completeness, the boundaries of this profile are stated directly. The
New Museum and Gray Area affiliations, previously uncertain, are now verified
from primary listings and a securities filing. The Public Works fund's
structure is recoverable --- the absence of a Form~ADV, a single
tracker-attributed co-investment, and an administration apparatus resolved to
named providers (Campbells for corporate services and registered office; Hash
for independent directors) --- and CIMA registration as an active
Private Fund (reference 2051840, effective June 2023) --- but its
capitalization, limited partners, fund administrator, named auditor, and any
activity after the October 2023 Form~D are not public and are not estimated
here. The auditor is withheld by Cayman law (non-public annual return), not by
a limit of this search. Deals listed under the founder's personal name on
fundraising trackers are not separated by those sources from the fund and are
treated as indicative only. The competing allocation figures are left
unreconciled by design.
Personal and family matters were not investigated; the recoverable record on
the individual ends with the educational and employment history in
Section~2. A profile of this kind reports where the record stops as
carefully as it reports what the record contains.
