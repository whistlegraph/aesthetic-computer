\section{The short version}

Scott Moore (\href{https://x.com/notscottmoore}{@notscottmoore}) is a
co-founder of \textbf{Gitcoin} and the founder of \textbf{Public Works}. He
helped build the main way open-source software and ``public goods'' get
funded in crypto, and he now runs a fund that backs \textbf{artists and
creative technologists}.\refn{1,7}

If someone wants to understand who pays for this kind of work --- and how to
get paid by him --- he is the right person to look at.

% ===================== 2 =====================
\section{The idea he is known for}

\textbf{Quadratic (``plural'') funding}: a matching system where the number
of people who chip in matters more than how much each gives. A crowd of small
donors beats one big one.\refn{5,6}

It came from a 2018 paper by Vitalik Buterin, Zoë Hitzig, and Glen Weyl.
Gitcoin was the first to run it at scale. Moore is the person who turned the
theory into working machinery.\refn{5}

% ===================== 3 =====================
\section{Gitcoin}

Founded 2017 by Kevin Owocki, with Moore and Vivek Singh as the other two
co-founders. It grew up inside ConsenSys, raised \$11.3M led by Paradigm in
2021, and spun out on its own. It now runs a token (GTC), a community
treasury, and tools any group can use to run their own funding
round.\refn{3,4}

% ===================== 4 =====================
\section{Public Works --- what it is}

His current fund. The pitch (``venture mutualism''): put in the least money
needed, build alongside the project, and let it end up owned by its
community. A stated goal is to feed value back to Gitcoin.\refn{10,11}

Public Works says it funds \textbf{artists and creative technologists}, and
has backed Fuser, Gitcoin, Metalabel, and Spline.\refn{7,8}

% ===================== 5 =====================
\section{Public Works --- the structure}

Legally it is \textbf{Public Works LP}, a Cayman Islands private fund.\refn{12}

\begin{itemize}
\item Cayman regulator confirms it: registered \textbf{Private Fund},
active, since June 2023.\refn{14}
\item US filing (Oct 2023) is a private-fund notice --- no public investor
list, no size disclosed.\refn{12}
\item Run with outside professionals: registered office via \textbf{Campbells};
two independent directors from \textbf{Hash} (Danielle Faul, Cobus
Pietersen) alongside Moore.\refn{13}
\item No US investment-adviser registration, so no public assets-under-
management figure.\refn{13}
\end{itemize}

The size, the investors, and the auditor are simply not public --- that is
how Cayman funds are built, not a gap in the research.

% ===================== 6 =====================
\section{Following the money}

The matching money behind this world comes from crypto-wealthy donors,
protocol treasuries, and DAOs --- e.g. Nouns DAO gave 169 ETH to one Gitcoin
round; Optimism set aside a large share of its tokens for public
goods.\refn{9}

One Public Works deal is publicly traceable: the \textbf{Rise Labs} seed
round, about \textbf{\$3.2M, Sept 2024}, in a group that also included
Vitalik Buterin.\refn{15} Other deals listed online under his name are his
personal angel investing, not clearly the fund.\refn{15}

Headline totals vary wildly (\$50M / \$70M / \$500M) because each counts
something different; no single number is solid.\refn{1,5,9}

% ===================== 7 =====================
\section{Art-and-tech ties}

This is the part that matters here. His funding is aimed straight at art and
technology:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{NEW INC} (the New Museum's incubator) --- current Mentor.\refn{8}
\item \textbf{Gray Area} (San Francisco arts non-profit) --- board member.\refn{7,8}
\item \textbf{Metagov} --- contributor / researcher.\refn{8}
\end{itemize}

NEW INC and Gray Area are in the same world as the institutions studied
elsewhere in this papermill --- he connects directly into it.

% ===================== 8 =====================
\section{How to reach him}

He already tends to say yes to supporting good work --- the friction is
practical. To make it easy:

\begin{itemize}
\item Message him: \href{https://www.publicworks.fm/}{publicworks.fm}
(``Reach Out'') or \textbf{@notscottmoore} on X. Reference where you met in
person --- not a cold ask.\refn{16}
\item He sends crypto by default. Have an \textbf{ENS name} (e.g.
\texttt{name.eth}) ready, and say which networks work (Ethereum, Base,
Optimism). Put it right in the message.
\item Frame the work as a creative-technology / public-good project, small
and specific. One or two links, no deck.
\item Easiest for him: a personal send, via Public Works, or by entering a
Gitcoin round (his own system --- and it gets matched).
\end{itemize}

% ===================== 9 =====================
\section{What is not public}

Stated plainly: the fund's size, its investors, its auditor, and anything it
did after its 2023 filings. Personal and family details were not looked into.
Where the record stops, this says so.
