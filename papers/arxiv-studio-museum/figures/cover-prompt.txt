A single Harlem brownstone stoop emblem floating dead-centre on cream paper — three reddish-brown stone steps with a slim wrought-iron railing, but the stoop is inverted, the steps reading downward into the page like an invitation rather than upward like a barrier. This is David Adjaye's signature "inverted stoop" architectural motif, rendered as if it were an isolated bronze maquette. The topmost stone tread is smooth and uncarved — no lettering, no inscription, no wordmark anywhere. A single warm light from the upper left throws a long shadow across one tread. The form floats with no surrounding building, no street, no figure; the bottom-most step fades softly into the cream of the page.
