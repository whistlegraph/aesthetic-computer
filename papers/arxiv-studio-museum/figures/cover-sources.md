## Visual elements → sources

| Element | Source |
|---|---|
| **Floating studio-easel with paint-spattered stool** | The **canonical Studio Museum object**: the institution is built around the [Artist-in-Residence program](https://www.studiomuseum.org/artist-in-residence-program), which has hosted hundreds of Black artists since 1968 (Tom Lloyd was the first 1969 resident). The easel-and-stool reads instantly as "where a working artist works." |
| **Adjaye Associates facade silhouette** | The **new Studio Museum building at 144 W 125th St**, designed by **Sir David Adjaye** (Adjaye Associates), opened 15 November 2025 after a 2018 closure for rebuild. Adjaye's signature dark-bronze ribbed facade and recessed entry are the building's identity. (Note: Adjaye separated from the project in July 2023; **Pascale Sablan** continued the build-out.) Source: [studiomuseum.org/new-building](https://www.studiomuseum.org), [Architectural Record coverage](https://www.architecturalrecord.com/articles/16367-studio-museum-in-harlem). |
| **Single bold figurative silhouette referencing the AIR program lineage** | A nod to the program's alumni — but deliberately abstract, not any specific artist. The AIR "F-shows" (*Freestyle* 2001, *Fictions* 2017, etc., curated by Thelma Golden) are the program's defining periodic group shows. |
| **Faded periphery** | Vignette; deliberately omits 125th Street streetscape, Harlem context, MTA signage. |

## Founder + key people referenced

The cover does not depict a person, but it references the lineage:
- **Six founders (1968)**: Charles Inniss, Carter Burden, Campbell Wylly, Eleanor Holmes Norton, Betty Blayton-Taylor, Harriet Donnelly.
- **Edward Spriggs** — first director, 1969–75.
- **Mary Schmidt Campbell** — director 1977–87.
- **Kinshasha Holman Conwill** — deputy/acting director through late 80s and 90s.
- **Lowery Stokes Sims** — director 2000–2004.
- **Thelma Golden** — Director and Chief Curator 2005–. Curator of the *Freestyle* (2001) Harlem-Studio-Museum F-show that launched the term "post-black art." Her tenure is the longest in the institution's history. [Wikipedia: Thelma Golden](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thelma_Golden), [Mellon $10M endowment funding her position (2024)](https://www.mellon.org/grants/grants-database/grants/the-studio-museum-in-harlem/).

## What was deliberately excluded

- A Thelma Golden portrait (the museum is bigger than its director).
- Specific AIR alumni's signature works — would single out individuals from a 50+ year cohort lineage.
- The Adjaye-vs-Sablan handover controversy (2023 separation).
- The capital-campaign donor wall (Eychaner / Mellon / Ford named gifts).
- 125th Street streetscape.
