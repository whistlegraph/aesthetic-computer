# Aesthetic Computer — Demoplay Script
# ~3 minute video for Prix Ars Electronica 2026

## Concept

No voiceover narration. No slides. The video IS the instrument being played.
Text cards appear between scenes — short, poetic, typeset in AC's font.
Sound comes from the pieces themselves. The video feels like watching someone play music.

---

## Scene 1 — The Prompt (0:00–0:20)

**CARD:** "No menus. No files. Just a prompt."

A phone screen. The AC prompt blinks. A finger types: `notepat`
The screen transforms into a grid of colored pads. The user taps out a melody.
Sound fills the frame. Cut to:

The prompt again. Types: `wand`
A drawing tool appears. A stroke of color trails the finger.

The prompt again. Types: `prompt`
We're back. The instrument resets. The cycle is the interface.

---

## Scene 2 — Pieces (0:20–0:55)

**CARD:** "354 pieces. Each one a world."

Rapid montage — 2-3 seconds each, cut on the beat of a notepat rhythm:
- `line` — geometric drawing
- `plot` — pixel placement
- `chat` — live messages appearing
- `spray` — spray paint tool
- `mood` — posting a mood to the social feed
- `whistlegraph` — a collaborative drawing game
- Custom user pieces from @handles

Each piece fills the whole screen. No chrome, no UI decoration. Pure interaction.

---

## Scene 3 — KidLisp (0:55–1:30)

**CARD:** "A language a child can read."

Split screen: left side shows KidLisp code being typed, right side shows the visual output updating live.

```
(each i 12
  (circle:filled (/ screen:w 2)
                 (/ screen:h 2)
                 (* i 20)
                 (color i 100 200)))
```

Concentric colored circles bloom on screen.

Then: a child (or beginner) typing in the prompt, asking an AI to help write a KidLisp piece. The AI responds with code. The code runs. Art appears. The human tweaks a number. The art changes. Back and forth — human and machine improvising together.

**CARD:** "16,000 programs written. Many by beginners."

---

## Scene 4 — Social (1:30–2:00)

**CARD:** "Social infrastructure inside the runtime."

Two phones side by side. Both on the same piece. One person draws, the other sees it appear in real time. They're making something together.

Cut to: the mood feed. Short text posts, timestamps, handles. A social network that lives inside a creative instrument.

Cut to: a profile page. Someone's published pieces listed. Tap one — it loads instantly. You're inside their creation.

---

## Scene 5 — The Pack (2:00–2:20)

**CARD:** "One file. No server. Works forever."

Someone types `pack` in the prompt. A single HTML file downloads. They open it offline — the piece runs perfectly. They email it. They put it on a USB stick. They mint it on-chain.

The piece survives outside the platform. It's a self-contained artwork.

---

## Scene 6 — The Instrument (2:20–2:50)

**CARD:** "Software as instrument."

Back to the prompt. Now the user moves fast — they've memorized commands. They type rapidly:
`notepat` → play a quick rhythm →
`prompt` → `wand` → draw a fast sketch →
`prompt` → `moods` → post a thought →
`prompt` → navigate to `@someone/piece` → explore someone else's work

The speed is the point. No loading screens, no navigation trees. Just fluency. The person and the machine are in flow.

---

## Scene 7 — End (2:50–3:00)

**CARD:** "Aesthetic Computer"
**CARD:** "aesthetic.computer"
**CARD:** "Open source. Browser-native. Mobile-first."

Fade to the prompt, blinking.

## Production Notes

- **Screen capture**: Use actual AC on a real phone + desktop browser
- **Audio**: All sound from the pieces themselves (notepat, speaker pieces, etc.)
- **Typography**: Text cards in YWFT Processing (AC's custom font)
- **Editing rhythm**: Cut on musical beats from the notepat sequence in Scene 1
- **No voiceover**: Let the instrument speak for itself
- **Resolution**: 1920x1080 or 4K, landscape primary with phone footage portrait-in-landscape
- **Color**: AC's native palette — the pink/purple brand colors appear naturally
