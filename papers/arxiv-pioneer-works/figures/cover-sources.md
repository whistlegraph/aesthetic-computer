# Cover-prompt sources — Pioneer Works

The cover is a **single floating arched cathedral-window** of the 19th-century iron-works building, with one of Yellin's signature glass-collage forms suspended inside it. Each element is sourced.

## Visual elements → sources

| Element | Source |
|---|---|
| **The tall 19th-century red-brick arched window** | The actual Pioneer Works building at **159 Pioneer Street, Red Hook, Brooklyn**. The 1866 Pioneer Iron Works foundry; ~25,000 sq ft, two stories, with massive arched cast-iron-framed windows that are the building's most identifiable architectural feature. Yellin bought it in 2011 for $3.7M. Photos: [pioneerworks.org/about](https://pioneerworks.org/about), [Brownstoner profile](https://www.brownstoner.com/architecture/pioneer-works-red-hook-brooklyn/). |
| **Suspended glass-collage shards in vertical column** | **Dustin Yellin's signature sculpture practice** — laminated glass slabs with miniature collage figures embedded between layers. See [Wikipedia: Dustin Yellin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dustin_Yellin) and the "Triptych" series at the Brooklyn Museum (2013). The visual reference is essential because Yellin is both founder and recurring funder (proceeds from his art sales feed PW operations). |
| **Cast-iron column outlines flanking the arch** | The building's original 1866 cast-iron structural columns remain on the interior. Documented in the building's adaptive-reuse architectural drawings (Sage Architecture, 2012 renovation). |
| **Faded periphery dissolving to cream paper** | Vignette aesthetic; deliberately omits the surrounding Red Hook industrial neighborhood (cobblestone streets, harbor, gantries) to keep the cover iconic-not-narrative. |

## Founder + key people referenced

- **Dustin Yellin** (b. 1975) — founder/president 2012–. The cover's "single iconic form" cipher *is* a Yellin sculpture in implied form, even though no figurative detail is visible. The choice to feature his signature work (rather than a portrait of him) reflects Pioneer Works' fact-of-record: Yellin-as-artist subsidizes Pioneer-Works-as-org.
- **Janna Levin** — Director of Sciences (2014–). Not visually referenced; the cover prioritizes architecture + sculpture over program lineup.
- **Gabriel Florenz** — founding artistic director (2012–), ED from March 2025. Yellin's second cousin. Not visually referenced.

## What was deliberately excluded

- The cathedral-like multi-disciplinary interior (artists at studios + scientists at chalkboards + musicians) — too narrative; original prompt described this and was rewritten as a single-form vignette.
- The "PIONEER ST" hand-painted street sign (from earlier prompt iterations) — would have introduced text, breaking the no-text rule.
- A figurative Yellin portrait — the cover shows his work, not him.
