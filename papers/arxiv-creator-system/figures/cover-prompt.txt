A single vertical geological core sample standing upright like a slender column, the kind a scientist drills from deep earth and lifts out in one intact cylinder. It is cleanly stratified into four distinct horizontal bands stacked from bottom to top, each a different muted earthy tone — a dense raw-sediment base, a finer processed layer above it, a bright durable seam near the top, and a polished crowning band — reading like sediment compressed into knowledge. Faint hairline grain and embedded specks run through the rock, hand-shaded in colored pencil with chalky gouache highlights along the cut face. One clean cylindrical object, centered and monumental, edges crumbling softly into bare cream paper.
