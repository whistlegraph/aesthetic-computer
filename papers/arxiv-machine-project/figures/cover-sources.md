## Visual elements → sources

| Element | Source |
|---|---|
| **Floating storefront window with hand-lettering** | The actual **Machine Project storefront at 1200 N Alvarado St, Echo Park, Los Angeles** (2003–January 2018). The defining physical presence of the org for 15 years. Photos: [machineproject.com](https://machineproject.com), LA Times closure coverage (Jan 2018). The window-as-form reads instantly as "DIY storefront art space." |
| **Implied gear/cog or screwdriver/soldering iron** | Machine Project's **DIY-workshop ethos**. Mark Allen ran weekend workshops on taxidermy, hot-air-balloon-making, fermentation, drone-building, etc. The hand-tool iconography is the org's visual signature. |
| **Hand-lettered storefront-sign typography** | Machine Project's design language was **deliberately scrappy**: hand-painted signs, photocopied flyers, zine-aesthetic announcements. The "no logo, no text" cover-rule means we suggest but don't reproduce the lettering. |
| **Faded edges into cream paper** | Vignette; deliberately omits the surrounding Echo Park streetscape (Sunset Blvd, the lake, the 1200-block neighbors). |

## Founder + key people referenced

- **Mark Allen** (b. 1972) — founder/director, 2003–. Artist, performer, "machinist." [machineproject.com/people](https://machineproject.com).
- The org's organizational structure was always small — Allen + a rotating cast of collaborators. **No specific collaborator is depicted** because Machine Project's culture was anti-personality-cult.

## Notable signature events (referenced obliquely)

- **LACMA "Field Guide"** (15 November 2008) — 60+ projects, 35 artists, co-funded by LACMA Public Programs + Ralph M. Parsons Endowment. The single-day takeover of LACMA that announced Machine Project to the broader museum world.
- **Hammer A.I.R.** (June 2009 – December 2010) — multi-year residency at the Hammer Museum, ~75–80 programs, underwritten by James Irvine Foundation's $1M grant *to the Hammer*.
- **Tang Platinum Collection** (Sep 2015 – Jan 2016 + 2016 hardcover catalog).

These are not *visually* in the cover (a cover is not a CV), but the storefront-window form reads as the substrate where all of this was incubated.

## Building tenancy chain (cross-reference Heavy Manners Library dossier)

Machine Project closed its 1200 N Alvarado storefront on **January 13, 2018** at a single farewell event (2–9pm). The same physical space (Unit D) became home to **Heavy Manners Library** in 2021. The two dossiers reference each other in `data/locations.csv`.

## What was deliberately excluded

- A Mark Allen portrait (the org's posture is anti-personality).
- LACMA's exterior or any partner-museum building.
- Specific workshop subject-matter (taxidermy, balloons, etc.) — too narrative.
- The post-2018 "non-place-based" itinerant phase (the storefront *is* the visual identity in retrospect).
