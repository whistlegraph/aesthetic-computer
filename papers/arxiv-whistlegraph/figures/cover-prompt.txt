A single butterfly drawn as one continuous hand-made marker line on paper, its two wings and slender body formed from a single unbroken contour that loops and rises like a sung melody. The line behaves as both picture and musical phrase: where it climbs the stroke thickens into a note-like swell, where it dips it thins, so the silhouette doubles as a graphic score for a sung lyric. Rendered in warm marker orange and a touch of blue-pencil shading, with a faint single horizontal staff-line passing softly behind it, as if the drawing were resting on one line of a song. The butterfly fills the central two-thirds, confident and childlike and instantly memorizable, edges dissolving into bare cream paper.
