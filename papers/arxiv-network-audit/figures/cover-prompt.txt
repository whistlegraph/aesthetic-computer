A single oval brass identity nameplate, the kind pinned to a uniform or screwed onto a door, worn and softly polished at its rounded edges. Engraved into its center is one lowercase "@" symbol on its own, deep-cut and filled with dark enamel, with the entire rest of the plate left blank — no name, no number, just the at-sign and empty engraved space where a name would go. Two tiny screw-holes sit at the left and right edges. The brass catches a muted warm light, its surface faintly scratched from handling, the metal dissolving to bare cream paper at the rim.
