Cover for "Gaps in Granularity" — a conceptual diagram, not a portrait,
because the paper is about a control surface.

The thesis, drawn on pure white paper: one smooth, expressive, continuous
gesture — the scratch, the hand's actual intent — sweeps across the frame in
the AC gradient (pink → purple → green). Behind it, a flat gray staircase
quantizes that gesture to the nearest item on a speed menu: 0.5×, 1×, 1.5×, 2×.
Thin pink dashed connectors measure the error between the two at each sample —
that error IS the gap in granularity. A white-cored ring rides the smooth curve
like a needle on a groove. Faint concentric record grooves sit behind, upper
left, as the origin of the gesture. Lower right, the menu itself, echoed as a
plain dropdown glyph with 1.5× selected — the impoverished consumer control the
paper indicts.

Wordless (the .tex sets the title). AC palette: pink #b44887, purple #7850b4,
green #46c85a, gray #8a8390, dark #40384a, paper #fdfdfb.
