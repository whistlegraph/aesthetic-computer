A single embossed leather book spine standing upright dead-centre on cream paper, viewed straight-on. Oxblood-red leather with three gilt horizontal bands top and bottom. Stamped large in gold leaf at the centre of the spine, a dignified elephant head in profile, the trunk curling upward — the institution's emblem, occupying the spine's prime engraving zone where a title would normally sit. No wordmark, no readable letters, no title text. The book is one volume, no shelf, no other books, no hands; bottom edges dissolve softly into pure cream.
