## Visual elements → sources

| Element | Source |
|---|---|
| **Floating stack of books with translucent data-strands rising from them** | The literal **HathiTrust mission**: digitized books as a preservation-and-research substrate. As of 2024, **18M+ digitized volumes** across 219+ member institutions; 19M+ across 290+ in 2025. The data-strand metaphor visualizes "books-as-data" — full-text searchable scholarly libraries. Source: [hathitrust.org/about](https://www.hathitrust.org/about). |
| **Light-rail-like double-helix ribbons connecting the books** | A nod to **scholarly preservation as continuous infrastructure** — the Authors Guild v. HathiTrust (2011–2014) ruling established fair-use for full-text searching of digitized scholarly libraries. The DNA-helix-like ribbons read as data-flow without being literal. |
| **Sealed envelope or vault detail at the base** | The "trust" half of the org's name. HathiTrust holds books on behalf of member libraries; the trust relationship is the legal-and-organizational foundation. The sealed-envelope detail evokes both archive-preservation and the consortium-trust mechanism. |
| **Faded periphery** | Vignette; deliberately omits the University of Michigan Library building (Ann Arbor) — HathiTrust is a *service* of UMich, not a freestanding institution with a recognizable HQ. |

## Etymology + naming reference

The word "Hathi" is **Hindi for "elephant"** — chosen for elephants' proverbial memory ("an elephant never forgets"). The cover's books-stack form is the visual stand-in; an elephant figure was deliberately not used (would be too literal/cute, undermining the gravity).

## Founder + key people referenced

- **John Wilkin** — founding Executive Director, 2008–2013. Now CEO of Lyrasis. UMich Library veteran.
- **Mike Furlough** — Executive Director, 2014–.
- **Paul Courant** — UMich co-creator, founding economist of the consortium.
- **HTRC co-PIs**: Beth Plale (Indiana), J. Stephen Downie (Illinois) — the HathiTrust Research Center.
- **Founding 12 universities (Oct 13, 2008)**: the CIC (now Big Ten Academic Alliance) + 11 University of California libraries + UVa.

## What was deliberately excluded

- An elephant icon (too cute).
- A literal University of Michigan logo or building.
- Specific member-institution shields (290+ members would be impossible to represent fairly).
- The Authors Guild courtroom imagery (the case is the dossier's content, not the cover's).
