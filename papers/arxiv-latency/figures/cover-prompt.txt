A single chunky keyboard keycap, lifted and tilted so its hollow underside faces the viewer, floating alone. Instead of the usual cross-shaped switch stem, the cavity beneath the cap holds a tiny round speaker cone — a concentric paper-and-copper driver nestled where the mechanism should be, with one thin solder wire trailing from it like a tail. The keycap's top face is a plain warm beige with a faint worn legend, the way a much-pressed key looks. A few delicate concentric sound-ripple arcs emanate from the speaker's center, the only motion in the image. The object is solid, weighty, and singular, rendered as if a keypress and the sound it makes were the same physical thing.
