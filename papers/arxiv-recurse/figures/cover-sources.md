# Cover-prompt sources — Recurse Center

The cover is a **single floating laptop with a recursive nested-square pattern** emerging from the screen, with sticky notes drifting around the edges as quiet satellites. Each element is sourced.

## Visual elements → sources

| Element | Source |
|---|---|
| **Floating laptop, lid open at ~120°** | The org's primary working object. Recurse Center batches are programmer-residencies; everyone arrives with a laptop and works on personal/learning projects. The 120° angle reflects an "actively in use" pose, not idle. |
| **Recursive nested-square pattern emerging from the screen** | **Direct visual pun on the org's name** — "Recurse Center" (renamed March 25, 2015 from "Hacker School"). Recursion is the canonical CS-pedagogy concept; nested squares (Mandelbrot-style) are its most-recognized visual. The form reads instantly to programmers and is opaque-but-suggestive to non-programmers. |
| **Sticky notes drifting around the laptop as quiet satellites** | The famous **"social rules" pinned to the wall** — Recurse Center's culture document includes four rules ("no well-actually's," "no feigned surprise," "no backseat driving," "no subtle -isms"). These are usually printed and posted. The drifting sticky-notes nod to the rules without making them legible. Source: [recurse.com/manual](https://www.recurse.com/manual). |
| **Soft fade at edges** | Vignette; deliberately omits the physical Recurse Center space (a small Manhattan office; the org doesn't lead with its real-estate). |

## Founder + key people referenced

The cover's "single programmer working" implication points to:
- **Sonali Sridhar** — co-founder, 2011. Co-founder/CEO. [LinkedIn / Recurse blog](https://www.recurse.com/blog).
- **Dave Albert** — co-founder, 2011. Co-founder Emeritus since March 2023.
- **Nick Bergson-Shilcock** — co-founder, 2011.

The original "Hacker School" name was retired in 2015 because the term "hacker" was being misread; the rename announcement is canonical Recurse-Center text and the cover deliberately uses the recursion-spiral (post-rename identity) rather than any "hacker" iconography. Source: [Recurse Center "Why Hacker School Is Now Recurse Center"](https://www.recurse.com/blog/77-hacker-school-is-now-the-recurse-center).

## What was deliberately excluded

- Specific founder portraits (the org leads with collective culture, not personality).
- The "hacker school" original name's visual residue.
- Any specific code or programming language (would imply favoritism).
- The hiring-partner companies (Stripe, Etsy, etc. — these are how Recurse is funded but they're not the cover's subject).
- Specific batch types (12-week vs mini-batch vs RC Together).
