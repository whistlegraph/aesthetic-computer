A single low-cost school Chromebook laptop, the plasticky grey clamshell shut tight, drawn three-quarter from the front. A heavy steel padlock hangs from a short chain looped through the seam between the closed lid and keyboard base, clamping the machine shut so it can never be opened. The lid wears a small dull corporate logo embossed into the plastic, and a worn institutional asset sticker on one corner. The whole device sits closed and locked like a sealed box, no glowing screen, just a denied, padlocked grey rectangle.
