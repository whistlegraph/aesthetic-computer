Two interesting facts about the pre-2011 filings:
- **FY2009 + FY2010 are 990-EZ**, not full 990, indicating revenue was low enough (under $200K) that the simplified form was permitted in those years. The org dropped from full 990 to EZ during the financial crisis.
- **FY2002 is the first filing**, three years after the 1999 IRS ruling. There is no public 1999, 2000, or 2001 filing on ProPublica.

## Notable shape observations (raw, for the dossier)

- Net assets went negative FY2011–FY2012 (-$41K, -$36K) — financial low point
- Major revenue spike FY2016 ($1.46M revenue against $496K expenses → $976K surplus) — coincides with Net Art Anthology launch
- FY2018 spike ($1.7M, also surplus) — Anthology mid-run
- FY2022 spike ($2.16M, $1.01M surplus) — coincides with author's First Look commission year and Webrecorder spin-out
- FY2023 + FY2024: expenses outrun revenue by ~$450K and ~$390K respectively; net assets fell from $1.1M (FY2022) to $262K (FY2024)
