# Research Plan · arxiv-rhizome

**Working title**: *Rhizome: A Dossier* (placeholder)

**Status**: Plan only — no draft yet. Folder created 2026-05-02.
**Author**: @jeffrey
**Mode**: Survey / fact-surfacing. Not argued from a question. The paper exists so @jeffrey (and any reader) can hold the institution in mind as a single object — its genealogy, its history, its programs, its people, its money, its footprint. No thesis, no verdict.

---

## What this is

A dossier on Rhizome.org — the New York–based digital art organization founded by Mark Tribe in 1996. The paper assembles, in one place:

- the genealogy of the word "rhizome" (Deleuze & Guattari → networked-art theory → adoption by Tribe)
- a periodized cultural history of the organization (1996 → 2026)
- the program inventory (ArtBase, Net Art Anthology, Webrecorder, Commissions, Editorial, Benefit)
- the people (founders, EDs, key curators, board)
- the financial record (Form 990s, named grants, auction revenue) presented as a multi-year table
- the cultural footprint (commissioned and accessioned artists, canonical exhibitions, recurring critics)
- a reading list keyed to where each source illuminates which part of the institution

Reading it should feel like opening a folder, not following an argument.

## 2 · The word

Trace the figure of the rhizome before the org:

- Deleuze & Guattari, *A Thousand Plateaus* (1980 / Eng. 1987), introduction "Rhizome": no root, multiple entry points, plateaus, lines of flight.
- Sadie Plant, *Zeros and Ones* (1997 — already in the AC readings library) — cyberfeminist read of computing through the rhizome figure, contemporary with Rhizome.org's founding.
- Alexander Galloway, *Protocol* (2004 — on the SoSoft acquisition list) — distinguishes Deleuzian rhizome from actual TCP/IP topology.
- McKenzie Wark, *A Hacker Manifesto* (2004 — already acquired) — rhizomatic vocabulary in class analysis of the digital.

Why this matters here: the org's name is a 1996 quotation of a 1980 figure. The dossier just notes the lineage; no argument that the org does or doesn't live up to it.

## 5 · Programs

One subsection each, ~half a page:

- **ArtBase** — the curated archive of net art. Born 1999, relaunched 2019.
- **Net Art Anthology** — 100 works, two-year run (2016–2019).
- **Webrecorder / Conifer** — open-source web archiving, eventually spun out.
- **Commissions / First Look** — co-presented with the New Museum.
- **Editorial / Rhizome blog** — continuous since the early 2000s.
- **Benefit auction** — annual fundraising via artist donations.
- **Seven on Seven** — annual conference pairing artists and technologists (2010–2019).
- **Discussion lists** — Rhizome Raw / Rhizome Digest, a foundational program in the early years.

Each subsection answers: what is it, when did it run, who runs it, where does the money come from, where does the output live.

## 6 · Money

Pure record. No verdict.

### Tables

- **15-year revenue / expense table** (FY2010–FY2024) from Form 990s, columns: total revenue, contributed, earned, program expense, management & general, fundraising, net assets, full-time-equivalent staff, ED compensation.
- **Named-grants inventory** — all NEA, NYSCA, Mellon, Knight, Sloan, NYC DCLA, Andy Warhol Foundation, etc. grants by year and amount where public.

Numbers are surfaced, not interpreted. The dossier shows what the budget was; reader can draw what they want from it.

## What this dossier is not

- Not a thesis. There is no question being answered.
- Not a critique. The funding record is surfaced, not judged.
- Not a comparison. Other orgs (Eyebeam, V2_, Furtherfield, Ars Electronica, NEW INC) are mentioned only where they directly intersect Rhizome.
- Not a memoir. The author's commission is recorded as a fact in the footprint table, nothing more.
- Not exhaustive. The dossier covers what is publicly recoverable; gaps are flagged inline.
