# Cover-prompt sources — The Kitchen

The cover is a **single floating Sony Portapak video camera** in three-quarter view, with a coiled umbilical cable braiding into a microphone-and-piano-cord gesture. Each element is sourced.

## Visual elements → sources

| Element | Source |
|---|---|
| **Sony Portapak video camera (half-inch reel-to-reel deck + boxy hand-held camera, umbilical cable)** | The **founding tool of The Kitchen**. **Steina Vasulka and Woody Vasulka** founded The Kitchen in **1971** in the literal disused kitchen of the **Mercer Arts Center**, 240 Mercer Street, NYC. Their primary instrument was the [Sony Portapak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portapak) (CV-2400 or similar; the half-inch consumer-grade portable video deck released 1967). Steina was a violinist; Woody was a filmmaker; together they pioneered video art with this exact tool. Source: [The Kitchen's About page](https://thekitchen.org/about/), [Wikipedia: Vasulkas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steina_and_Woody_Vasulka). |
| **Reel of magnetic tape spinning in the recorder housing** | The Portapak's defining mechanism. Captures the analog-video moment that The Kitchen institutionalized. |
| **Umbilical cable braiding into microphone + piano cord** | The Kitchen's **multi-disciplinary lineage**: video → music → dance → theater. The cable's braid evokes signal chain (audio + video) and the literal "kitchen drawer's worth of utensils" reading on the org's name. Music programming under directors Rhys Chatham, Garrett List, George Lewis, Paul DeMarinis, Arto Lindsay made The Kitchen a defining downtown New Music venue. |
| **Hint of off-screen smoke or stage-light curtain** | Period nod to The Kitchen's no-wave / experimental-music programming of the late 1970s and early 1980s — Glenn Branca, Sonic Youth's early performances, etc. The barely-there gesture keeps the cover from becoming overtly musical. |
| **Faded cream periphery** | Vignette; deliberately omits the **484 W 19th St** Westbeth-area building (in renovation 2022–2026 by **Rice+Lipka Architects**). |

## Founder + key people referenced

- **Steina Vasulka** (b. 1940, Iceland) — co-founder, 1971. Violinist-turned-video-artist. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steina_Vasulka).
- **Woody Vasulka** (1937–2019, Czech-American) — co-founder, 1971. Filmmaker-turned-electronic-image-pioneer. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Woody_Vasulka).
- Founding board (1973 incorporation as **Haleakala, Inc.**): **Robert Stearns** (first ED), **Paula Cooper** (gallery), **Caroline Stone**, **Philip Glass**.
- Recent leadership: **Tim Griffin** (ED 2011–2024), current Russell.

## What was deliberately excluded

- The Vasulka portraits (canonical [1971 Portapak photo](https://www.vasulka.org/) shows them with the camera) — too biographical for the cover.
- 484 W 19th St building exterior (currently in renovation, doesn't read as the canonical Kitchen building).
- No-wave-era performer likenesses (would single out individuals).
- The Mercer Arts Center building (collapsed August 1973, no longer extant).
