A single civic carbon-arc searchlight — one heavy round-drummed theatrical beacon on a low splayed tripod, angled upward, its one broad beam rising and fanning into a soft cone that dissolves to nothing. Weathered cast housing with a riveted seam, a thick power cable looping off the base, the lens face glowing hot white-gold at the center with high specular contrast. No buildings, no skyline, no crowd, no human figures. Just the one lamp and its single beam. The searchlight IS the entire image — public art thrown into shared civic air.
