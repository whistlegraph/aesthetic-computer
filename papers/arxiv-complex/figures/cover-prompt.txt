A single hand-addressed brown-paper parcel, tied with rough twine in a cross, seen straight-on and slightly worn at the corners. Its face is crowded with cancelled postage stamps and smudged ink postmarks, the handwritten address half-legible and deliberately unreadable. Through one torn flap a strange folded score peeks out — a glimpse of bright hand-drawn notation the postal machinery clearly never inspected. It is the lone object: a mail-art payload routed through an engine that carried it but could not read it, edges dissolving into bare cream paper.
