A single weathered wooden artist's easel, standing alone and facing the viewer head-on, its three splayed legs and central ledge forming a stark upright silhouette. The easel holds one blank rectangular panel — bare and waiting, the empty stage of a crit where work is put up to be talked back to. The wood is honey-toned and paint-flecked from years of studio use, a few drips of cadmium red and ochre on the ledge the only color. It reads as both an art-school relic and a humming machine — the upright frame of an operating system that produces artists, quiet and load-bearing.
