americomputadora — album cover. the flag of an imaginary nation, drawn
like REAL vexillology: bold, flat, simple, instantly readable across a
room. square, the flag fills the whole frame edge to edge.

design — three flat fields and ONE emblem:

the field splits into three clean horizontal bands of flat color: dusty
rose-red on top, faded cream in the middle, warm purple at the bottom.
the red band carries three thin gold circuit-trace lines with right-angle
bends and tiny via dots — subtle, like pinstripes. the purple band
carries one simple dotted trail-line looping in a single playful curve.

centered on everything, BIG — one interlocked emblem, the only complex
thing on the flag: a five-point star, a square pixel-chip with little pin
teeth, and a round five-petal flower, overlapped and fused into a single
bold geometric mark, sky-blue and gold and orange, three shapes sharing
one body. flat hard edges, thick confident outlines, like a screen-printed
national crest. slight print misregistration so the color layers ghost a
few millimeters apart.

texture: clean flat silkscreen color with honest wear — grain, a few
cracks in the ink, softly sun-faded, one gently frayed edge. NOT muddy,
NOT sepia, NOT burlap, NOT cluttered: bright flat polychrome fields with
crisp geometry, the wear sits ON TOP of clean color.

palette: dusty rose-red, faded cream, warm purple, sky blue, gold,
orange. bright, warm, confident. NOT a photograph, NOT a 3d render, NOT
cinematic, NOT the real american flag, NO official insignia.

square album cover. no text, no words, no letters, no numbers, no logos,
no brand marks anywhere. flat color, one big emblem, quiet wear.
