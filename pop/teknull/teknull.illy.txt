an aggressively LOWPOLY 3D render, square — a videogame character
screenshot, PS1/N64-era polygon budget rendered with modern moody
lighting. EVERYTHING is triangles: the man is maybe 800 polygons
total. his FACE is large flat triangles — an angular wedge nose,
faceted cheekbones and jaw, flat-shaded skin with NO texture, NO
pores, NO smooth gradients across the face. his hair is a chunky
angular polygon shell, not strands. hands are blocky mitts of a few
facets. clothes are stiff faceted shells with hard creases. the gear,
floor, walls, fog — all visibly triangulated, flat-shaded per facet,
hard polygon edges catching the light. NO photorealism anywhere, NO
smooth organic surfaces, NO subsurface skin, NO fabric weave — if any
part looks like a photograph, it is WRONG. crisp throughout, no
motion blur.

the lowpoly man is built FROM the man in the reference photos — the
polygon head still reads as him: about 30, medium-length brown
tousled hair as angular clumps, his soft red plastic glasses on
(chunky lowpoly frames). his clothing is the faceted lowpoly version
of the ACTUAL clothing in the reference photos — same colors, same
garments, do not invent new ones. never a tank top, never a costume.
he stands side-on at a tall rack of audio hardware, one arm reaching
out, a blocky fingertip pushing up a single slider. his eyes are
nearly-closed polygon slits, head dipped in a slow nod — locked into
the groove, feeling it, not performing.
