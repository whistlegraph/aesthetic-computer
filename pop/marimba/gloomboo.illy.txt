gloomboo — album cover for a slow sad-cafe waltz.

a colored-pencil + gouache drawing on warm cream paper, square. soft
hatching, restrained strokes, a low-contrast palette that lets the
paper tone breathe through. tapered pencil edges, visible paper grain.
diegetic light only — flat overcast light spilling in from a tall
street-side window on camera-right. NOT a photograph, NOT cinematic,
no neon, no glow, no overlay shine.

a quiet small round cafe table for two by a tall street-side window.
the two figures are seen mostly from the chest up, slightly across the
table. peers at one shared eye-line, neither centered as a hero.
neither looks at the camera; both gaze gently down into their empty
ceramic mugs. shoulders soft, hands wrapped low around the cups.
present, not theatrical, not crying — just sitting with it.

on the left — jeffrey, about 30, tousled medium-length brown hair, a
thin red glasses-cord at his neck. he wears a LIGHT-BLUE PINSTRIPE
LONG-SLEEVE BUTTON-DOWN SHIRT with a real patch chest pocket on the
left side. NOT a sweater, no sweater anywhere on him. his yellow
Sailor Pro Gear fountain pen — short cigar-shaped barrel, flat-topped
cap, polished metal clip, gold trim ring — is CLIPPED INTO that shirt
chest pocket: the clip hooked over the pocket's top hem, the barrel
tucked down inside so only the capped top stands above. drawn clean
and accurate, never a ballpoint or marker.

on the right — a peer about jeffrey's age in a heavy oatmeal knit
hoodie pulled up around their neck, hood resting back on the
shoulders. they cradle their mug with both hands. quiet face, lips
relaxed, eyes lowered into the cup. a small pewter ring on one finger.

on the round table between them: two empty ceramic mugs (one almost
finished, a thin coffee ring at the bottom), a single half-eaten
pastry on a small saucer between them, a folded paper napkin. on the
chair beside jeffrey, the lid of a chartreuse MacBook Neo shows
LID-BACK to camera — only the back of the lid visible, the keyboard
plane completely out of frame. a torn little white paper scrap on the
lid is hand-penned with a thick whistlegraph butterfly (NOT an apple
logo, NOT a pals logo, NOT any brand). nothing else in front of the
laptop in camera.

behind them, the tall street-side window fills the upper third with
soft gray rain falling against the glass — out-of-focus streaks of
overcast city beyond. wet sidewalk implied, a passing umbrella far
back, blurred to a soft shape.

palette: warm cream paper ground; muted slate-gray window light; a
washed-out sky beyond the rain; light-blue pinstripe and oatmeal
knit; warm brown hair and soft taupe; muted earthy mug-brown; one
small coral pop on the glasses-cord; chartreuse only on the Neo lid.
INTENTIONALLY low-contrast — but the cream paper underneath keeps the
whole picture warm, never cold, never grayed out into despair.

square album cover. no text, no wordmarks, no readable logos. neither
figure looks at the camera.
