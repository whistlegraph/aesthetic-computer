confident hatching and striping build tone — tapered pencil edges,
visible paper grain, optical mixing in the knit textures. diegetic
light only — clean morning sun raking in from camera-left, no overlay
shine, no glow filter. NOT a photograph, NOT cinematic.

a head-and-shoulders crop of a SINGLE figure on a green park bench,
seen from across a picnic table. their head fills the upper frame —
close, warm, album-cover close. they are NOT centered as a hero; they
sit a little off-center. they do not look at the camera; they gaze
just off to the upper-left, calmly following an orange-and-black
butterfly that has just flown past.

wide awake — alert, neutral-smiling, a touch of morning weekend ease,
eyes open, face lifted, unhurried and present. they wear hand-made
monarch wings strapped to their back — orange + black markings on
paper-mache panels, edges visibly hand-cut, two slim wooden dowels
running along the bone of each wing. the wings rise just above their
shoulders into the upper frame. they wear a pale-yellow short-sleeve
button-down with a small enamel butterfly pin on the collar. a thin
black antenna headband arcs above their hair, springs tipped with two
tiny felt balls.

NO other people, NO second figure. NO laptop, NO computer, NO phone,
NO devices, NO picnic-table props of any kind — just the cosplayer,
the bench, and the park behind them. the painted-wood edge of the
picnic table may run empty across the very bottom of the frame.

three or four real monarch butterflies (orange + black, painted-paper
edged, drawn at slight different scales for depth) flutter on a
diagonal trail that leads the figure's gaze. one is closer, one is
mid-distance, two are smaller and further. NOT a swarm — a small
ordered procession.

square album cover. no text, no wordmarks, no readable logos. the
