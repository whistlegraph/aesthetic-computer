dewabap — album cover for a slow dew-drop morning marimba track.

a colored-pencil + gouache drawing on warm cream paper, square.
confident hatching and striping build tone — tapered pencil edges,
visible paper grain, optical mixing in the wet grass and mist.
diegetic light only — clean low golden morning sun raking in from
camera-right, no overlay shine, no glow filter. NOT a photograph,
NOT cinematic.

a wide low crop of two figures kneeling side-by-side in tall morning
grass at the edge of a meadow. they fill the lower-middle of the
frame, heads at the same eye-line. peers, neither centered as a hero.
neither looks at the camera; both gaze down into the grass at their
knees, calmly studying the dew.

both are alert, soft-smiling, present. unhurried, weekend ease, the
first real moment of the day.

on the left — jeffrey, about 30, tousled medium-length brown hair, a
thin red glasses-cord at his neck. he wears a LIGHT-BLUE long-sleeve
BUTTON-DOWN SHIRT with a faint vertical pinstripe and a real patch
chest pocket on the left side — the outfit from his reference photos.
NOT a sweater, no sweater anywhere on him. his yellow Sailor Pro Gear
fountain pen — short cigar-shaped barrel, flat-topped cap, polished
metal clip, gold trim ring — is CLIPPED INTO that shirt chest pocket:
the clip hooked over the pocket's top hem, the barrel tucked down
inside so only the capped top stands above the pocket. drawn clean
and accurate, never a ballpoint or marker.

on the right — a peer about jeffrey's age, neutral-smiling. they wear
a soft yellow rain jacket, unzipped a little at the collar. they hold
a small round magnifying glass in one hand, lowered toward a blade of
grass at their knee — the lens catches a tiny ring of warm gold from
the side-sun. their hair is pulled back simply; they kneel at the same
height as jeffrey, equal eye-line, equal weight in the composition.

across the lower-right of the frame: a chartreuse MacBook Neo sits on
a folded picnic blanket beside them, LID-BACK to camera — only the
back of the lid faces us, the keyboard plane completely out of frame.
a torn little white paper scrap on the lid hand-penned with a thick
whistlegraph butterfly (NOT an apple logo, NOT a pals logo, NOT any
brand). no other devices, no other props.

every blade of grass beaded with hand-painted dewdrops — tiny pearls
of cream and pale sky-blue catching the side-light, each drop drawn
with a single confident highlight. soft mist rises from the grass in
diagonal wisps, drawn with thin parallel pencil strokes, mostly in
the mid-ground between the figures and the meadow behind.

palette: warm cream paper ground; pale dawn sky-blue overhead; deep
sap green and warm olive in the grass; light-blue cotton shirt and
soft buttery-yellow rain jacket; soft brown hair and warm grey; one
coral pop on jeffrey's glasses-cord; chartreuse on the Neo; warm gold
on the side-sun and the dewdrop highlights. hand-drawn, hatched,
print-tech-aware. clean low morning sunlight, no haze filters, no
neon, no glow.

square album cover. no text, no wordmarks, no readable logos. neither
figure looks at the camera.
