mooncalf — album cover for a moonlit-porch ballad.

a colored-pencil + gouache drawing on warm cream paper, square.
confident hatching and tapered pencil edges; striping for tonal
gradients; the cream of the paper still breathes through the dark
washes. diegetic light only — a real full moon up in the upper-right
of the frame, cool silver light raking down across the porch from
that single source. NOT a photograph, NOT cinematic, NOT noir.
tender and curious, never gloomy.

two figures sit side by side on a wooden porch swing, both turned
slightly inward, both with their faces tilted gently up toward the
moon. peers at one shared eye-line, neither centered as a hero.
neither looks at the camera; both gaze just past the upper-right of
the frame, calmly following the moon together. quiet small smiles
of wonder — wide awake, asking a question without saying it.

on the left — jeffrey, about 30, tousled medium-length brown hair, a
thin red glasses-cord at his neck. he wears a light-blue long-sleeve
BUTTON-DOWN SHIRT with a faint vertical pinstripe and a real patch
chest pocket on the left side. NOT a sweater, no sweater anywhere on
him. his yellow Sailor Pro Gear fountain pen — short cigar-shaped
barrel, flat-topped cap, polished metal clip, gold trim ring — is
CLIPPED INTO that shirt chest pocket: the clip hooked over the
pocket's top hem, the barrel tucked down inside so only the capped
top stands above the pocket.

on the right — a quieter peer, an adult about jeffrey's age, gently
smiling. they wear a soft heather-grey pullover HOODIE (hood down)
over plain dark indigo jeans. hands resting easy on their knees.

across the lower-left of the frame: a chartreuse MacBook Neo open on
the porch boards beside the swing, LID-BACK to camera — only the
back of the lid faces us, the keyboard plane tilted away. a torn
little white paper scrap on the lid is hand-penned with a thick
whistlegraph butterfly (NOT an apple logo, NOT a pals logo, NOT any
brand). the moonlight catches the chartreuse and pulls it slightly
cooler, more lime-silver, where the moon falls on it.

setting: a wooden porch — visible plank floor, two slim turned posts
holding up a low railing in the foreground, the chains of the swing
catching faint silver highlights. behind the porch a dark sweep of
yard and trees, hatched in deep indigo and prussian. above the
treeline: a full clear moon in the upper-right, painted as a calm
silver disc with the faintest pencil-hatch craters; a sprinkle of
small star-pricks across the deep blue night sky around it. no neon,
no flares, no glow filters.

palette: warm cream paper ground; deep indigo + prussian for the
night sky and yard; silvery moonlight across the porch boards and
faces; light-blue cotton on jeffrey's shirt; heather grey on the
peer's hoodie; soft brown hair; buttery yellow on jeffrey's pen; one
coral pop on the glasses-cord; cool-shifted chartreuse on the Neo.
hand-drawn, hatched, print-tech-aware. quiet silver night, no haze.

square album cover. no text, no wordmarks, no readable logos.
neither figure looks at the camera.
