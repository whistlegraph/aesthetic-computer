buzaboo — album cover for a bee-in-flowers pop track.

a colored-pencil + gouache drawing on warm cream paper, square.
confident hatching and striping build tone — tapered pencil edges,
visible paper grain, optical mixing in the petals and the linen of
the beekeeper's shirt. diegetic light only — clean midday sun from
above-camera, no overlay shine, no glow filter. NOT a photograph,
NOT cinematic, NOT neon.

a three-quarter crop of two figures standing side by side at a sunny
cottage apiary. peers at one shared eye-line — neither centered as a
hero, neither looks at the camera. both gaze gently down at the
wildflowers in front of them, calmly watching the bees work.

both are wide awake — alert, neutral content smiles, a touch of
summer-afternoon ease. unhurried, present, daytime.

on the left — jeffrey, about 30, tousled medium-length brown hair, a
thin red glasses-cord at his neck. he wears a light-blue long-sleeve
BUTTON-DOWN SHIRT with a faint vertical pinstripe and a real patch
chest pocket on the left side — the outfit from his reference photos.
NOT a sweater, no sweater anywhere on him. his yellow Sailor Pro Gear
fountain pen — short cigar-shaped barrel, flat-topped cap, polished
metal clip, gold trim ring — is CLIPPED INTO that shirt chest pocket:
the clip hooked over the pocket's top hem, the barrel tucked down
inside so only the capped top stands above the pocket.

on the right — a beekeeper peer, an adult about jeffrey's age. they
wear a white linen long-sleeve beekeeper's shirt, the mesh hood
PUSHED BACK behind their head so the face is fully visible. neutral
content smile. soft leather gloves still on at the wrist, cuffs
buttoned. no centering, no veil over the face.

behind them, in a row across the middle of the frame: three or four
white wooden hive boxes (Langstroth stacks, two boxes high each) on
short cinder-block legs in the grass. neat, painted, country-cottage.

across the lower third of the frame: a wildflower patch — yellow
coneflower, white daisy, lavender thistle, papery clover — drawn in
hatched colored-pencil. three or four real honeybees on the flowers,
drawn small and accurate, paper-edged wings, golden + dark stripes.

at the right edge of the frame: a chartreuse MacBook Neo open on a
weathered wooden bench by the hives, LID-BACK to camera — only the
back of the lid faces us, the keyboard plane angled away. a torn
little white paper scrap on the lid hand-penned with a thick
whistlegraph butterfly (NOT an apple logo, NOT a pals logo, NOT any
brand). no other devices, no other props on the bench.
