marimbaba — album cover.

a colored-pencil + gouache drawing on warm cream paper, square.
confident hatching and striping build tone — tapered pencil edges,
visible paper grain, optical mixing in the knit textures. diegetic
light only — a warm desk-lamp glow, no overlay shine. NOT a
photograph, NOT cinematic.

a tight head-and-shoulders crop of two men side by side, seen from
across the desk. their heads fill the upper frame — close, warm,
album-cover close, almost touching the top edge. peers at one shared
eye-line, neither centered as a hero. neither looks at the camera.

both are quiet and somber — a calm, chill, late-night mood. no grins,
no laughter: softened, subdued faces, eyes half-lowered and heavy,
lips closed, a touch wistful. jeffrey is mellow and drowsy, gaze low
and unfocused; Bill Gates is pensive and still, faintly melancholy.
unhurried, restful, a little lonely-tender — the hush of two people
typing late into the night. eyes down toward their hands, never at
the viewer.

on the left — jeffrey, about 30, tousled medium-length brown hair, a
thin red glasses-cord at his neck. he wears a light-blue long-sleeve
BUTTON-DOWN SHIRT with a faint vertical pinstripe and a real patch
chest pocket on the left side — the outfit from his reference photos.
NOT a sweater, no sweater anywhere on him. his yellow Sailor Pro Gear
fountain pen — short cigar-shaped barrel, flat-topped cap, polished
metal clip, gold trim ring — is CLIPPED INTO that shirt chest pocket:
the clip hooked over the pocket's top hem, the barrel tucked down
inside so only the capped top stands above the pocket. the pen sits
in the pocket, never floating on bare fabric. drawn clean and
accurate, never a ballpoint or marker.

on the right — Bill Gates, recognizably him: older, grey hair, soft
rectangular glasses. he wears a muted sage-green crewneck sweater — a
clearly different colour from jeffrey's blue shirt. his deep-red
fountain pen — the same cigar-shaped barrel and flat-topped cap as
jeffrey's — hangs from the sweater's neckline because its polished
metal clip is HOOKED OVER AND GRIPPING the ribbed collar edge: the
clip pinches the knit hem, visibly engaged and doing its actual job,
the pen suspended from that grip. the clip is in use — not decorative,
not floating flat on the knit. the same pen family in a different
colour, drawn clean and accurate.

across the very bottom of the frame: both pairs of hands on an IBM
Model M keyboard — the classic buckling-spring board: a pebble-grey /
greige gently-curved case, slightly sculpted off-white keycaps, the
small oval corner badge rendered as an indistinct emblem (NOT a
readable wordmark). fingers caught mid-keystroke. only the hands and
the top edge of the Model M show; it runs off the bottom of the frame.

NO screen of any kind — no monitor, no laptop, no laptop lid, no
device behind the keyboard. nothing in the frame but the two heads,
their shoulders, and their typing hands on the Model M.

palette: warm cream paper ground; light-blue cotton shirt and sage-green knit; soft
brown hair and warm grey; buttery yellow on jeffrey's pen, deep red on
Bill's; one coral pop on the glasses-cord; pebble-grey on the Model M;
gentle lamplit warmth. hand-drawn, hatched, print-tech-aware.

square album cover. no text, no wordmarks, no readable logos. neither
figure looks at the camera.
