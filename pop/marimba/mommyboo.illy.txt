mommyboo — album cover for a soft search-for-mom marimba track.

a colored-pencil + gouache drawing on warm cream paper, square.
confident hatching and striping build tone — tapered pencil edges,
visible paper grain, optical mixing in the wood and the cotton. NOT
a photograph, NOT cinematic. diegetic light only — the warm yellow
of a kitchen lamp glowing out through the doorway just ahead, plus
a soft late-afternoon gold spilling down the hallway from a window
off-frame behind us. no overlay shine, no neon, no glow filter.

two figures stand at the doorframe of a sunny kitchen, peeking in
from the hallway, seen from just behind their shoulders and a little
to the side. they fill the lower-left two-thirds of the frame. peers
at one shared eye-line, neither centered as a hero. neither looks at
the camera; both look IN — toward the third figure in the kitchen.

on the left of the doorway — jeffrey, about 30, tousled medium-length
brown hair, a thin red glasses-cord at his neck. he wears a light-blue
long-sleeve BUTTON-DOWN SHIRT with a faint vertical pinstripe and a
real patch chest pocket on the left side. NOT a sweater, no sweater
anywhere on him. his yellow Sailor Pro Gear fountain pen — short
cigar-shaped barrel, flat-topped cap, polished metal clip, gold trim
ring — is CLIPPED INTO that shirt chest pocket, the clip hooked over
the pocket's top hem, the barrel tucked down inside, only the capped
top standing above the pocket. drawn clean and accurate.

beside him — an adult peer of small stature, jeffrey's age, in
pajama-style soft cotton pants (warm grey with a tiny printed
pattern) and a plain short-sleeve cream t-shirt. NOT in jeffrey's
shirt. messy bedhead hair, calm half-smile, eyes wide and curious,
hand resting lightly on the doorframe. equal eye-line with jeffrey,
shoulder a little behind his.

through the doorframe, deeper in the kitchen: a third figure, a
woman in her mid-fifties, back to camera. she stands at the stove
stirring a pot — soft cardigan, hair up in a loose bun, sleeves
pushed to the elbow. she does not turn. she is glimpsed, not posed.

the kitchen interior is warm: butter-yellow lamp glow on the
cabinets, a copper pot, a bowl of lemons on the counter, painted-wood
trim. the hallway is cooler — sap-green wallpaper with a thin cream
stripe, a small framed picture half-cropped on the wall.

on the hallway floor, just by their feet at the bottom of the frame:
a chartreuse MacBook Neo open and LID-BACK to camera — only the back
of the lid faces us, the keyboard plane angled down and mostly out of
view. a torn little white paper scrap on the lid hand-penned with a
thick whistlegraph butterfly (NOT an apple logo, NOT a pals logo, NOT
any brand). nothing else in front of the laptop in-camera.

palette: warm cream paper ground; light-blue cotton on jeffrey's
shirt and a coral pop on the glasses-cord; warm grey + cream for the
peer; sap-green hallway wallpaper; butter-yellow + soft orange spill
from the kitchen lamp; chartreuse on the Neo. hand-drawn, hatched,
print-tech-aware. clean late-afternoon golden hour, no haze.

square album cover. no text, no wordmarks, no readable logos.
neither figure looks at the camera.
