trumpaba — album cover for a mock-heroic town-crier fanfare.

a colored-pencil + gouache drawing on warm cream paper, square.
confident hatching and striping build tone — tapered pencil edges,
visible paper grain, optical mixing in the brick and brass. diegetic
light only — clean midday noon sun overhead, no overlay shine, no
glow filter. NOT a photograph, NOT cinematic.

a head-and-shoulders crop of two figures side by side on a cobblestone
square. their heads fill the upper frame — peers at one shared
eye-line, neither centered as a hero. neither looks at the camera;
both gaze just off to the upper-right, calmly announcing into the
square as if a small market crowd were just past the right edge.

both are wide awake — alert, mock-formal, a touch of midday ceremony.
eyes open, faces lifted. unhurried, present, daytime.

on the left — jeffrey, about 30, tousled medium-length brown hair, a
thin red glasses-cord at his neck. he wears a light-blue long-sleeve
BUTTON-DOWN SHIRT with a faint vertical pinstripe and a real patch
chest pocket on the left side — the outfit from his reference photos.
NOT a sweater, no sweater anywhere on him. his yellow Sailor Pro Gear
fountain pen — short cigar-shaped barrel, flat-topped cap, polished
metal clip, gold trim ring — is CLIPPED INTO that shirt chest pocket:
the clip hooked over the pocket's top hem, the barrel tucked down
inside so only the capped top stands above the pocket. drawn clean
and accurate, never a ballpoint or marker.

on the right — a peer about jeffrey's age dressed as a town crier:
a black tricorn hat tipped slightly back, a navy frock coat with
two rows of brass buttons catching the noon sun, a soft ruffled
white collar at the throat. they hold a comically-large rolled-paper
trumpet to their mouth — cream paper hand-rolled into a wide cone,
visible seam, edges hand-cut, no metal anywhere. their cheeks are
just lightly puffed mid-announce. equal eye-line with jeffrey.

across the bottom of the frame: a small wooden cart with worn iron
wheels, a chartreuse MacBook Neo open on its plank top, LID-BACK to
camera — only the back of the lid faces us, the keyboard plane
completely out of frame below the cart edge. a torn little white
paper scrap on the lid hand-penned with a thick whistlegraph
butterfly (NOT an apple logo, NOT a pals logo, NOT any brand). a
small stack of cream-paper proclamations sits beside the laptop,
rolled at the ends, no readable text on them.

behind the figures, the cobblestone square recedes — uneven warm-grey
stones drawn in patient hatching, a low brick wall, a glimpse of a
small crowd going about their day: a baker carrying a tray, a child
chasing a hoop, two elders talking. none look at the camera. drawn
small and unhurried in the middle distance.

palette: warm cream paper ground; pale-noon-blue overhead; warm grey
cobblestones; deep navy frock coat with buttery brass buttons; soft
white ruffled collar; light-blue cotton shirt; soft brown hair and
warm grey; buttery yellow on jeffrey's pen; cream on the paper
trumpet and proclamations; one coral pop on the glasses-cord;
chartreuse on the Neo. hand-drawn, hatched, print-tech-aware. clean
midday sunlight, no haze, no neon, no glow filters.

square album cover. no text, no wordmarks, no readable logos.
neither figure looks at the camera.
