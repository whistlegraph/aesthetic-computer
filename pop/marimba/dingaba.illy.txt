a colored-pencil + gouache drawing on warm cream paper, square.
confident hatching and striping build tone — tapered pencil edges,
visible paper grain, optical mixing in the wood-stain and cardigan
knit. diegetic light only — late-afternoon sun raking in from the
camera-right side, warm gold falling across the door's painted
panels and pooling on the welcome mat. NOT a photograph, NOT
cinematic.

two figures at equal eye-line on a friend's wooden front porch,
seen close — head-and-shoulders cropped, an album-cover crop.
neither centered as a hero, neither looks at the camera. both
gaze gently at each other, mid-greeting — eyes meeting just
inside the half-opened door.

on the left of frame — jeffrey, about 30, tousled medium-length
brown hair, a thin red glasses-cord at his neck. he wears a
light-blue long-sleeve BUTTON-DOWN SHIRT with a faint vertical
pinstripe and a real patch chest pocket on the left side — the
outfit from his reference photos. NOT a sweater, no sweater
anywhere on him. his yellow Sailor Pro Gear fountain pen —
short cigar-shaped barrel, flat-topped cap, polished metal clip,
gold trim ring — is CLIPPED INTO that shirt chest pocket: the
clip hooked over the pocket's top hem, the barrel tucked down
inside so only the capped top stands above the pocket. drawn
clean and accurate. in his free hand, held casually at chest
level, a small bouquet of bright yellow tulips wrapped in a
twist of plain brown kraft paper. seven or eight stems, leaves
visible, no florist plastic.

on the right of frame — the friend, an adult peer about
jeffrey's age, mid-30s, gently smiling in the doorway. they
wear weekend jeans and an open knit cardigan in soft heather-
grey over a plain white t-shirt — the cardigan is on the FRIEND,
not on jeffrey. one of their hands rests on the inner edge of
the door, the other is lifting in a small wave-greeting. the
door is half-opened toward camera, revealing a slice of a warm
interior hallway behind them — a brass coat-hook, a sliver of
patterned runner rug.

at the bottom of the frame, sitting on the welcome mat at
jeffrey's feet: a chartreuse MacBook Neo, closed, LID-BACK to
camera — only the back of the lid faces us, the keyboard plane
hidden against the mat. a torn little white paper scrap on the
lid hand-penned with a thick whistlegraph butterfly (NOT an
apple logo, NOT a pals logo, NOT any brand). no other devices,
no other props.

square album cover. no text, no wordmarks, no readable logos.
