kikbouba — album cover for a sound-symbolism marimba study.

a colored-pencil + gouache drawing on warm cream paper, square.
confident hatching and striping build tone — tapered pencil edges,
visible paper grain, optical mixing in the gouache flats. diegetic
light only — clean afternoon laboratory sun raking in from a high
camera-right window, no overlay shine, no glow filter. NOT a
photograph, NOT cinematic.

a head-and-shoulders crop of two figures side by side standing at a
wooden lab bench, seen straight on. their heads fill the upper frame
— close, warm, album-cover close. peers at one shared eye-line,
neither centered as a hero. neither looks at the camera; both gaze
just off to the lower-left, calmly considering the two flashcards
their peer holds up.

both are wide awake — alert, curious, neutral-smiling, a touch of
laboratory focus. eyes open, faces lifted. unhurried, present,
afternoon-coded.

on the left — jeffrey, about 30, tousled medium-length brown hair, a
thin red glasses-cord at his neck. he wears a light-blue long-sleeve
BUTTON-DOWN SHIRT with a faint vertical pinstripe and a real patch
chest pocket on the left side. NOT a sweater, no sweater anywhere on
him. his yellow Sailor Pro Gear fountain pen — short cigar-shaped
barrel, flat-topped cap, polished metal clip, gold trim ring — is
CLIPPED INTO that shirt chest pocket: the clip hooked over the
pocket's top hem, the barrel tucked down inside so only the capped
top stands above the pocket. drawn clean and accurate.

on the right — a peer researcher, an adult about jeffrey's age, with
a neutral curious smile. they wear a white short-sleeve linen shirt,
buttoned to the collar, sleeves crisp and slightly rumpled at the
edges. they hold up TWO large hand-painted flashcards, one in each
hand, raised to chest level. the LEFT card (in their right hand)
shows a jagged spiky star-burst shape in inky black, all sharp
angles, with a small blank label-block beneath it. the RIGHT card
(in their left hand) shows a soft round blob shape in a warm muted
ochre wash, all curves, with a small blank label-block beneath it.
the cards are clearly hand-painted, edges visibly hand-cut.

across the bottom of the frame: a chartreuse MacBook Neo open on
the wooden lab bench between them, LID-BACK to camera — only the
back of the lid faces us, the keyboard plane completely out of
frame below the bench edge. a torn little white paper scrap on the
lid hand-penned with a thick whistlegraph butterfly (NOT an apple
logo, NOT a pals logo, NOT any brand). the lab bench's raw wooden
edge runs off the bottom of the frame.

behind the figures, on a cream painted wall, a corkboard fills the
upper-left third: more hand-painted flashcards pinned at slight
varied angles — a jagged crystal, a smooth ovoid, a thorned outline,
a soft cloud, a comb of spikes, a kidney bean — a small ordered
gallery of the shape vocabulary the experiment is sampling.

palette: warm cream paper ground; soft cream wall behind; raw warm
oak on the bench; light-blue cotton shirt on jeffrey, crisp white
linen on the peer; soft brown hair and warm grey; buttery yellow on
jeffrey's pen; inky black on the spiky flashcards; muted ochre on
the round flashcards; one coral pop on the glasses-cord; chartreuse
on the Neo. hand-drawn, hatched, print-tech-aware. clean afternoon
laboratory light, no haze, no neon, no glow filters.

square album cover. no text, no wordmarks, no readable logos.
neither figure looks at the camera.
