slinkybap — album cover for an afternoon staircase pop track.

a colored-pencil + gouache drawing on warm cream paper, square.
confident hatching and striping build tone — tapered pencil edges,
visible paper grain, optical mixing across the wooden treads.
diegetic light only — warm afternoon sun spilling through a window
at the top of the staircase, raking down across the varnished wood.
NOT a photograph, NOT cinematic.

two figures stand side by side at the top of a long indoor wooden
staircase, seen from a half-step below them — peers at one shared
eye-line, neither centered as the hero, neither looks at the
camera. both gaze calmly down the stairs, attention on the coil
mid-fall.

on the left — jeffrey, about 30, tousled medium-length brown hair,
a thin red glasses-cord at his neck. he wears a light-blue
long-sleeve BUTTON-DOWN SHIRT with a faint vertical pinstripe and
a real patch chest pocket on the left side — the outfit from his
reference photos. NOT a sweater, no sweater anywhere on him. his
yellow Sailor Pro Gear fountain pen — short cigar-shaped barrel,
flat-topped cap, polished metal clip, gold trim ring — is CLIPPED
INTO that shirt chest pocket: the clip hooked over the pocket's
top hem, the barrel tucked down inside so only the capped top
stands above the pocket. drawn clean and accurate. his hands are
held open at hip height, fingers loose — he has just let the coil
go. he wears soft brown trousers and plain canvas sneakers.

on the right — a peer, a kid-coded adult: small stature, an
afternoon-loose pair of denim overalls over a thin striped tee, a
neutral quiet grin, short hair. equal eye-line with jeffrey, hands
in their overall pockets, leaning slightly forward to watch the
coil go.

between and below them, walking down the staircase: a real rainbow
metallic slinky, mid-step. its top coils still rest at jeffrey's
feet on the landing, the body arches in a long elastic curve, and
its bottom coil has just touched the next tread down. each
individual loop drawn as a thin glinting band of color — a soft
rainbow of pencil hatches, red through violet, repeating along the
length of the coil. afternoon light catches its top rim.

the staircase runs from foreground to background, six or seven
treads visible, varnished wood with darker risers, narrow runner
absent. a tall window at the head of the stairs throws warm light
across the upper landing.

on a small hallway side table at the top of the landing, behind
the figures: a chartreuse MacBook Neo, LID-BACK to camera — only
the back of the lid faces us, the keyboard plane completely out of
frame. a torn little white paper scrap on the lid hand-penned with
a thick whistlegraph butterfly (NOT an apple logo, NOT a pals
logo, NOT any brand). no other devices, no other props on the
table.

palette: warm cream paper ground; varnished honey-brown wood; warm
afternoon sun gold across the upper landing; light-blue cotton
shirt and faded denim overalls; soft brown hair and warm grey;
buttery yellow on jeffrey's pen; full pencil-rainbow on the slinky
coils; one coral pop on the glasses-cord; chartreuse on the Neo.
hand-drawn, hatched, print-tech-aware. clean afternoon sunlight,
no haze, no neon, no glow filters.

square album cover. no text, no wordmarks, no readable logos.
neither figure looks at the camera.
