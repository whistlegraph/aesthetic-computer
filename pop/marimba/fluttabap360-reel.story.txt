THE STORY — "the metamorphosis"
===============================

the keeper's ORIGIN MYTH, told in thirty seconds. same near-future
AESTHETIC COMPUTER nature-park as the visualizer story ("the monarch
keeper", fluttabap360.story.txt), same character (the butterfly
cosplayer — jeffrey identity-ref, about 30, soft brown curly hair,
pale-yellow short-sleeve button-down, hand-made monarch paper-mache
wings on wooden dowels, antenna headband), same hand-drawn
colored-pencil + gouache on warm cream paper, diegetic light only.

but this is the tale of where the keeper CAME FROM: he hatches from a
paper-mache chrysalis on a park oak, learns his body, learns the dance,
plays a marimba of laptop computers (the aesthetic computers — the ONLY
screens this world will ever allow, and only in that one beat), packs
them away, gives his body back to the monarchs, hardens mid-air into a
little robot drone, and sails out of the park into outer space. the
park's data-monarch lifecycle, whole, in one breath.

medium law carries over from the visualizer story verbatim — NOT a
photograph, NOT cinematic, NO glow filters, NO neon, no text, no
wordmarks; the keeper never looks at the camera — WITH TWO SANCTIONED
EXCEPTIONS, one per beat:
  · laptops beat — laptop computers exist, screens glow-LESS and lit
    like paper, each showing the NOTEPAT tile interface reproduced
    faithfully from the real screenshot (the keymaps-paper illy ref,
    marketing/campaigns/keymaps-cover/refs/ac-native-notepat.png):
    twin 4×3 candy-color tile banks + outer slider strips + waveform
  · drone + space beats — the monarch may be a hand-drawn brushed-metal
    robot; space is heavy indigo pencil with stars as bare cream paper

EIGHT WIDE STORY BEATS (the establishing masters)
===================================================================

1. cocoon (0.0–4.0) — THE CRACK
   dawn over the lawn. a HUMAN-SIZED paper-mache chrysalis — jade-green
   gouache with a hand-drawn crown of gold dots, seams visibly hand-cut
   — hangs from a thick oak branch. it CRACKS: one bright seam splitting
   down the side, a sliver of pale-yellow (his shirt!) showing through.
   two monarchs wait on the branch like midwives. faint hand-ruled
   ground-grid in the lawn below. the world holding its breath.

2. hatch (4.0–8.0) — CRAWLING OUT
   the keeper crawls out along the branch — crumpled, brand-new, curls
   matted, antenna headband bent askew, the paper-mache wings damp and
   folded flat against his back. he grips the bark, head down, blinking
   at his first morning. delighted-confused. the split chrysalis husk
   swings gently behind him.

3. grow (8.0–11.5) — THE UNFURL
   down on the lawn he UNFURLS: rising from a crouch to full height in
   one long stretch, arms spreading wide, the wings drying and creaking
   open to full span, antenna springs popping upright, felt balls
   bobbing. monarchs circle in to inspect the new one.

4. dance (11.5–15.0) — FIRST STEPS
   first steps go straight into the ballet: relevé, the low arabesque,
   the soft port de bras — the cover pose, learned in one go, like it
   was folded into him all along. lit up with the joy of it, monarchs
   falling in beside him on their arcs.

5. laptops (15.0–20.0) — THE LAPTOP MARIMBA (the gag, longest beat)
   he kneels behind FIVE laptop computers laid in a gentle arc on the
   lawn like the bars of a marimba, a slim wooden mallet in each hand,
   PLAYING the keyboards — one mallet mid-bounce off a spacebar. each
   screen shows the NOTEPAT tile interface (glow-less, hand-hatched,
   a different chord pressed on each machine), monarchs perched along
   the screen-tops watching the recital. the aesthetic computers,
   played the only way the park knows how.

6. molt (20.0–24.0) — THE BODY GOES BACK
   the laptops packed in a neat little stack on the grass beside him.
   he stands facing away, arms rising into one last port de bras — and
   the transformation takes: his outline dissolving into a swirl of
   orange-and-black, a single LARGE monarch lifting off from where he
   stood, the pale-yellow button-down settling soft and empty on the
   lawn. the wings claim him. (the FLY lift in the music lands HERE.)

7. drone (24.0–27.0) — THE HARDENING
   climbing over the park, mid-air: the monarch's painted-paper wings
   harden panel-by-panel into thin brushed-metal plates — tiny rivet
   dots, a small lens-eye glinting — a monarch-shaped robot drone,
   still orange + black, its dotted signal-arc trailing crisper now.
   the park and its ground-grid shrinking below.

8. space (27.0–30.0) — OUTER SPACE
   deep indigo pencil laid down heavy; stars picked out as bare cream
   paper. the drone-monarch drifts up-frame among the stars, Earth a
   small blue-green marble below, sunlight raking the metal wings
   orange. the park's ground-grid has become the faint curve of
   orbit-lines. a held last breath — the keeper is everywhere now.
