goosabap — album cover for an autumn migrating-geese pop track.

a colored-pencil + gouache drawing on warm cream paper, square.
confident hatching and striping build tone — tapered pencil edges,
visible paper grain, optical mixing across the russet ground and the
amber sky. diegetic light only — clean late-afternoon autumn sun
raking in low from camera-left, no overlay glow, no filter shine.
NOT a photograph, NOT cinematic, NOT neon.

a chest-up framing of two figures standing side by side in an open
autumn field, seen from a few paces back. their heads sit in the
middle band of the frame, both faces lifted toward the upper-right
corner. peers at one shared eye-line, neither centered as a hero.
neither looks at the camera; both gaze up and off, following a V of
geese flying overhead from camera-left to upper-right.

both are alert, unhurried, autumn-calm — neutral half-smile, eyes
open, breath fogging just a little on the cool air. weekend ease.

on the left — jeffrey, about 30, tousled medium-length brown hair, a
thin red glasses-cord at his neck. he wears a light-blue long-sleeve
BUTTON-DOWN SHIRT with a faint vertical pinstripe and a real patch
chest pocket on the left side — the outfit from his reference photos.
NOT a sweater, no sweater anywhere on him. his yellow Sailor Pro
Gear fountain pen — short cigar-shaped barrel, flat-topped cap,
polished metal clip, gold trim ring — is CLIPPED INTO that shirt
chest pocket: the clip hooked over the pocket's top hem, the barrel
tucked down inside so only the capped top stands above the pocket.
drawn clean and accurate, never a ballpoint or marker.

on the right — a birdwatcher peer about jeffrey's age. plaid flannel
button-down in warm rust + amber tones, over indigo jeans, sleeves
rolled to mid-forearm. a pair of small black binoculars hang from a
brown leather strap around their neck, resting just below the
collarbone, lenses pointing down. a neutral upward gaze, lips a
hair apart, eyebrows lifted in quiet interest.

across the very bottom of the frame: a folded plaid picnic blanket on
the russet leaves with a chartreuse MacBook Neo set on top, LID-BACK
to camera — only the back of the lid faces us, the keyboard plane
completely out of frame below. a torn little white paper scrap on the
lid hand-penned with a thick whistlegraph butterfly (NOT an apple
logo, NOT a pals logo, NOT any brand). no other devices on the
blanket. one or two yellow-brown leaves on the blanket's edge.

above and behind the figures, in the upper-right two thirds of the
amber autumn sky: a V of seven Canada geese flying on a clean
diagonal from camera-left toward the upper-right corner, lead goose
out front, two trailing wings of three. wings drawn at slightly
different beats so the formation feels alive. small black-grey-cream
markings — long black neck, white cheek patch, brown body — drawn
loose and confident. the V leads the figures' eye-line.
