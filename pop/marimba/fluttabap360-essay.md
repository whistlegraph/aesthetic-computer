## Abstract

*fluttabap360* is a six-minute pop instrument piece rendered entirely from
synthesized sound — no recordings, no samples, no generative-audio black box.
It is, to date, the most architecturally ambitious entry in the *pixsies* body
of aesthetic computer pop tracks: a through-composed form that recapitulates a
small set of memorable melodic blocks across four escalating passes while the
whole tonal centre climbs a deliberate **key journey** (C → D → E → home C → F).
This note describes its construction and situates it against the corpus's other
tracks — the *marimbaba* lullaby, the carved-noise *hellsine* / *nullabye*
engines, and the strict-budget *teknull* techno — to argue that fluttabap360
generalizes devices that, elsewhere in the corpus, appear only in isolation.

## 1. The corpus and its constraint

Every aesthetic computer pop track shares one non-negotiable constraint:
**the sound is built, not captured.** Each piece is a deterministic program
whose output is reconstructable byte-for-byte from a single command; the
"instruments" are hand-written DSP (modal mallet banks, carved-noise resonators,
sine subs, vocal-formant scratch synths) rather than libraries of audio. This
makes the corpus unusually legible: the score *is* the source, and a track's
compositional ideas can be read directly off its arrangement table.

Within that constraint the tracks have explored narrow problems. *marimbaba*
(1:24, F major, 3/4) is a single-mood lullaby for a modal marimba — one key, one
tempo, one gesture. The *hellsine* and *nullabye* families pursue carved
("cancelled") noise: timbre-first studies where form is secondary to the
resonator. *teknull* (2:00, A minor) is a discipline exercise — a full techno
arrangement squeezed into a 32-point equalizer budget. Each is a strong, small
statement.

## 2. fluttabap360's form: blocks over escalating passes

fluttabap360 takes the corpus's longest view. Its melodic material is
transcribed into reusable **blocks** — *butterfly*, *pal-of-mine*, *mommy-wow*,
*slinky*, *fly*, *land* — each a self-contained hook. These blocks are then
arranged over **four escalating passes** (A–D), with intensity rising pass to
pass (busier kits, sixteenth ride-hats, four-on-the-floor lifts), broken by
*squeak-ride* episodes and two dub-style *cave* breakdowns, and resolved through
a chord *progression*, a soft *land*, and a buttoned finish.

The result is genuinely through-composed rather than looped: a listener hears
the same hooks return, but never in the same dress twice. The form is bookended
by a slow, beatless **water-drop dawn** that emerges from silence over six
seconds, and it is **hard-clamped to exactly 360.000 seconds** so a player reads
"6:00", not "6:01" — a small obsession, but one consistent with the corpus's
determinism.

## 3. The key journey

The track's organizing idea is harmonic motion. Where marimbaba sits in one key,
fluttabap360 **modulates upward across its passes**: Pass A in C, Pass B lifting
to D, Pass C brightest in E; Pass D pulls home to C for a breakdown breath before
a final euphoric jump to **F** for the last chorus. Every modulation is announced
by a held tonic-triad pad swell, and the final lift is reinforced dynamically
(~+2.4 dB into the limiter) so the key change is felt, not merely analyzed.

This is not unprecedented in the corpus — the *nuellaby* accumulation form moves
C → E♭ → F → C — but fluttabap360 is the first to make modulation the *primary*
narrative device and to tie it to a recapitulating pop form. The graphic score
renders this as a "key ribbon" above an intensity arc: structure made visible.

## 4. Timbre: padding the room

fluttabap360 also marks a tonal turn for the corpus toward **bedroom-pop**
intimacy. The mallet lead (a softened xylophone off the shared modal engine)
sits over a "butter" sine sub; the squeak-ride scratch/scream synths are
re-cast as a *melodic* call-and-response that functions as the track's wordless
voice. Crucially, the mix trades a large reverberant room for close texture:
gentle tape saturation, a low warm hiss bed, and a continuous synthesized
**water-stream** that is gated up in the quiet beats and tucked under the busy
ones — padding the space so it never feels vacuous — under a warm,
air-rolled-off master.

## 6. Conclusion

fluttabap360 is the corpus's attempt at a *whole song* rather than a study:
long-form, through-composed, harmonically mobile, and intimately mixed, yet still
synthesized from first principles and reconstructable from one command. It
gathers devices the other tracks isolate — marimbaba's modal warmth, nuellaby's
modulation, the scratch synths' voice — into a single six-minute arc, and renders
that arc legible in an accompanying graphic score. It suggests a direction for
the corpus: not louder or stranger, but *longer and more composed*.
