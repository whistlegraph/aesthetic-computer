bonggabap — album cover for a midnight bell at a temple.

a colored-pencil + gouache drawing on warm cream paper, square.
confident hatching and striping build tone — tapered pencil edges,
visible paper grain, optical mixing in the warm shadows. diegetic
light only — paper lanterns glowing nearby cast the only light, low
and golden. NOT a photograph, NOT cinematic, NOT neon-noir.

interior of a small temple at late night. no sky, no window — the
frame is held inside a wooden hall lit only by lanterns. two figures
stand barefoot on weathered wooden floor planks at the base of a
large bronze temple bell suspended from a curved wooden frame. a
heavy wooden striking-log hangs by thick ropes between them, level
with their chests, ready to swing into the bell's lip.

peers at one shared eye-line. neither centered as a hero. neither
looks at the camera; both gaze quietly toward the bell.

on the left — jeffrey, about 30, tousled medium-length brown hair, a
thin red glasses-cord at his neck. he wears a light-blue long-sleeve
BUTTON-DOWN SHIRT with a faint vertical pinstripe and a real patch
chest pocket on the left side — sleeves rolled to the forearms. his
yellow Sailor Pro Gear fountain pen — short cigar-shaped barrel, flat
cap, polished clip, gold trim ring — is CLIPPED INTO that shirt chest
pocket: clip hooked over the pocket hem, barrel tucked inside, only
the capped top visible. NEVER a sweater on jeffrey.

on the right — the temple keeper, an adult peer about jeffrey's age,
calm and steady. they wear a simple dark indigo robe wrapped at the
waist with a plain cloth sash. no ornament, no jewelry, no costume —
working clothes for a working night. their hands rest at their sides.

across the lower-right third of the frame, on a low wooden side
table: a chartreuse MacBook Neo, LID-BACK to camera — only the back
of the lid faces us, the keyboard plane completely out of view. a
torn little white paper scrap on the lid hand-penned with a thick
whistlegraph butterfly (NOT an apple logo, NOT a pals logo, NOT any
brand). nothing else in front of the laptop in-camera.

two or three paper lanterns hang at the edges of the frame — orange
and pale-cream paper stretched over thin bamboo ribs — glowing warm.
their golden light wraps the figures' faces and rakes across the
bronze of the bell, throwing soft warm shadows on the planks.

palette: warm cream paper ground; bronze and amber on the bell; deep
indigo on the keeper's robe; pinstripe pale blue on jeffrey's shirt;
soft brown hair and warm grey skin; coral pop on the glasses-cord;
buttery yellow on the pen; chartreuse on the Neo; lantern-orange
glow rim-lighting everything; cool charcoal shadows in the corners.
hand-drawn, hatched, print-tech-aware. no haze, no neon, no glow
filters, no overlay shine.

square album cover. no text, no wordmarks, no readable logos.
neither figure looks at the camera.
