flutterbap — album cover: only plastic butterflies.

a square album cover. NO people, NO figure, NO face, NO hands, NO body —
only plastic butterflies. nothing else living, no insects that are real.

a joyful cluster of cheap glossy molded-plastic decorative butterflies —
the kind sold as craft-store clip-ons and garden-stake ornaments. dozens
of them, many sizes, overlapping and layered to fill the whole square
frame edge to edge: an abundant, happy pile-up. clearly injection-molded
acrylic — hard candy-bright color, crisp glossy highlights and soft
plastic sheen, slightly translucent wings catching the light, faint mold
seam-lines running along the wing edges, thin twisted-wire antennae,
little dots of dried hot-glue where some were stuck to a backing. a few
still wear their tiny spring-clip or green garden-wire stake.

palette: candy-bright and saturated — tangerine orange, sky blue, hot
pink, lemon yellow, lime green, violet — glossy PLASTIC, not painted
paper, not real wings. all set against a warm cream paper ground.

rendering: a colored-pencil + gouache drawing on warm cream paper, square
— the same hand-drawn singles-series look (confident hatching, tapered
pencil edges, visible paper grain, optical mixing) — but every butterfly
unmistakably reads as shiny PLASTIC: hard highlights, mold seams, wire
antennae, clip hardware. diegetic morning light raking from camera-left,
crisp specular glints on the plastic, no glow filter, no overlay shine.
NOT a photograph, NOT cinematic.

square album cover. no text, no wordmarks, no readable logos, no
signature. only plastic butterflies.
