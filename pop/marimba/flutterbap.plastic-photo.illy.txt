flutterbap — album cover: only plastic butterflies, as a photograph.

a square album cover. a high-end PRODUCT PHOTOGRAPH — a glossy studio
still-life. NO people, NO figure, NO face, NO hands, NO real insects —
only plastic butterflies. nothing alive.

a clustered pile of cheap glossy molded-plastic decorative butterflies —
the kind sold as craft-store clip-ons and garden-stake ornaments — heaped
and overlapping to fill the square frame. unmistakably injection-molded
acrylic: hard candy-bright color, sharp specular highlights and reflections
on the smooth plastic, slightly translucent wings glowing where the light
passes through, fine mold seam-lines along every wing edge, thin
twisted-wire antennae, tiny spring-clips and green garden-wire stakes,
small dots of dried hot-glue. you can almost hear them click together.

photographic look: shot on a macro lens, crisp shallow depth of field —
the front butterflies tack-sharp, the back of the pile melting into a
soft creamy bokeh. clean bright softbox studio light from camera-left,
gentle reflections and soft shadows on a seamless warm-cream sweep.
realistic plastic materials, real glossy highlights — photoreal, NOT a
drawing, NOT an illustration, NOT cinematic color grading.

palette: candy-bright and saturated — tangerine orange, sky blue, hot
pink, lemon yellow, lime green, violet — glossy plastic against warm
cream.

square album cover. no text, no wordmarks, no readable logos. only
plastic butterflies.
