a real photograph of a hand-crafted NEEDLE-FELTED WOOL diorama, in
tall vertical portrait orientation — shot like a quiet documentation
photo of a miniature art piece. visible wool fibers everywhere, fuzzy
edges, tiny felting imperfections that prove human hands. NOT a 3d
render, NOT clay, NOT plastic, NOT plush-toy smooth — soft matted
wool throughout. crisp focus, gentle dim lighting, soft felt shadows.

the recurring character: a single needle-felted wool BUNNY — a
wonderfully woolly little rabbit with an off-white body and soft-grey
ear tips and paws, two LONG felt ears, a little pink felt nose, tiny
dark glossy bead eyes, and a round puff of a tail. he wears tiny
round soft RED felt glasses perched on his nose — his signature. he
walks upright on his hind legs like a polite little museum visitor.
the SAME bunny in every scene — identical wool colors, same long
ears, same red glasses.

his EARS are his mood: perked tall when awake and curious, sagging
softly as he grows sleepy, fully flopped flat when he sleeps. he
always has BOTH of his two long ears — even when one flops over, the
second ear stays visible too. NEVER draw him with only one ear.

GLASSES GEOMETRY (CRITICAL): the bunny has exactly TWO eyes. a
bunny's eyes sit HIGH and WIDE on the head — so the red glasses must
ride HIGH on his face, up over the eyes, NOT down low on the muzzle
or snout. each eye — open bead eye or closed lash-line — is centered
INSIDE its round red lens, one eye per lens, the lens ringing the
eye like real worn glasses. the red rims are NEVER empty. NEVER draw
an eye ABOVE the rims with the empty lenses sitting lower on the
snout (one real eye plus two empty red circles reads as extra eyes —
the single worst failure). in a three-quarter or profile view the
same rule holds: the visible eye sits INSIDE its lens. if a beat
says the glasses are off his face, his eyes appear alone with NO red
rims anywhere near them.

scene rules: no text, no wordmarks, no wall labels, no readable
logos anywhere. no screens, no phones. no people. sleepy, hushed,
tender, a little funny.
