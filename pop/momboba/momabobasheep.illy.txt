momabobasheep — album cover. the concept, literal: jeffrey goes to
the MoMA, drinks a boba, and falls asleep counting sheep in the
galleries. (Mo-MA + boba + sheep.) the entire scene is NEEDLE-FELTED
WOOL — a handmade soft-felt diorama, photographed for real.

a real photograph of a hand-crafted needle-felt diorama, square —
shot straight on like a quiet documentation photo of a miniature art
piece. visible wool fibers everywhere, fuzzy edges, tiny felting
imperfections that prove human hands. NOT a 3d render, NOT clay, NOT
plastic — soft matted wool throughout. crisp focus, gentle museum-dim
lighting.

the scene: a serene modern-art museum gallery, nearly empty, built
entirely from felt. the floor is pale wood-toned felt; filling the
entire back wall edge to edge, a monumental water-lily pond painting
rendered as a FELTED TAPESTRY panel — soft impressionist blues,
greens and lilac wool blended like brushstrokes, a sleeping pond in
wool. no other artworks, no wall text, no signage.

on a long backless felt gallery bench in front of the painting: a
needle-felted wool doll of the man in the reference photos —
recognizably him in doll form, about 30, medium-length brown wool
hair, tousled — genuinely, deeply asleep, slumped sideways along the
bench, eyes felted shut, one little arm folded under his head like a
pillow. his doll wears a tiny felt version of the ACTUAL clothing he
is wearing in the reference photos — match the real clothing
faithfully in felt, do not invent new garments. his soft red felt
glasses are OFF — folded closed on the bench near his hand.

standing on the bench beside his head: a tiny felted boba tea — a
pale milk-tea wool cup with a domed top and a fat pastel felt straw,
little dark wool tapioca pearls visible at the bottom — quietly
defying every museum rule.

scattered across the felt gallery floor around the bench, a small
flock of needle-felted SHEEP — wonderfully woolly off-white bodies
(the realest wool in the scene), little dark grey felt faces and slim
legs — three or four of them, some grazing, one turned toward the
sleeping doll as if it just finished being counted.

light: soft, dim, warm-neutral gallery light from above — gentle felt
shadows pooling under the bench and the sheep. sleepy, hushed,
tender, a little funny: a tiny wool man who came to look at art, and
the pond painting and the sheep finished the job.

his doll face stays clearly visible, never cropped. no other figure,
no screens, no phones anywhere.

square album cover. no text, no wordmarks, no wall labels, no
readable logos anywhere.
