boombaboom — album cover. the concept, literal: the track is built
from jeffrey's "boom-ba-boom" chant sung over sine bells, a sine kick,
and a healing 432hz sine-swarm — and the source video is a close-up of
a whistlegraph being inked: a grid of little circles each with a dot
inside (the "booms" — like notes, like little eyes), a bold V
checkmark, and a zigzag. the whole scene is rebuilt as NEEDLE-FELTED
WOOL — a handmade soft-felt diorama, photographed for real.

a real photograph of a hand-crafted needle-felt diorama, square — shot
straight down, almost top-down, like a quiet documentation photo of a
miniature scene. visible wool fibers everywhere, fuzzy soft edges, tiny
felting imperfections that prove human hands. NOT a 3d render, NOT clay,
NOT plastic — soft matted wool throughout. crisp focus, no motion blur.

gently rumpled with felted paper-grain. across it, a constellation of
small CIRCLES each with a single dot in the middle — the "booms" — laid
out in a loose grid like a star-chart of little eyes, every circle and
dot worked in thick BLACK YARN couched and stitched flat onto the white
felt, slightly wobbly and hand-drawn-looking, not perfect. below the
circles, a bold black-yarn V / checkmark and a small jagged ZIGZAG
stitch, exactly like the inked strokes in the source. embroidered black
wool, raised a little off the white felt so the stitches catch the
light.
