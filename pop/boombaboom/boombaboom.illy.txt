boombaboom — album cover, ABSTRACT. no scene, no figure, no objects, no
felt, no people — a pure abstract composition built only from the track's
own marks and frequencies. square.

the track is jeffrey's "boom-ba-boom" chant over sine bells, a sine kick,
and a healing 432hz sine-swarm; the source whistlegraph is a grid of
little circles each with a dot inside (the "booms"), a bold V checkmark,
and a zigzag. abstract those marks into a flat graphic painting:

a loose floating constellation of black RING-AND-DOT glyphs — open
circles each holding one centered dot, the "booms" — at many sizes,
scattered and drifting across the square like notes on an invisible
star-chart, some clustered, some alone. through and behind them, sets of
concentric SINE RIPPLES radiate outward (the 432hz swarm / the bells) as
thin nested rings, overlapping into soft moiré. one bold gestural black V
stroke and one jagged ZIGZAG cut across the field like calligraphic
brush-marks, slightly wobbly, hand-made. a few of the rings glow with a
soft pale halo — the healing hum.

palette: a calm healing TEAL-GREEN and pale-aqua wash over warm cream,
with the rings, dots, V and zigzag in confident matte BLACK; gentle
glowing halos in soft mint and white. flat, graphic, screenprint /
risograph feel — visible grain, a little misregistration, hand-made
imperfection. soft and hypnotic, tender, a little holy. diegetic soft
light, no glow filter overlay, NOT a photograph, NOT a 3d render.

square album cover. no text, no wordmarks, no readable letters or logos.
only abstract marks, rings, and color.
