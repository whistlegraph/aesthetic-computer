jukewizard — app mascot. a small hand-drawn wizard for a music
rating-and-notes tool, colored-pencil + gouache on warm cream paper,
the aesthetic computer hand-drawn style: confident hatching, visible
paper grain, warm and friendly, never corporate.

ONE little wizard figure, full body, centered: a squat cheerful wizard
in a deep-teal robe speckled with tiny gold five-pointed stars, a
floppy pointed hat tipping forward, big round glasses, soft padded
headphones over the hat. with one hand he holds up a slender wand
topped with a single glowing gold star (a rating star he is awarding).
beside him stands a small warm-glowing JUKEBOX — a rounded retro
jukebox with an arched top, its front lit by a row of little colored
lights in the aesthetic-computer note palette (red, orange, yellow,
green, blue, purple, violet), a record spinning behind its window.

floating gently around him: a curling timeline ribbon with a few
evenly spaced tick marks, and two or three little speech-bubble
"comment" tags pinned along it (each bubble blank — NO readable text),
plus a couple of tiny hand-inked eighth-notes drifting near his
shoulder.

palette: deep-teal robe, warm cream ground, buttery-gold star glow,
the seven note-palette colors used ONLY in the jukebox lights, one
coral pop on the hat band. soft warm light from the jukebox and the
gold star illuminating his delighted face.

square, generous margins of plain cream paper around the figure. NO
text, NO words, NO letters, NO logos, NO numbers anywhere. one figure
only, no background scene — just the wizard, his star-wand, the little
jukebox, and the floating timeline on cream paper. hand-drawn print
aesthetic, NOT photoreal, NOT 3-d render, NOT neon, every hand with
exactly five natural fingers.
