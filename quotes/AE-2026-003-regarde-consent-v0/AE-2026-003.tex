\cathead{regindigo}{The whole thing, without the jargon}
Today, when a company wants to use someone's face or voice, it asks whatever
middleman happens to be holding it --- and the person never gets a say. The idea
in your paper flips that around: the request goes to the \textbf{person} first,
and is answered on their terms. What I'm building is the working version of that
flip --- small, but real, and something you can actually run.

\vspace{0.45em}\acrule{3}

\headgap

\cathead{regindigo}{What the v0 is}
A handful of small parts that fit together into one runnable flow:

\vspace{0.3em}
\begin{rtable}
\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{>{\columncolor{\cellbgcolor}\cellstrut}c|L}
\boxon & \textbf{Dispatch router} --- the policy decision point, keyed to the named person. It reads a request, sorts it into one of the four classes (prediction, verification, data-operation, probe), and returns allow, deny, or refuse. \\ \hline
\boxon & \textbf{Verifiable receipt} --- every decision emits a hash-chained, signed receipt. Issuer and verifier produce byte-identical canonical form from the same payload, so a receipt checks out offline with no call back to me. \\ \hline
\boxon & \textbf{Security \& infrastructure} --- a hardening pass over the receipt mechanism (key handling, signature scheme, resistance to replay and forgery, closing the refusal side-channel), built on a private, isolated instance and kept to your private use throughout. \\ \hline
\boxon & \textbf{Walkthrough app} --- a small interface that drives a single request through the whole path so the mechanism is something you can poke at, not just read. \\ \hline
\boxon & \textbf{Proof-of-concept workflows} --- the canonical flows scripted as demos, including the refusal path (refusal-as-side-channel is the one to get right). \\
\end{tabularx}
\end{rtable}

\modgap

\cathead{regverify}{What runs where}
The v0 is built to run \textbf{entirely on your stack}. No live dependency on
the Aesthetic\acdot Computer PDS or on anything I host --- it stands on its own,
and you can run it after I walk away. The first thing we settle is the
\textbf{interface sketch}: the canonical payload and receipt schema on one page.
Schema before metaphor --- it's the right first artifact and it doubles as our
scope contract.

\modgap

\cathead{regamber}{The one thing to pin first}
The verify-offline property lives or dies on determinism, so before any code
the canonicalization is named and frozen. I'd propose \textbf{JCS / RFC 8785} as
the default unless your filings point somewhere else --- agree that surface up
front and the rest of the build is clean.

\modgap
