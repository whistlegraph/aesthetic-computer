# Menu Band press kit

## One line

Menu Band is a tiny Mac menu-bar instrument that plays built-in sounds and
sends notes to any DAW over a virtual MIDI source.

## Short description

Menu Band puts a two-octave piano in the right side of the macOS menu bar.
People can click it, type while the app is focused, or plug in a hardware MIDI
keyboard. It includes a built-in General MIDI synth, exposes a virtual MIDI
source for DAWs, records performances, samples microphone audio, and in 1.6.5
adds a separate CDJ Radio deck for KPBJ, R8DIO, NTS 1, and NTS 2. Menu Band is
open source and costs $4.99 on the Mac App Store.

## What is new in 1.6.5

- Hardware MIDI input turns Menu Band into a compact sound module.
- A separate CDJ Radio deck tunes KPBJ, R8DIO, NTS 1, and NTS 2.
- The live radio buffer can be scratched and sampled onto the piano.
- Recording cues and keyboard-command behavior are smoother and clearer.

The independently distributed Advanced build also includes Spotify process
audio and room audio. Those host-level features are intentionally not present
in the sandboxed Mac App Store build.
