# Menu Band 1.6.5 launch score

Release target: **1.6.5 (165)**. The Mac App Store build is the sandbox-safe
edition; the notarized direct build adds global typing, Spotify process audio,
room audio, and the other advanced host integrations.

## Gate

- [ ] Direct DMG is signed, notarized, stapled, published, and install-tested.
- [ ] Mac App Store build 165 is uploaded and attached to version 1.6.5.
- [ ] Store metadata and exactly three 1440×900 screenshots are attached.
- [ ] Review submission is accepted by App Store Connect.
- [ ] `menuband.app` is live with both films and a working $4.99 store link.
- [ ] Campaign links have the real Apple provider token; never publish a
      placeholder `pt` value.
- [ ] A soft-launch cohort has run the shipping build for 24–48 hours without
      a crash, audio regression, or review concern.

## Rollout

1. **Soft launch:** website, release notes, and a short post to existing
   followers. Invite concrete MIDI/radio/recording feedback.
2. **Review window:** hold the larger press and community list while Apple is
   reviewing 1.6.5. The live 1.5.5 listing remains a safe destination.
3. **Launch day:** after 1.6.5 is `READY_FOR_SALE`, publish the primary post,
   Bluesky/Mastodon variants, and the short press note using separate campaign
   links from `campaign-links.mjs`.
4. **Day +1:** answer questions and publish the 18-second “Meet Menu Band” film.
5. **Day +3:** publish “Sing the scale” and the hardware-MIDI/CDJ Radio angle.
6. **Day +7:** record downloads, product-page views, conversion, sessions,
   crashes, refunds, direct DMG downloads, and qualitative replies.

## Success signals

The first launch is a learning release, not a vanity-metric sprint. Record:

- App Store product-page views → first-time downloads by campaign.
- Paid proceeds/refunds once Sales and Trends reports settle.
- Sessions and opt-in diagnostics from App Store Connect Analytics.
- Direct DMG downloads from the Spaces/CDN access report.
- The phrases people use when they understand the product without prompting.

Apple campaign reporting suppresses tiny cohorts; a campaign normally needs at
least five first-time downloads before it appears. Keep campaign names stable.
