Menu Band Tips

Menu Band is a playable instrument that lives in your menu bar. Click the little keyboard up top to play; open the popover (the ♪ chip) for instruments, chords, sampling, and more.

The basics

The status item in your menu bar is the keyboard — its keys draw inline and light up as you play. Two surfaces open from there:

  • The popover — click the ♪ chip. Holds the General MIDI instrument chooser, the QWERTY key map, and the special cells (MIDI OUT, SAMPLE, radio, and 🦜 Squawk).
  • The floating panel — a larger liquid-glass keyboard with the waveform, held-note pills, and the layout / Gamepad / LLMs controls.

Playing notes

Play with the mouse on the menu-bar keys, or with your computer keyboard. The QWERTY map in the popover shows which physical key plays which note; the home row is a melodic run and the number row shifts you up an octave.

Two keymaps are available from the floating panel's mode row: Menu Band (the default melodic layout) and AWSED (a conventional piano-style mapping). Use the arrow cluster under the instrument grid to step through voices while you audition them.

Chords

Hold a note and add a modifier to voice a whole triad from one finger. The grammar is designed so ⌃ alters the shape you're already holding — major's fifth goes up, minor's down, sus2's second lifts to a fourth:

ModifierChord
⌘Major
⌥Minor
⌃Augmented
⌘⌥Suspended 2nd (sus2)
⌥⌃Diminished
⌘⌥⌃Suspended 4th (sus4)

The chord name shows in the popover as you hold it.

Sampling

Pick the SAMPLE cell (right end of the top row in the instrument grid), then hold the ` backtick key to record a short clip from your microphone. Release to stop. Your recording becomes the instrument — every key plays it back, pitched to that note. It's speed-independent, so high notes don't chipmunk.

CDJ Radio → Piano Sampler: while a station or Spotify is playing, press SAMPLE → PIANO in the CDJ Radio card. The latest 2.5 seconds become the sampler instrument. This is an explicit handoff; CDJ Radio never remaps the piano keys by itself.

Squawk — voice dictation

Squawk turns your voice into text and types it into whatever app is in front — handy for talking into a terminal or a chat without leaving the keyboard. Transcription runs on your Mac (offline, no account).

  1. Open About Menu Band → Advanced and tick Squawk — voice dictation.
  2. Hold ⌘⌃⌥` and talk; release to insert the text. (The 🦜 cell in the popover and the Squawk button on the floating panel also toggle it with a click.)
  3. The first time, macOS asks for Microphone and Speech Recognition permission — allow both.

Typing into other apps is available in the direct-download version. The Mac App Store version can transcribe but, per sandbox rules, cannot type into other apps.

Instruments, MIDI & CDJ Radio

  • Instruments — the numbered grid is all 128 General MIDI voices, colored by family. Click to choose; drag to audition.
  • MIDI OUT — pick the 0 MIDI OUT cell to send notes to a DAW or hardware over a virtual MIDI port instead of the built-in synth.
  • CDJ Radio — the strip along the bottom selects KPBJ, R8DIO, NTS, or Spotify as a continuous deck. It plays alongside the selected piano instrument and runs through Menu Band's effects and master mix.
  • Spotify — in the direct-download build, pick SPOTIFY in that strip to open CDJ Radio's headless player. It shows artwork and current-track progress, has previous/play/next and seeking, searches the Spotify catalog, and spins its artwork in the menu bar. Spotify Premium and one-time juked authenticate setup are required.

Play with an LLM

Menu Band exposes its whole performance surface as macOS notifications, so a model like Claude can drive it — autoplay melodies, conduct the live engine, speak, and relay parts across machines. Open the floating panel and click LLMs to copy a ready-made guide you can paste into Claude.
