# MicroVision Future PPS Calculations

**Date:** 2019-03-08 to 2019-03-10
**From:** Walt Scudder → Bob Zawistowski → Chad Zawistowski (forwarded to Jeffrey)
**Subject:** MicroVision's future PPS calculations

Walt and a friend built a back-of-envelope PPS model and shared it with Bob Zawistowski, whose son Chad provided skeptical counteranalysis.

## Walt's Bull Case
- **Assumed volume:** 1 billion units/year (IoT + LiDAR + HoloLens + smart speakers + smartphones)
- **Profit margin:** 30–40% (per CFO Steve Holt's Q4 2018 CC guidance)
- **PE ratio:** 14 (S&P 500 historical average)
- **Royalty component:** ~20% of unit price at ~100% margin

### Known Deals at the Time
- $25.2M contract (suspected Microsoft HoloLens 2): $15.2M engineering + $10M components
- $10M display-only exclusive license (suspected FoxConn)
- LiDAR engineering samples shipping to interested customers

## Chad Zawistowski's Bear Case
- **Assumed volume:** 35 million units (comparable to Xbox Kinect)
- **PE ratio:** 5
- **Result:** $0.33 EPS, $1.70 share price (40% upside)
- Called MVIS "a commodity part manufacturer" comparable to Micron, not Nvidia
- Skeptical of motion-capture adoption based on Magic Leap and Kinect failures

## Walt's Rebuttal
- PE of 14 is more appropriate than 5
- Royalty payments at ~100% margin not factored into Chad's model
- Scale would drive down manufacturing costs significantly
- Multiple Tier 1 customers (MSFT, Amazon, FoxConn) creates diversified revenue
