# AIM Intelligent Machines Takeover Theory

**Date:** 2026-03-20
**Source:** Walter Scudder (via Jeffrey) — Walt believes AIM will take over MicroVision.
**Signal:** AIM and MicroVision now share office space in the Seattle/Eastside area.

## The Companies

**AIM Intelligent Machines** (aim.vision)
- Autonomous earthmoving: retrofits heavy equipment (excavators, dozers, trucks) to operate autonomously
- HQ: Monroe, WA / offices at 2018 156th Ave NE, Bellevue, WA
- Backed by DCVC (deep tech VC), holds defense contracts
- Mission: "applying the power of AI and robotics to move earth at scale"

**MicroVision** (MVIS)
- MEMS-based LiDAR + laser beam scanning
- HQ: 18390 NE 68th St, Redmond, WA (a few miles from AIM's Bellevue office)
- Market cap: ~$190M at $0.61/share (March 2026)
- Products: MAVIN (long-range), MOVIA (short/mid-range), NED laser projector
- Recently acquired Luminar assets ($33M), raised $43M via convertible notes

## Why the Fit Is Almost Too Clean

AIM needs exactly what MVIS makes:
- Compact, mass-producible, affordable LiDAR for harsh outdoor environments
- Integration with existing heavy equipment drive systems (bolt-on)
- Multi-machine autonomy (one operator, multiple machines)
- Defense-grade sensing

From the Grok blind valuation (same day):
> "The LiDAR can also be used to assist in automating heavy equipment, allowing multiple machines to be operated by a single person."

## Shared Office Signal

If AIM and MicroVision are sharing office space, this goes beyond a partnership. Shared offices typically indicate:
- Joint engineering / integration work
- Pre-acquisition due diligence
- Talent sharing or secondment
- Deep product co-development

## Acquisition Math

- MVIS market cap: ~$190M
- AIM backing: DCVC + defense contracts
- MVIS just consolidated Luminar IP (Iris + Halo sensors) — an acquirer gets the combined portfolio
- 62.7% retail ownership = no single institutional blocker, but shares are scattered and acquirable
- NED licensing rights alone estimated at $200–500M (more than the entire current market cap)

## Assessment

**More credible than a decade of Microsoft/Amazon/Apple rumors.** Those were speculative ("mystery customer" theories). This has:
1. Geographic co-location (now sharing offices)
2. Perfect product-market fit (autonomous heavy equipment needs LiDAR)
3. Overlapping defense angle (AIM defense contracts + MVIS/Anduril partnership)
4. MVIS is priced like a distressed asset ($0.61)
5. AIM has VC backing capable of facilitating a deal

**Open question:** Does AIM (or its backers at DCVC) have the capital or appetite to acquire at the ~$190M+ level? Or is this more likely a deep partnership / licensing deal that could precede acquisition?

Walt's read: AIM will take over MicroVision.
