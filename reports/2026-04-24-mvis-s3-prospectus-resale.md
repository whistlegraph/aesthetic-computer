## What this filing actually is

This is **not a new capital raise.** It is a *resale shelf* — MVIS is fulfilling its
contractual obligation to register the shares that sit *underneath* the convertible
notes High Trail Special Situations LLC and High Trail II LLC (both Hudson Bay
Capital affiliates, managed by Sander Gerber) received on **February 23, 2026**.

The notes are already issued. This filing just lets High Trail freely resell the
common stock once they convert — or once they receive shares in lieu of cash for
the monthly partial redemptions.

## What it means

### 1. Dilution overhang is now formal
61.3M registered shares = **18.7% dilution** on the current 326.9M float. The
market has been pricing this in since February, but with an active resale
prospectus, every conversion can hit the tape immediately.

### 2. Conversion price is *above* the market
$0.8819 conversion vs. $0.63 spot (Apr 23). High Trail loses money converting
today. So the practical near-term path is **monthly cash redemptions**, not
share conversions — which drains MVIS's $74.8M cash directly.

### 3. The $2.00 forced-conversion trigger is the bull case
If MVIS rallies and stays above $2 for 20 trading days, MVIS can wipe out the
notes by forcing conversion. **No cash leaves the building, debt disappears.**
This is the cleanest exit for the company and aligns with retail bull theses.

### 4. The covenant math is tight
- Cash on hand (per Q4 2025 earnings): **$74.8M**
- Minimum liquidity floor: $33.9M × 110% = **$37.3M** (or $21.5M, whichever is higher → **$37.3M**)
- Headroom before covenant breach: ~$37M
- 2026 projected burn: **$65–70M**

So absent revenue acceleration or a stock recovery, MVIS has roughly **6–7
months** of operating runway before tripping the covenant — at which point
High Trail can foreclose on the bank/securities accounts.

### 5. The 19.99% share cap is the next catalyst
The 61,315,970 figure exists because Nasdaq Rule 5635(d)(2) caps issuance at
19.99% of pre-deal shares without a stockholder vote. If MVIS calls a vote to
lift the cap, it signals they expect more dilution is necessary. Watch the
proxy filings.

### 6. Hudson Bay Capital is a known short-friendly counterparty
The prospectus dedicates an unusual amount of language to "short squeeze" risk
— a tell that the underwriters know HBC's reputation in the MVIS retail
community. HBC has historically hedged convertible positions with short
exposure, profiting on the spread regardless of which way the stock moves.

## Bottom line

This filing makes the February deal **operational** — High Trail can now start
liquidating into the market on conversion. It does not change the fundamentals
established in February: MVIS bought time and integration capital at the cost
of:

- A senior lien on its cash
- A real risk of forced asset foreclosure if cash drops below ~$37M
- Up to 18.7% dilution if the stock rallies above $0.8819
- Debt elimination if the stock can hold above $2.00 for 20 days

For the AIM-takeover thesis: this **does not preclude** a takeover, but a
buyer would have to deal with the High Trail lien, which sits ahead of common
equity. A change-of-control clause likely exists in Exhibits 10.1/10.2 (8-K
Feb 24, 2026) — worth pulling those to confirm.
