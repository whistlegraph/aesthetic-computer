# Jeffrey Alan Scudder — Economic History & Funding Timeline

_Last updated: 2026-03-14_

## Summary

This report documents the economic conditions under which Aesthetic Computer and its predecessor projects were built. It serves as a data point for the "Who Pays for Creative Tools?" paper and as an honest accounting of solo creative tool development without institutional backing.

---

## Career Timeline & Income Sources

### 2007 — Dishwasher, The Perch Restaurant (Denali, AK)
- Summer job in Alaska between college years

### 2007–2011 — BFA, Ringling College of Art and Design (Sarasota, FL)
- Gallery assistant, IT assistant, gymnastics coach (part-time income)
- AICAD New York Studio Program residency (2010)

### 2011–2013 — MFA, Yale School of Art (New Haven, CT)
- Teaching assistant, web admin, checkout assistant (stipend + part-time)
- Studio assistant for Nathan Skiles

### 2013–2014 — Designer, Linked by Air (NYC)
- First salaried position in design/tech
- Co-founded Gordilloscudder gallery (Brooklyn)

### 2015–2016 — Part-time Faculty, Parsons School of Design (NYC)
- Adjunct pay (~$3,000–5,000/course)

### 2016 — Visiting Professor, UCLA DMA + Contractor work
- UCLA visiting position (one quarter)
- Contract work: Kickstarter, NADA/Linked by Air

### 2017 — Technical Curator, GIPHY (NYC)
- First tech-industry salary

- Left Gallery / Tumpin release (32 editions, one of earliest blockchain art projects)

- Tour venues: Louisiana Museum, KUNSTHAL AARHUS, Kammer-Pop Hamburg, Dans for voksne Oslo, West Germany Berlin, Spanien 19C

## Notes

- The $250K NFT revenue was earned primarily in a 12-month window (late 2021–late 2022) during a historic crypto bull market. This window has closed.
- The SOU salary of ~$45K was below the national median for assistant professors (~$65K). It was earned during COVID-19, when teaching moved online.
- Infrastructure costs of ~$150–200/month are modest by platform standards but unsustainable without income.
- The 2,811 registered handles and 93,122 boot events represent real users making real things. This is not a prototype.
- People have built on and taken ideas from AC's design: the prompt-as-instrument metaphor, the piece model, the KidLisp approach to generative art on social platforms.
