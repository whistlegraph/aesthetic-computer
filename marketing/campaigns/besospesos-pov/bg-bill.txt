a photograph of a colored-pencil and gouache drawing on warm cream
paper, tall vertical portrait. confident hatching and striping build
tone. tapered pencil edges, visible paper grain, optical mixing,
print-work sensibility. diegetic light only — no overlay glow, no
motion blur, no soft-focus haze. crisp marks foreground to back. the
drawing fills the whole frame edge to edge — no cream border, no
vignette.

first-person pov — this is what i see standing in a warm hallway
printer nook. on a low oak cabinet along the LEFT side of the frame
sits a big beige laser printer, paper tray hanging open, one sheet
jammed mid-feed at a sad angle, a fan of printed pages spilling off
the cabinet onto the floor. a small wall sconce above it throws warm
yellow light down the wall. a framed needlepoint hangs slightly
crooked beside the sconce — its stitching is abstract, unreadable.

the CENTER-RIGHT of the frame is open hallway floor on a flat woven
runner — calm and empty, nobody standing there. that space stays
clear.

at the very bottom edge of the frame: my own two hands holding a
fresh wrapped ream of printer paper.

no people anywhere in the scene. no readable text anywhere — page
contents are faint grey hatched lines only. no screens.
