a candid horizontal photograph, close-up, sharp and honest — NOT cinematic.
everything in crisp focus: NO motion blur, NO bokeh, NO out-of-focus areas
anywhere. wide landscape orientation. no people in frame (or only a hand at the edge).

a conceptual still: a refurbished plain black thinkpad laptop, lid open, sitting
on a plain desk. the thinkpad is an old surplus machine — a fifty-dollar
thinkpad. its screen is almost entirely BLACK, with only a single short warm
greeting printed on it: one chunky pink pixel-font line, lowercase, reading
exactly:

    hello, jeffrey

and nothing else on the screen — no second line, no version number, no
software title, no product or brand name, no logo, no menu, no progress bar.
just that one friendly two-word greeting glowing pink on black, as if a
hand-made personal computer is saying hello to its owner. it must NOT look like
a commercial software splash screen or an app loading screen — it looks like a
tiny homemade terminal greeting. real screen content, never a thumbnail of the
surrounding room. the matte-black thinkpad lid and keyboard are sharp and
legible.

a warm desk lamp lights the scene from one side; the pink screen-glow spills
onto the keyboard and desk — diegetic light only. on the desk beside it: a
single usb stick (the whole system boots from it). plain, modest, documentary.

palette: matte black thinkpad, warm lamp, soft pink screen-glow on black.

avoid: motion blur, bokeh, soft focus, sci-fi neon, recursive screens (never a
picture of this desk on the screen), citrus-green macbook here (this segment is
the black thinkpad), ANY product / app / software / brand name on the screen
(no "sonic pi", no "polyend", no "tracker", no titles, no version numbers —
ONLY the words "hello, jeffrey"), extra logos, cartoon styling, a busy
loading-screen look.
