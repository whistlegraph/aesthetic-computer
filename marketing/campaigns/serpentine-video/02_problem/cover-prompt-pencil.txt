a horizontal 16:9 landscape COLORED-PENCIL DRAWING on paper — not a photograph. a limited, basic palette. build all tone from visible hatching and parallel striping; let the pencil strokes show their direction and pressure. tapered, slightly open edges; small areas left unfilled so the paper tooth shows through. colors mix optically — layered strokes that blend in the eye, with gentle moiré where strokes cross. confident, observed, hand-drawn marks. fully legible and in crisp focus — NO smudging, NO blur, NO soft wash, NO bokeh. compose wide for 16:9.

no people in frame. a conceptual drawing about personal computing being pulled into the data center, composed wide. on one side a single ordinary laptop sits open and alone on a plain table in a warm lamp-lit room; a thin glowing green cable runs out of its back and stretches all the way across the wide frame, away into the distance where — behind a pane of glass — a vast cold data-center hall recedes in long rows of dark server racks lit by cool blue indicator lights. warm domestic left, cold impersonal right, the cable the tether between them. the laptop screen shows a plain green-on-black terminal with a blinking cursor — real content, never a thumbnail of this scene. warm amber room hatched against cold blue server hall, a thin green-glowing tether across the middle. no text labels or logos.

avoid: blur, bokeh, soft wash, neon glow that does not trace to a real source, recursive screens, childish cartoon styling or flat vector art (this is a sincere hand-drawn colored-pencil drawing), mis-spellings.
