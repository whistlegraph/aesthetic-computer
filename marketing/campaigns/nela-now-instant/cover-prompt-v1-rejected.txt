A horizontal colored-pencil and gouache tableau on warm cream paper, in the
style of David Hockney's print work — basic palette built from confident
hatching and striping for tone, tapered edges, optical-mixing and gentle moiré
in the patterned surfaces. Editorial, satirical-of-historical-group-paintings
horizontality: a cross-section of an underground Chinatown art-house cinema at
night, lit warmly by lamps and a single big screen. ALL FIGURES SHARE EYE-LINE
— no central cult-leader composition, peers among peers.

Setting: a small intimate cinema lobby + auditorium fused in the same
panoramic frame. STAGE LEFT, a tiny lobby with a hand-lettered marquee whose
serif wordmark reads "NOW INSTANT" in deep emerald-green ink (#007A33) on
white, with a small typewritten paper slip pasted under it that says "TONIGHT
— NELA COMPUTER CLUB" framed in ASCII rules of light grey blocks (░░░). A
narrow ticket window where two people are accepting mimeographed black-on-
white tickets stamped once with a green NOW INSTANT seal. A short queue of
maybe four warmly-dressed twenty-and-thirty-somethings holding old-school
tickets — button-downs, cardigans, hoodies, printed tees — clutching real
laptops (a Lenovo ThinkPad with stickers, a Framework, a beat-up unibody
MacBook, a Pinebook).

STAGE CENTER and RIGHT, the auditorium — six rows of mismatched plush red and
chocolate cinema seats already partly filled, a faded velvet curtain pulled
aside. The big screen at the back of the frame is glowing a flat #007A33
emerald green showing a single line of monospace KidLisp code and a small
pixel-art shape (the screen is the diegetic light source — the green wash on
the front-row faces and the seat backs comes from THIS screen, not from any
overlay). A presenter at a small folding table beneath the screen — laptop
plugged into a thick projector cable — is mid-demo, smiling, mouth slightly
open, gesturing at the screen. Audience members are visibly delighted: one
laughing, one leaning forward, one squinting in mock-detective curiosity, one
taking notes on a paper program.

JEFFREY appears somewhere in the auditorium row, NOT centered, NOT presenting
this round — a peer in the audience holding HIS chartreuse-citrus-green
MacBook Neo on his lap, the four-stroke whistlegraph butterfly (replacing the
apple logo) glowing softly on the lid; he is grinning, mid-laugh,
chest-level gesture toward the screen, sharing the moment with the person
next to him. He wears a printed tee under an open flannel; a small
USB-stick-shaped vape pen with a tiny LED rests on the armrest beside him.

Background: a tall wooden bookshelf against stage-right wall stocked with
zines and paperbacks — the bookstore half of the venue — and one peer
flipping through a book. A narrow strip of Chinatown red lanterns visible
through the open lobby door at far stage left.

  pamphlet on a lap

Palette: warm cream paper, ivory and bone whites, soft chocolate browns,
brick red of cinema seats, lamp-amber, deep emerald #007A33 as the dominant
accent, jeffrey's chartreuse Neo as a single secondary citrus pop. Muted,
print-tech-aware, hatched, hand-drawn. NO neon, NO cinematic glow, NO
photoreal rendering — colored pencil and gouache, visible paper grain, gentle
dissolve at the edges into pure cream paper.

NEVER: recursive screens (other laptops show terminals / paint / scopes /
