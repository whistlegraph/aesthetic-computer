a documentary-style horizontal photograph of a small chinatown cinema
on a packed sold-out night. natural lighting, editorial / journalistic
framing — like a magazine feature shot. shallow but honest depth,
full-frame digital camera feel, no heavy filtering. every figure on
roughly the same eye-line — peers among peers, no lone-centered figure.

— the space —

inside now instant image hall, chinatown LA. one of the campaign refs
is a photograph of the actual room: render the architecture faithfully
from that ref. mossy / sage green polished-plaster walls and ceiling,
faintly cloudy and hand-troweled. matching sage-green carpet. tidy
rows of plush sage-green velvet club armchairs (not red, not brown —
green velvet). a dark-framed single cinema screen mounted high on the
front wall, with a dark wall-mount speaker on each side. small
recessed white ceiling downlights overhead. on the right side wall:
two warm brass sconces throwing soft starburst flares against the
green plaster. intimate room — about thirty seats.

view: from the back-side, three-quarter angle, looking toward the
screen. house lights are at low glow.

— the screen —

on the screen: tall friendly hand-lettered block letters reading
"BIG SCREEN COMPUTER CLUB", and nothing else. each individual letter
is a different bright color — playful kidlisp-style multi-color
typography (cherry red, marigold yellow, electric blue, magenta,
lime, orange, violet, etc.), each letter chunky and confident on a
clean black ground inside the dark frame. no subtitle, no monospace
tagline, no rule blocks, no pixel-art shape, no logo, no date — only
that one phrase. the screen is the dominant light source: its
multi-colored wash spills out across the room, catching the cheekbones,
foreheads, and shoulders of the audience in soft streaks of red,
yellow, blue, magenta, green. faces glow with mixed colored light,
mingling with the warm gold from the brass sconces on the side wall.
diegetic light only — every color visible on a face traces back to a
real source in the scene.

a single presenter stands beside a small folding table directly
beneath the screen, a laptop in front of them, a thick coiled cable
running up to the projection booth. relaxed, gesturing gently at the
screen with one hand.

— the crowd —

the house is PACKED. every seat full, a few people standing along the
back wall and side aisles. a sold-out feel.

the mix is mostly cool LA — fashion-aware locals in their 20s–40s, a
chinatown-meets-eastside crowd: well-cut vintage tees, oversized
button-downs, leather jackets, tank tops layered under cardigans,
delicate gold jewelry, dyed hair, varied skin tones and body types,
people who look like they came for the cinema and the night out.
many of them are eating and drinking — small striped cardboard
popcorn boxes, half-eaten pretzels, snack bags, tallboy beer cans,
plastic cups of natural wine, sparkling water, the occasional asian
snack (haw flakes, shrimp chips). casual, unposed, paying attention
to the screen.

scattered among the cool LA crowd, and especially clustered in the
front two rows, are the NELA computer club members — a quieter,
nerdier subset. they're the ones with small open clamshell laptops on
their laps, in a friendly assortment of different makes and ages —
thinkpads, frameworks, older macbooks, pinebooks, stickers visible on
lids. their laptop screens show varied real content in glancing
perspective: a terminal window in green-on-black, a tiny pixel sprite
editor, a music notation staff, a paint program, an oscilloscope
sketch, a small line of code, a chat window. never a thumbnail of the
surrounding scene. clubbers wear plainer gear — flannels, hoodies,
printed tees, soft sweaters, monospace-energy outfits — and read as
peers, not stars.

the cool LA folks have NO laptops — they're here for the show, with
snacks and drinks instead.

— palette —

dominant: mossy / sage green velvet seats, walls, carpet — deeply
saturated, the whole room reads green. on top of that green, a riot
of multi-colored screen-light spilling across faces — red, yellow,
blue, magenta, lime, orange, violet — each pinned to a letter on the
screen. accents: bone whites and ivory; warm gold from the brass
sconces; cool white from the recessed ceiling downlights. muted,
photographic, naturalistic — but the screen-light is genuinely
colorful and lively.

— avoid —

cartoon or illustrated styling. heavy filters or HDR. cinematic neon
glow that doesn't trace back to a source. red or chocolate-brown
cinema seats (the real seats are sage green velvet — match the venue
ref). pendant lamps (use the recessed downlights + brass sconces).
recursive screens (laptops show varied real content, never thumbnails
of this scene). extra text on the cinema screen — only the phrase
"BIG SCREEN COMPUTER CLUB" appears there, in multi-colored letters.
empty seats (the house is packed). everyone holding a laptop (only
the clubbers — front rows and scattered — have laptops; cool LA
folks have snacks and drinks). centering jeffrey or any single
figure as the host.
