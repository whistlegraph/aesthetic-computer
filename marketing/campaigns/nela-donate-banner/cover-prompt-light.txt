a wide banner for a neighborhood computer club, in a bright daytime
light-mode palette. a photograph of an ink-and-gouache drawing on warm
white paper — airy, clean, lots of light. accents in grass green and
cyan, but the paper stays bright white.

a long workbench by day, seen straight on, daylight pouring in from a
window. salvaged laptops and chromebooks stacked and opened up, getting a
second life — some lids up, some cracked open to their guts (the screens
glow green-on-black against the bright room). breadboards with little leds
and rainbow jumper wires. a tangle of orange and blue network cables with
rj45 ends. a small modular synth with patch cables. an oscilloscope
showing one looping green generative shape. a soldering iron with a thin
curl of smoke. a wooden card-catalog drawer pulled open, full of floppy
disks and hand-labeled cards.

warm and human, a little messy, hands-on repair energy — people fixing old
machines together. a few figures at the far end of the bench leaning in
over the work. chinatown brick wall painted pale, a big bright window with
the street outside.

drawn, not photographic — bold flat shapes, confident hatching for shadow,
visible paper grain, screen-print sensibility, generous white space.

NO text, NO words, NO logos, NO lettering anywhere. NO motion blur, NO
soft-focus haze — crisp marks front to back. one bench, one arrangement.
