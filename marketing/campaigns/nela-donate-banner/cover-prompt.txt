a wide banner for a neighborhood computer club. green-phosphor terminal
palette — deep black background, everything glowing in chartreuse green
and a little cyan, like an old crt monitor lighting a dark room.

a long workbench at night, seen straight on. salvaged laptops and
chromebooks stacked and opened up, getting a second life — some lids up
showing green-on-black code, some cracked open to their guts. breadboards
with blinking leds and rainbow jumper wires. a tangle of orange and blue
network cables with rj45 ends. a small modular synth with patch cables.
an oscilloscope showing one looping green generative shape. a soldering
iron with a thin curl of smoke. a wooden card-catalog drawer pulled open,
full of floppy disks and hand-labeled cards.

warm and human, a little messy, hands-on repair energy — people fixing
old machines together. a few figures at the far end of the bench, soft in
the dark, leaning in over the work. chinatown brick wall, a window with
faint neon outside.

drawn, not photographic — bold flat shapes, confident hatching for shadow,
visible paper grain, screen-print sensibility. the whole thing glows in
the dark like a terminal.

NO text, NO words, NO logos, NO lettering anywhere. NO motion blur, NO
soft-focus haze — crisp marks front to back. one bench, one arrangement.
